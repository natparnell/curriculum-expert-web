# Curriculum coverage report

*Generated 29/05/2026 from a 15-agent coverage map. Coverage is at broad strand level across EYFS to KS5.*

## Headline

- **15 subjects** mapped, **577 strand x key-stage cells** in total.
- **Overall coverage: 34%** — 130 covered, 132 partial, 315 with nothing.
- **5 whole subjects sit at ~0% coverage** (we build nothing for them): Art and Design, Music, Computing, Design and Technology, Physical Education.
- **598 gap-filler app ideas** were proposed across all gaps (raw pool; the backlog prioritises them).

## Biggest gaps inside subjects we already build for

*'None' strands in active subjects — the highest-impact quick wins.*

**English** (17)
- EYFS: Communication and language (listening, attention, speaking)
- EYFS: Early writing: letter formation and writing simple phrases
- KS1: Common exception words and contractions
- KS1: Reading comprehension: inference, prediction and discussion
- KS1: Spelling and transcription: suffixes, plurals and handwriting
- KS1: Spoken language: discussion, role play and Standard English
- KS2: Reading comprehension: inference, summarising, themes and author's language
- KS2: Sentence grammar: clauses, fronted adverbials, modal verbs, relative clauses and direct speech
- KS2: Spoken language: presentations, debate and reasoned justification
- KS3: Reading whole texts: fiction, non-fiction, poetry and drama across genres and periods
- KS3: Writing for purpose and audience: stories, scripts, arguments, letters and the writing process
- KS3: Spoken language: formal presentation, discussion and debate
- KS4: Non-fiction reading and comparing viewpoints across time (Paper 2, AO1/AO3)
- KS4: Spoken Language Endorsement (prepared formal presentation)
- KS5: English Language: language frameworks, variation and change (Language A-level)
- KS5: English Language: child language acquisition (Language A-level)
- KS5: Non-examined assessment: independent investigation and original writing with commentary

**Mathematics** (8)
- KS1: Multiplication and division (2, 5, 10 tables, arrays, equal groups)
- KS2: Number and place value (to 10,000,000, negative numbers, rounding, Roman numerals)
- KS2: Four operations: written methods and properties (column, long mult/division, order of operations, factors/multiples/primes)
- KS3: Number (rational numbers, standard form, indices, roots, fractions/decimals/percentages multiplicatively)
- KS3: Ratio, proportion and rates of change
- KS4: Number (surds, indices, standard form, bounds and accuracy)
- KS4: Ratio, proportion and rates of change (percentages, growth/decay, compound units)
- KS5: Core Maths: financial maths, estimation, critical analysis of data

**Science** (21)
- EYFS: Materials and physical processes (floating, melting, movement)
- EYFS: Care for and respect the natural environment
- KS2: Animals including humans (nutrition, skeletons, digestion, circulation, life cycles)
- KS2: States of matter and the water cycle
- KS2: Light and shadows
- KS2: Sound (vibration, pitch, volume)
- KS2: Earth and space (solar system, day/night, Moon phases)
- KS2: Evolution and inheritance (adaptation, variation, fossils as evidence)
- KS2: Working scientifically at KS2 (variables, fair tests, recording, conclusions)
- KS3: Chemistry: particulate nature of matter and states (particle model, changes of state, diffusion)
- KS3: Chemistry: chemical reactions, energetics and the Earth/atmosphere
- KS3: Physics: space (gravity, solar system, seasons, the universe)
- KS3: Working scientifically (planning, apparatus, analysis, evaluation)
- KS4: Biology: homeostasis and response (nervous/hormonal control)
- KS4: Chemistry: bonding, structure and properties of matter
- KS4: Chemistry: quantitative chemistry (moles, equations, calculations)
- KS4: Chemistry: chemical changes and energy changes
- KS4: Chemistry: chemical analysis, atmosphere and using resources
- KS5: Biology: response, homeostasis and control (nervous, hormonal, muscles)
- KS5: Chemistry: physical chemistry (atomic structure, energetics, kinetics, equilibria, thermodynamics, electrochemistry)
- KS5: Practical endorsement and CPAC skills (all three sciences)

**History** (13)
- EYFS: Understanding the past through stories and storytelling
- KS1: Events beyond living memory significant nationally/globally (Great Fire of London, first flight, Moon landing)
- KS1: Local history: significant events, people and places in the locality
- KS2: British prehistory to 1066 (Stone Age to Iron Age; Romans; Anglo-Saxons & Scots; Vikings)
- KS2: A study beyond 1066 (turning point/theme in British history, e.g. WWII, railways, migration)
- KS3: Medieval Britain 1066-1509: Church, state and society
- KS3: Early Modern Britain 1509-1745: Reformation, civil war, revolution
- KS3: Britain 1745-1901: ideas, political power, industry and empire (incl. slavery and abolition)
- KS3: Local history study and pre-1066 consolidation (migration, changing power/landscape over time)
- KS3: World history study and its interconnections (e.g. Mughal India, Qing China, Russian empires)
- KS4: Period study (50+ years of national/international history, e.g. Germany 1890-1945, American West)
- KS4: Thematic study across centuries (Health/Medicine, Power and the People, Crime and Punishment, Migration)
- KS5: Non-Examined Assessment: independent historical investigation

**Geography** (10)
- EYFS: Similarities and differences between this country and other places/communities
- KS1: Place knowledge: comparing a UK area with a contrasting non-European area
- KS2: Locational knowledge: UK counties/cities/regions, Europe, North and South America
- KS2: Latitude, longitude, hemispheres, tropics, time zones
- KS2: Human geography: settlements, land use, economic activity, trade and resources
- KS3: Locational knowledge: globally significant places, terrestrial and marine
- KS3: Resource geography: energy, food and water security
- KS3: Geographical skills and fieldwork: GIS, OS maps, data analysis, enquiry
- KS4: Resource management: food, water and energy
- KS5: Changing places

- KS5: Religious experience and religious language
- KS5: Meta-ethics, conscience and free will / moral responsibility
- KS5: Study of religion: sacred texts, developments in thought, religion and society/dialogue

- KS3: Reading and translation of authentic and literary texts
