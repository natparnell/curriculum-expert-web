Notes:
- The A-level descriptor labels its three components Christianity, Religious Ethics and Philosophy of Religion (the RS web page numbers them Component 1 Study of Religion, Component 2 Philosophy, Component 3 Ethics; same content, different ordering). The chosen religion for the Study of a Religion component is unambiguously Christianity.
- Minor discrepancy to flag: the RS department web page lists the AQA GCSE thematic units as Relationships and Families, Peace and Conflict, Crime and Punishment, and Religion and Life. The GCSE course-descriptor PDF names the same four themes (wording: Relationships and families; Crime and punishment; Religion and life; Religion, peace and conflict). AQA 8062 Paper 2 is examined on four themes, so four named themes is internally consistent. Treat all four as the studied set.
