Comparable Outcomes Explainer

Teacher aim: show pupils why grade boundaries move from year to year. The boundary mark is not fixed. When a paper is harder or easier, examiners shift the boundary so that a cohort of similar prior attainment ends up with broadly similar outcomes. This is statistical equating, the comparable outcomes principle.

STEP 1

Pick this year's cohort

Comparable outcomes starts before the exam. The cohort's prior attainment (their KS2 results) sets a predicted distribution of grades. A stronger cohort is expected to earn more top grades.

STEP 2

Change how hard this year's paper turned out

Drag the slider, or use the arrow keys when it is focused. You are changing only the paper's difficulty. The cohort and its prior attainment stay the same.

The purple line is where examiners place the grade 7 boundary after seeing how the paper performed. The target percentage at grade 7 and above stays anchored to the cohort, even as the marks needed change.
