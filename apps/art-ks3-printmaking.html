Relief printing and other print methods

In relief printing the raised surface of a block is inked and pressed onto paper, so the parts you cut away stay blank. This studio models relief printing. It is one of a range of techniques you will explore at KS3.

Relief

Lino & block printing

Cut into lino or a soft block; ink the raised surface; press to print. The carved lines stay paper-coloured.

Relief / direct

Mono printing

Roll ink onto a smooth plate, draw or lift areas, then take a single one-off print. No two are identical.

Recessed

Intaglio

Ink sits in lines scratched or etched below the surface; the surface is wiped clean. The opposite of relief.

Stencil

Screen printing

Ink is pushed through a fine mesh with a blocked-out stencil. Strong flat colour, ideal for layered repeats.

  • The printing block: only the raised surface carries ink to the paper.
  • Reversal: the print is a mirror image of the block. Text and direction flip.
  • Registration: lining up each press so layers and repeats sit where you intend.
  • Printing in layers: overlapping inks combine, building depth and new colours.
  • Reduction printing: cut more away after each colour, printing light to dark from one block.
  • Repeats & pattern: printing the same block in a grid builds rhythm across a surface.

Experiment & record Working like an artist

Try in the studio

  1. Carve the letter A, then print it. Sketch in your book how the printed A differs from the block.
  2. Print the same block five times with the registration offset at 0, then at +12 px. What changes?
  3. Switch to layered mode and print a second colour over the first. Record the new colour where they overlap.
  4. Use reduction mode: print, cut more away, change ink, and print again from the one block.

Record in your sketchbook

  1. Annotate which areas you cut and why, and how the negative shapes read in the print.
  2. Pull three versions of a print, changing one thing each time, and decide which is strongest and why.
  3. Note where registration drifted and how you would correct it next time.

Printmaking rewards repetition: refine the block, vary the inking, and keep evidence of every test.
