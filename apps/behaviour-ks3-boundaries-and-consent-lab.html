Boundaries and Consent Lab

Teacher aim: pupils explore healthy and respectful relationships by deciding what consent looks like, naming pressure and coercion, and rehearsing assertive responses. Work the scenarios as a class on the board, or let pupils run them individually. All names and situations are fictional.

Before we start: three ground rules

This lab is about respect, not blame. As you work through each scene, hold these in mind.

  • Consent must be a free, informed and clear yes. Silence, a shrug or going along with it is not a yes.
  • Anyone can change their mind at any point, and that choice must always be respected.
  • Pressure, guilt-tripping and threats are forms of coercion. A yes given under pressure does not count as consent.
There are no embarrassing reveals here. Every scene is about ordinary social pressure: lending things, photos, joining in, personal space. If anything raised brings up a worry, the help panel at the end shows who to talk to.
