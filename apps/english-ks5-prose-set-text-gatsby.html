          How to use this

          1. Explore. Click each symbol to learn where it recurs and what it carries. The colour of every card matches the symbol.

          2. Bank evidence. Add the symbols and the narrator judgement you find most persuasive.

          3. Judge Nick. The sliders aggregate into a single reading on the reliability meter.

          4. Compose. Turn a thesis, a method and your banked evidence into a model paragraph, then improve on it.

          Core themes

          • The American Dream and its corruption into money and display
          • Love, desire, idealisation: Gatsby loves an image of Daisy
          • Class: old money (East Egg) versus new money (West Egg)
          • Time: "Can't repeat the past? Why of course you can!"
          • Moral carelessness: "They were careless people, Tom and Daisy"

          Context (AO3)

          Jazz Age, 1920s: post-war boom, mass consumerism and conspicuous wealth.

          Prohibition (1920 to 1933): bootlegging is the unspoken source of Gatsby's fortune.

          Disillusion after 1914 to 1918: a generation's idealism gives way to hedonism and moral drift.

          Reception: a commercial disappointment in 1925, later canonised as a study of the American Dream.
