How to use the pitch trainer
  • Match a note: play the target, then sing or hum it back at the same pitch. Play it again and compare. If your voice and the note sound like one tone, you matched it.
  • Higher, lower or same: hear two notes and judge the direction of the second. This trains your ear to notice pitch, the core skill behind pitching accurately.
  • The app does not hear you, so you assess honestly. Honest self-assessment is a real KS3 skill.
  • Keyboard: the buttons are all reachable with Tab and Enter. Switch tabs with the arrow keys when a tab is focused.

Why warm up?
  • Sirens stretch your range smoothly and connect your low and high voice.
  • Scales train accurate, stepwise pitching.
  • Octave leaps build control over big jumps.
  • Humming warms the voice gently and helps you feel resonance.
  • The breathing guide builds the steady airflow that supports long, controlled phrases.

Dynamics and expression words
  • Forte (f) loud; piano (p) quiet.
  • Crescendo gradually louder; diminuendo gradually quieter.
  • Legato smooth and joined; staccato short and detached.
  • Expression also includes phrasing, where you breathe, and the feeling you put into the words.

👥 Singing in parts (ensemble)

Ensemble singing means holding your own line while others sing theirs. Isolate one part to learn it, then hear the parts together to understand how they fit.

Rounds, canons and harmony
  • A round is a melody sung by groups starting at different times; it always fits with itself.
  • A canon is the same idea: one voice imitates another a set distance behind.
  • Two-part harmony here moves mostly in thirds: a second line a comfortable interval above the tune.
  • The skill is keeping your own part steady while you hear another. Learn your line alone first, then add the others.

📚 Vocal technique

Good technique lets you sing with control, accuracy and confidence, and protects your voice.

🧍 Posture

  • Stand tall, feet hip-width apart, weight even.
  • Shoulders relaxed and down, not hunched.
  • Head level; imagine a string lifting the crown of your head.
  • Sitting? Sit forward, back straight, both feet flat.

🌬 Breathing

  • Breathe low: feel your tummy and ribs expand, not your shoulders rising.
  • Take a calm, silent breath before each phrase.
  • Let the air out steadily to support long notes.
  • Use the breathing guide in Warm-ups to practise.

🗣 Diction

  • Crisp consonants carry the words to the listener.
  • Open, tall vowels keep the tone warm and clear.
  • Sing on the vowel, then place the consonant cleanly.
  • Over-pronounce a little when singing in a group.

🎤 Pitching

  • Listen before you sing: hold the note in your head.
  • Aim straight at the centre of the note, not under it.
  • Hum to find a note before singing the word.
  • Use the Pitch trainer to sharpen your ear.

💬 Reflection prompts (KS3)

Use these to evaluate your own singing and set a target, the way musicians do.

  • How accurately did I pitch the notes today, and how do I know?
  • Where did my breath run out, and what could I change in my breathing or phrasing?
  • Which dynamic or articulation made the biggest difference to the feeling of the line?
  • When singing in parts, could I hold my own line while hearing another? What helped?
  • What is one specific target for my next singing session?
