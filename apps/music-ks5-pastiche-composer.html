Teacher notes and assessment links

This demo targets the Advanced composition and arranging strand, specifically free and brief-based composition with stylistic pastiche. It scaffolds the voice-leading conventions examined across the main A-level specifications.

What the checker tests

  • Parallel perfect fifths and octaves between any pair of voices on consecutive chords (the classic chorale fault).
  • Direct (hidden) fifths and octaves into a perfect interval reached by similar motion in the outer voices.
  • Spacing: more than an octave between adjacent upper voices (S-A, A-T).
  • Voice crossing and voice overlap between adjacent parts.
  • Range for each voice against standard SATB tessitura.
  • Large leaps (over a sixth, and any leap of a seventh) in inner and upper voices.
  • Doubling: warns when the leading note is doubled; rewards a complete triad.

Orchestration workshop fold-in

  • Switch the set style to String quartet pastiche: the same four parts are reframed as Violin I, Violin II, Viola and Cello, ranges widen, and the playback timbre changes, so pupils hear how identical harmony reads differently across forces.

Suggested classroom use

  • Load Spot-the-faults phrase, ask pupils to predict the flags before they read them, then correct the phrase to green.
  • Set a short brief (four chords, perfect cadence) and mark live against the rule count.
  • Project the score grid on the whiteboard for whole-class voice-leading drills.

Demonstration tool only. The checker models common conventions and does not replace teacher judgement; expressive exceptions are part of the art.
