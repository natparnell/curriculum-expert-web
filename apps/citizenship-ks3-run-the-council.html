Run the Council

Teacher aim: Pupils act as a local council, share out a fixed budget across public services, then read the live consequences for residents. Covers local and devolved government and the role of the citizen: how councils raise and spend money, the services they run, and how citizens can respond.

Teacher notes and discussion prompts

Curriculum link: KS3 Citizenship programme of study, "the roles played by public institutions and voluntary groups in society, and the ways in which citizens work together to improve their communities"; "the operation of Parliament... and the role of the citizen"; and the structures of local and devolved government.

Key idea: Budgets are choices. There is never enough money for everything residents want, so councillors must prioritise, and citizens hold them to account.

Discuss: Which service would you protect at all costs, and why? Who is harmed by a cut that you cannot see on a dashboard? How is this different from decisions made by the Scottish Parliament, Senedd Cymru, or the UK Parliament at Westminster?

Citizen action: voting in local elections, attending council meetings, signing or starting a petition, writing to a councillor, joining a community group. All are legitimate ways to influence the choices modelled here.
