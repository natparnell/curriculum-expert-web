Humanism: A Non-religious Worldview

Some people answer life's big questions without religion. Humanists believe this one life is precious, that we should use reason and evidence to understand the world, and that we can be kind and fair without believing in a God. In this lesson you will compare Humanist and religious answers to the big questions, explore the Golden Rule that so many worldviews share, and decide how to do the right thing.

Starting point

What do Humanists believe?

Humanism is a worldview: a way of understanding life and how to live well. Here are some important Humanist ideas.

🌍One precious life

Humanists do not believe there is a God or an afterlife, and they say there is no good evidence for one. So this life is the one life we have, which makes it precious. We should make the most of it and help others enjoy theirs.

🔬Reason and evidence

To understand the world, Humanists use reason, evidence and science. They ask questions, look at the proof, and change their minds when better evidence comes along.

🤝Good without religion

Humanists believe people can be good and lead a kind, caring life without religion. They choose to do good because it helps other people and makes the world a better place, not because a holy book or a God tells them to.

🌎People and planet

Human happiness and fairness matter. Humanists believe we should look after one another and care for the planet, so that life is better for everyone now and for those who come after us.
