How Does It Feel?

Teacher aim: children listen attentively to a short piece of music, then choose the face that matches its mood (happy, sleepy, spooky or busy). Use the talk prompts to open conversation about loud and quiet, fast and slow, and how music can make us feel different things.

For the grown-up: how to use this on the carpet
  • No real sound files are used. Each clip is made live by the device, so it works with no internet. Turn the volume up before you start.
  • Play each tune at least twice. The faces stay locked until the children have listened, so they cannot just guess.
  • Ask before they choose: "Is it loud or quiet? Fast or slow? Bouncy or smooth?" Link the sound to the feeling.
  • There is no harsh "wrong". A different choice opens a question, never a buzzer. Every mood can spark good talk.
  • Keyboard friendly: use Tab to move, Space or Enter to choose. Every action is read aloud for screen readers.
  • Curriculum link (EYFS Expressive Arts and Design, and the music programme of study): listen attentively, move to and talk about music, expressing feelings and responses.
