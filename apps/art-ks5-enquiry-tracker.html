How to use this and what it is checking

A strong personal investigation is not a tidy line from brief to outcome. It is a sustained enquiry: questions open up lines of thinking, experiments test them in materials, and decisions record where you changed your mind and why. This map asks you to make that visible.

  • Question cards are open lines of enquiry, for example a question about an artist, an idea, or a technique.
  • Experiment cards are things you actually made or tried, in any media.
  • Decision cards capture a judgement that changed your direction, with your reason.
  • Use the link control on a card, then click a second card to draw an arrow showing how one idea led to another.

The review rewards depth (questions answered by experiments), reflection (decisions that follow experiments) and breadth (more than one strand of enquiry). It flags dead ends, isolated cards and a map that is all making with no thinking, or all thinking with no making.

Keyboard: Tab to a card, use arrow keys to move it on the map, press L then Tab to a second card and Enter to link, Delete to remove.
