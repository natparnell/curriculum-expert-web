    Quick reference

    Rule of thirds
    Split the frame into nine equal parts. Place key subjects on the lines or their crossings, not dead centre.
    Golden ratio
    Lines at about 0.382 and 0.618 of the frame. The crossings sit slightly closer to centre, giving a gentler, classical balance used in many paintings.
    Power point
    Where two grid lines cross. The eye is naturally drawn here, so a focal point here feels balanced and intentional.
    Space
    Leaving room in the direction a subject faces or moves (lead room) makes a picture feel settled rather than cramped.
