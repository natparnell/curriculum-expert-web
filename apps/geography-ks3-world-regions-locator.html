Locating the world: lines, hemispheres and regions

The Earth is divided by imaginary lines that help us describe location precisely. Lines of latitude run east to west, parallel to the Equator (0°), and tell us how far north or south a place is. Lines of longitude (meridians) run north to south from pole to pole, measured east or west of the Prime Meridian (0°) at Greenwich, London.

The Equator splits the world into the Northern and Southern Hemispheres. The Prime Meridian and the 180° line split it into the Eastern and Western Hemispheres. Most of Africa, all of Russia, Asia and the Middle East sit in the Eastern Hemisphere; the great bulk of Africa, Asia and Russia lies in the Northern Hemisphere.

Knowing where the major regions sit, and the physical features within them (the Sahara, the Himalayas, the Nile, the Ural Mountains) and their major cities, lets you reason about climate, trade, population and human activity across the world.
