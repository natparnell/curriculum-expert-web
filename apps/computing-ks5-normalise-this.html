ACID transactions: an atomic transfer

A normalised design is only safe if updates are reliable too. Run a money transfer between two trip-fund accounts. A transaction is atomic: it either fully commits or fully rolls back.

Transfer £80 from Year 12 to Year 13. Try a clean commit, then a crash to see why ACID matters.
What ACID guarantees
Atomicity: all steps happen or none do. Consistency: only valid states are written (the total stays £620). Isolation: concurrent transactions do not interfere. Durability: a committed result survives a crash.
