Observation Logbook

KS4 Art and Design · AO3: Recording ideas, observations and insights

Teacher aim: Pupils complete a timed observational drawing, then write a prompted annotation. The app checks their recording and reflection against AO3 expectations and gives targeted feedback.

1Choose your study object

Set the timer first, then draw from the real object in front of you. The on-screen pad is your quick study sheet; the prompts will refer to the object you pick here.

2Timed observational study

A short time limit forces decisions about the most important marks. Pick a challenge length, then keep your eyes on the object more than on the page.

2:00

Standard timed study: draw the whole object, looking up at it as often as you can.

Press Start study, then draw your seashell here. The pad is just for a quick study; the real learning is in looking.

3Annotate your study (AO3)

AO3 rewards recording that shows close looking and personal insight, not a description of what the object is. Answer each prompt in a sentence or two.

Observe

AO3 record What did you actually notice when you looked closely? Name a specific detail (a line, edge, mark, or shape) and where it sits.

0 words

Technique

AO3 technique How did you record it? Refer to a media skill: line, tone, shading, mark-making, pressure or proportion.

0 words

Reflect

AO3 insight What worked, what did not, and what will you try next time? This is your personal insight.

      How this maps to AO3

      This logbook targets Assessment Objective 3 across GCSE Art and Design specifications: "Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions as work progresses." The timed study builds observational recording skill; the three prompts (Observe, Technique, Reflect) build the written reflection examiners look for.
