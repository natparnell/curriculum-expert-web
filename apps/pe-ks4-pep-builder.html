Physical Education · Key Stage 4 · GCSE coursework scaffold

PEP Builder

Teacher aim: pupils plan, log and analyse a six-week Personal Exercise Programme, applying the principles of training and judging their own data against FITT and SMART criteria. Live feedback marks each stage; auto-generated graphs drive the evaluation.

1. Plan your programme

A strong PEP plan names the component of fitness, applies all four FITT principles and sets a SMART target. Fill the boxes; the marker on the right updates the moment you change anything.

2. Log your sessions

Record every session across the six weeks. Enter the session distance or reps (your workload), your average working heart rate, and your perceived effort (RPE, 1 light to 10 maximal). The app checks that you are applying progressive overload.

Editable training log. For each session enter week, the activity, the workload in your chosen unit, working heart rate in beats per minute, and rate of perceived exertion from one to ten.

Add at least four sessions across two or more weeks to unlock the overload check.

3. Progress graphs

These charts are built from your log. Use the toggle to compare workload, heart rate and effort. A falling heart rate at the same workload is a classic sign of improving fitness.

Workload by session

4. Analyse and evaluate

This is where the marks are. Read your own data summary, then answer the evaluation prompts. The app checks your reasoning against the data and the GCSE assessment criteria.

a) Did your data show improvement? Justify using two specific figures from your log.
b) Which principle of training best explains the change you saw? Name it and explain.
c) Evaluate one limitation of your PEP and state how you would adapt it next time.
