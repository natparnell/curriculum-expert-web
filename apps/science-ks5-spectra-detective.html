How a chemist reads these spectra

Mass spectrum: the molecular ion peak (highest m/z) gives the relative molecular mass. Losses from it are fragments: 15 = CH₃, 29 = CHO or C₂H₅, 17 = OH, 43 = C₂H₃O (acylium) or C₃H₇.

IR: a broad 2500–3300 band = O–H of a carboxylic acid; a sharp strong ~1700 = C=O; a broad ~3300 = O–H of an alcohol; ~1050–1300 = C–O.

¹H NMR: the number of signals = sets of chemically distinct protons; the chemical shift suggests the environment; the integration ratio gives relative proton counts; splitting (n+1) reveals neighbours.

¹³C NMR: the number of signals = sets of distinct carbons. A peak near 170–185 ppm signals a carbonyl carbon.
