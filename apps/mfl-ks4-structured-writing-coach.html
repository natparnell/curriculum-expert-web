How Edexcel GCSE writing is marked, and how to use this coach

The Edexcel GCSE writing paper sets three task types. A short structured response asks you to write a set number of sentences (often around 40 to 50 words at Foundation, longer at Higher) covering required bullet points. A longer open-ended response asks you to give and justify opinions on a theme. A translation into the target language tests accuracy of vocabulary, grammar, spelling and accents.

What the criteria reward

Content & coverageEvery bullet point answered, with relevant detail.
Range of languageVaried vocabulary, connectives and complex structures.
Three time framesClear use of past, present and future tenses.
Opinions with reasonsViews justified with because / since / in my opinion.
AccuracyCorrect verbs, genders, agreement, accents and word order.
