How to use this coach

Staging is meaning. Where an actor stands, how close they are to others, and whether they are raised or low all communicate to the audience before a word is spoken. This coach lets you treat the stage as a top-down map so you can plan your blocking deliberately rather than by accident.

Build moment by moment. A scene is a sequence of beats. Arrange your ensemble for the opening, press Capture this moment, then add a new moment and re-arrange for the next beat. Use Walk the scene to watch the actors move between your captured positions, exactly as you would in a run-through.

Read the proxemics. The readout panel interprets your choices in Eduqas terms: intimate, personal, social and public distance, the implied status of a raised actor, and where audience focus falls. Treat the feedback as a director's eye, not a mark scheme.

Proxemics
The use of distance between performers to signal relationship, status and emotion.
Levels
Differences in height (floor, standing, rostra) used to show power, hierarchy or isolation.
Blocking
The planned positions and movements of actors through a scene.
Focus
The technique of drawing the audience's attention to one point on stage.
Ensemble
A group of performers working as a unit, reacting to and supporting one another.
Marking the moment
A rehearsal technique that highlights a key beat so the audience knows it matters.
