The maths behind this model

Net mass balance Bn = accumulation − ablation (metres water equivalent per year). Positive means the glacier gains mass; negative means it loses mass.

ELA is the altitude where annual accumulation exactly balances annual ablation. Warmer, lower-accumulation years push the ELA up the valley; colder, snowier years pull it down.

Lag, not instant response. The snout reacts to the cumulative balance of the recent years (a smoothed response time), so a single good year does not immediately reverse a retreating glacier. That delay is why glacier snouts are used as a long-term climate signal.

Figures here are illustrative and rounded for teaching. They are realistic in scale for an Alpine-type valley glacier but are not measurements of any specific glacier.
