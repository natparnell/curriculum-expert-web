SUVAT working

Exam scenarios

Scenario 1: stone thrown horizontally from a cliff

A stone is projected horizontally at 15 m/s from the top of a 20 m cliff. Find the time to reach the sea and the horizontal distance travelled. Take g = 9.8.

uₓ = 15, uᵧ = 0, h = 20
Vertical (taking down as positive):
  s = uᵧt + ½gt²
  20 = 0 + ½(9.8)t²
  t² = 40/9.8 = 4.082 → t = 2.02 s
Horizontal: x = uₓt = 15 × 2.02 = 30.3 m
Scenario 2: projectile over level ground

A ball is kicked at 25 m/s at 35° above the horizontal from ground level. Find the time of flight, the range and the greatest height. Take g = 9.8.

uₓ = 25cos35° = 20.48, uᵧ = 25sin35° = 14.34
Time of flight (return to y = 0):
  0 = uᵧt − ½gt² → t = 2uᵧ/g
  t = 2(14.34)/9.8 = 2.93 s
Range: R = uₓt = 20.48 × 2.93 = 59.9 m
Greatest height: H = uᵧ²/(2g) = 14.34²/19.6 = 10.5 m

Modelling assumptions

  • The projectile is modelled as a particle (all mass at a point, no spin).
  • No air resistance, so the only force acting is weight.
  • Gravity g is constant and acts vertically downwards.
  • The ground is horizontal and the launch is in a vertical plane.

How it works

Resolve the launch velocity into components: uₓ = u·cosθ stays constant, while uᵧ = u·sinθ changes under gravity. Horizontally there is no acceleration, so x = uₓt. Vertically the acceleration is −g, so y = h + uᵧt − ½gt² and vᵧ = uᵧ − gt.

At the highest point vᵧ = 0, giving the time to apex t = uᵧ/g. The flight ends when y returns to ground level (or reaches the launch base in the height mode), found by solving the quadratic for t.
