Money ideas in play

Student loans are not like normal debt
A UK Plan 5 student loan is repaid at 9% of income above a threshold, only while you earn over it, and is written off after the term ends. It does not appear on a credit file and does not affect a credit score directly, though lenders may consider the monthly deduction.
How a credit score is built
Scores rise with on-time payments, low use of available credit, a long stable history and being on the electoral roll. They fall with missed payments, high balances, lots of applications at once and county court judgments.
Income tax, National Insurance and take-home pay
A salary is gross pay. Income tax (20% basic rate above the personal allowance) and National Insurance are deducted before you see your money. Net pay is what lands in your account.
Pensions and the employer match
Auto-enrolment puts a slice of pay into a pension. Many employers match extra contributions, so opting up can be close to free money that compounds for decades.
Mortgages: deposit, term and total interest
A bigger deposit means borrowing less and usually a lower interest rate. A longer term lowers the monthly payment but raises the total interest paid over the life of the loan.
