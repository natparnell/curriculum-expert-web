Journey Through Digestion

Teacher aim: Pupils follow a mouthful of food through the human digestive system, tap each organ to learn its job, then prove their understanding. Folds in a heartbeat circuit to link digestion with the circulatory system.

1. Follow the food

Press Start the journey to send the mouthful on its trip, or tap any organ to learn what it does. Use Tab and Enter to move between organs with a keyboard.

Tap an organ to begin

The digestive system

Each organ has its own job. Press a part of the body, or press Start the journey to watch the food travel from the mouth all the way down.

You have explored 0 of 5 organs. Explore all 5 to unlock the quiz.

2. Show what you know

Pick one answer for each question. You get instant feedback explaining why.

Where does digestion start?

Which organ takes the goodness (nutrients) into the blood?

What is the main job of the large intestine?

Put the journey in order. What comes straight after the stomach?

All four correct. Well done, you know the journey.

3. Heartbeat circuit (the link to circulation)

Once food is digested, the blood carries the goodness around the body. The heart is the pump. Tap the beat pad in a steady rhythm to set the heart beating, and read your beats per minute.

Tap the pad to start the beat.
0 bpm
Tap here in time, or press Space.

A resting child's heart usually beats about 80 to 120 times a minute. When you run around, the muscles need more oxygen from the blood, so the heart beats faster.
