Rights on Trial

Citizenship • Key Stage 5 • Law, rights and global justice

Teaching aim: Students argue landmark human-rights cases, weigh competing rights, and predict how the court balanced them. They then compare their prediction with the actual ruling and the legal principle behind it.

Choose a case to try

Each case sets a real-world conflict where two protected rights collide. Most rights under the European Convention on Human Rights (and the Human Rights Act 1998) are qualified: courts ask whether an interference is lawful, pursues a legitimate aim, and is a proportionate balance. Your job is to weigh the rights, build the stronger arguments, and forecast the verdict.

1. The rights in conflict

Two protected rights are pulling in opposite directions. Read each, then decide how much weight the court should give the claimant's side.
