🧊 Identity Iceberg

Teacher aim: Pupils explore that identity has a visible "tip" and a much larger hidden depth. They sort traits for a character, then their own iceberg, and challenge stereotypes and assumptions. Strand: identity, diversity and respect for difference.

Pick a character to build

Choose one fictional pupil. You will sort what people see from what stays hidden.

Build the iceberg

Drag a trait onto the iceberg. Put what people notice at first near the tip (above the water) and what stays private in the deep (below the water). You can also click a trait then press Enter to place it.

Keyboard: Tab to a trait, press Enter to send it to the top zone, then use the placed-trait buttons (Up / Deeper / Remove) to move it.

Trait bank

For a character. Decide which are usually visible and which are usually hidden.

Your own iceberg

Now tick the traits that are true for you. Mark whether each is something classmates can usually see or something they would not know unless you told them. Nothing is saved or shared.
