4Take it further

The exam definitions you need
  • Diffusion: net movement of particles from a higher to a lower concentration (down a concentration gradient). Passive, no energy from respiration.
  • Osmosis: the diffusion of water across a partially permeable membrane, from a dilute solution (high water potential) to a concentrated solution (low water potential).
  • Active transport: moving substances against a concentration gradient, from low to high. Requires energy from respiration (ATP), for example mineral ions into root hair cells.

In this lab the solute (sugar) is too large to cross the membrane, so only water moves. Water always travels towards the more concentrated solution.

Link: the cell cycle and mitosis

A cell that takes in too much water can burst; in plants the cell wall stops this and the cell becomes turgid. Healthy cells in the right osmotic balance can divide. One full cell cycle has three stages:

  • Growth and DNA synthesis (interphase): the cell grows, makes new sub-cellular structures (ribosomes, mitochondria) and copies each chromosome so there are two copies of every strand of DNA.
  • Mitosis: one set of chromosomes is pulled to each end of the cell and the nucleus divides.
  • Cytokinesis: the cytoplasm and cell membrane divide to form two genetically identical daughter cells.

Mitosis is used for growth, repair of damaged tissue and, in some organisms, asexual reproduction. Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that divide by mitosis to produce cells that can specialise.
