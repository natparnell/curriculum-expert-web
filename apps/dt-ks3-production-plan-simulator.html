KS3 Design and Technology · Make

Production Plan Simulator

Teacher aim: Pupils sequence the manufacture of a product, allocate the correct precise tools and quality checks, then run the plan to find bottlenecks and errors. Covers the National Curriculum Make strand: planning, selecting from wider materials and components, and using precise tools, techniques and processes accurately.

📋 The brief: a pine and acrylic phone stand

Workshop technician Mr Okafor needs a one-off prototype phone stand made from a pine base with a clear acrylic upright. Your job is to plan the manufacture so it can be made safely, accurately and in the right order. Get the plan right, then press Run the plan to simulate the build.

  • Order the manufacturing stages logically (you cannot finish a part before you have marked and cut it).
  • Pick the most precise, correct tool for each stage.
  • Choose the quality checks that actually catch errors.

🔢 1. Sequence the manufacture

Drag the stages into a sensible order, or use the keyboard.

Keyboard: focus a stage with Tab, press Enter or Space to pick it up, then use ↑ / ↓ to move it and Enter to drop.

    🔧 2. Allocate a precise tool to each stage

    Choose the tool a workshop would actually use for accuracy and safety.

    ✅ 3. Choose your quality checks

    Pick the checks that genuinely catch manufacturing errors. Tick at least two.

    Press Run to simulate the build with your plan.

    What the simulator is checking
    • Stages in a workable order (mark out, then cut, then shape, then finish, then assemble).
    • Each tool matched to its task for accuracy (for example a try square for marking right angles, not a ruler alone).
    • Quality checks placed so errors are caught before they are made permanent.
    • Safety steps (goggles, guards) present before machining.
