Gear and Pulley Playground ⚙️

Design and Technology · Key Stage 2 · Mechanical systems

Teacher aim
Pupils explore how gears and pulleys of different sizes change the speed and direction of movement, then design a mechanism to do a chosen job.

Gear Lab: change the teeth, watch the ratio

Turn the driver gear (blue). Watch how the follower gear (orange) speeds up, slows down and spins the other way. Drag the sliders or use the arrow keys on each slider.

What is happening? Pick the teeth, then read the ratio. A bigger follower turns slower; a smaller follower turns faster.

Two meshing gears always turn in opposite directions. Try making the follower spin twice as fast as the driver.

Pulley Lab: a belt instead of teeth

Pulleys are joined by a belt, not by teeth. Change the wheel sizes and see the ratio. Unlike gears, two pulleys on a normal belt turn the same way.

What is happening? A normal belt keeps both pulleys turning the same way. A crossed belt flips the follower so it turns the opposite way.

Try a small driver and a big follower to slow the follower down, like the wheels on a bicycle.

Cam Toy: turning round into up and down

A cam is a special shape on a turning axle. As it spins, it pushes a follower up and down. Choose a cam shape and watch the bobbing toy on top.

3
What is happening? The cam changes round movement (rotary) into up and down movement (linear and reciprocating). Different shapes give different bobs.

The off-centre round cam gives a smooth, even bob. The snail cam lifts slowly then drops suddenly. Which would make a frog hop in a jerky way?
