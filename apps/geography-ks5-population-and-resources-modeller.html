Malthus vs Boserup

Theory recap: Malthus (1798) and Boserup (1965)
AspectThomas MalthusEster Boserup
Core claimPopulation grows geometrically, food only arithmetically.Population growth is the driver of agricultural change.
The ceilingA fixed carrying capacity. Overshoot triggers crisis.The ceiling is raised by innovation, so it moves.
What stops growthPositive checks (famine, disease, war) and preventive checks.Necessity prompts intensification, not collapse.
View of technologyCannot keep pace with demand.Demand pressure invents the technology needed.
Real-world echoesSahel famines, Club of Rome limits-to-growth modelling.Green Revolution yields, intensified rice terracing.

In this model the lens changes one thing: under Boserup, sustained pressure (high population relative to food) feeds back into the technology term, lifting yields over time. Under Malthus, technology is fixed and the capacity is a hard ceiling that population overshoots.

Demonstration model for classroom use. Figures are illustrative and simplified to expose the Malthus and Boserup mechanisms, not to forecast any real region.
