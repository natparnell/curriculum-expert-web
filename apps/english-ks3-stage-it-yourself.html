Stage It Yourself

Teacher aim: pupils explore how a director's choices (where actors stand, where they look, how the stage is lit) shape an audience's interpretation of the same Shakespeare lines. Two plays, one drag-and-drop stage, instant feedback on the mood you have created.

Upstage (far, distant) Downstage (close to audience) Stage right Stage left

Drag the actor tokens around the stage with a finger, mouse or pen. Keyboard: Tab to an actor, then arrow keys to move, Enter to read the new interpretation aloud.

Blocking notes

Blocking means where the actors stand and how close they are. Try moving them apart, together, or turning the focus character downstage towards the audience.

What this staging suggests

Place the actors to begin.
Drag the actors onto the stage and choose a lighting state. The app reads your choices and tells you what an audience might feel.

Stage It Yourself uses short, out of copyright passages from Shakespeare's plays. Character names are the playwright's own. Demonstration tool for classroom discussion: there are no single correct answers, only choices a director can defend.
