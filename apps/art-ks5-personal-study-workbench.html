Personal Study Workbench

KS5 Art and Design · Critical, contextual and theoretical analysis

Teacher aim: students build one evidenced analytical paragraph for the related study (personal study) by linking a critical point, a quotation and a connection to their own practice, then act on targeted feedback before drafting in full.

Structure prompts for the related study (open for the writing frame)

A strong personal study paragraph triangulates three things. Use this order; the workbench below builds exactly this shape.

1. Critical point (your judgement)State an analytical claim about how the work creates meaning. Use the formal elements and contextual ideas, not just description.
2. Evidence: quotationSupport the claim with a quotation from a critic, the artist, or a theoretical source. Embed it; do not drop it in.
3. Link to your own practiceShow how this analysis has shaped a decision in your portfolio. This is the personal in personal study.

Across the whole essay, repeat this cycle, vary your sources, and keep returning to your developing practice.
