KS2 Science · Electricity

💡 Light the Bulb

Teaching aim: build a simple circuit, then use it to test whether everyday objects are conductors or insulators. Pupils learn that a bulb only lights when the loop is complete and the switch is closed.

1. Build and test the circuit

Drag an object into the gap in the wire. Close the switch. Does the bulb light?

wire current flowing
Put an object in the gap and close the switch to find out.

2. Test bench

Pick an object to put in the gap. Tap or drag it onto the test gap.

Keyboard: press Tab to an object, then Enter to place it in the gap. Use the Switch button to turn the power on. Press Remove object to try another.

3. What did you find?

Each object you test is sorted here. A conductor lets the current pass and lights the bulb. An insulator blocks it.

Did you know? Metals like copper and steel are good conductors. That is why wires are made of metal. Materials like plastic, wood and rubber are insulators, so they keep us safe.
