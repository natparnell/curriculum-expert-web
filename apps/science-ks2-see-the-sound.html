ScienceKS2 Sound

See the Sound 🔊

Sound is made when something vibrates. Pluck, bang, and watch the vibrations turn into a wave.

Teacher aim: Pupils explore how vibrations make sound, and how changing pitch (how high or low) and volume (how loud or quiet) changes the wave they see.

1. Make a vibration

Choose an instrument, then tap or click it (or press the Pluck button). Watch it wobble.

Tip: tap the string to pluck it. The faster it wobbles, the higher the pitch.

2. Change the sound

Move the sliders. Watch what happens to the vibration and the wave below.

Low (slow wobble) on the left, high (fast wobble) on the right.

Quiet (small wobble) on the left, loud (big wobble) on the right.

3. The live waveform

A wave is a picture of a vibration. Tall waves are loud. Squashed-up waves are high.

Height of the wave = how loud (volume) How close the bumps are = how high (pitch)

Pluck the string to start. The vibration becomes a wave you can see.

What scientists know about sound

  • Sounds are made by vibrations. When something moves back and forth quickly, it makes a sound.
  • Vibrations travel to your ears through the air (and through water and solids too).
  • Pitch is how high or low a sound is. Faster vibrations make a higher pitch.
  • Volume is how loud or quiet a sound is. Bigger vibrations make a louder sound.
  • A tight, thin or short string vibrates faster, so it sounds higher. A loose, thick or long string sounds lower.
