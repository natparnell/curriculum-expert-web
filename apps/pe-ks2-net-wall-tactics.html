Net & Wall Games Tactics

Teaching aim: to understand and apply the basic principles of attacking and defending in net and wall games (badminton, tennis, table tennis, short tennis). Learn to hit into space to move your opponent, return to a central base to cover the court, and vary your shots to choose the right one for each situation.

3 Attacking and defending principles

Attack

Hit into space

Send the ball away from your opponent so they have to move and stretch to reach it.

Attack

Move them around

Make your opponent run side to side and front to back. A tired, off-balance player makes mistakes.

Attack

Vary your shots

Mix depth, height and direction: a deep shot then a drop shot keeps them guessing.

Defend

Return to base

After each shot, move back to a central position so you can cover the whole court.

Defend

Defend your space

Do not leave a big gap open. Watch where your opponent is aiming and get ready.

Both

Right shot, right time

Choose the shot that fits the situation: attack when they are out of position, play safe when you are.

4 Reflection prompts

  • Which shot moved your opponent the furthest, and why?
  • When did returning to base help you defend the next shot?
  • How did changing the depth or direction of your shots cause problems for your opponent?
  • When is it better to play a safe shot rather than going for a winner?
  • What would you do differently to win the next point?
