The Euthyphro dilemma in one breath

Is the pious loved by the gods because it is pious, or pious because it is loved by the gods? If God commands the good because it is good, morality is independent of God (the arbitrariness problem dissolves but God is no longer the source). If something is good only because God commands it, then goodness looks arbitrary: cruelty would be good had God commanded it.

Free will vocabulary

Hard determinism: every event, including choice, is caused; free will is an illusion, so moral blame is problematic. Libertarianism: humans have genuine, undetermined freedom of the will, so we are responsible. Compatibilism (soft determinism): determinism is true, yet "free" can mean "acting from your own desires without external compulsion", so responsibility survives.

Conscience: rival accounts

Aquinas: conscience is reason making moral judgements (synderesis and conscientia), God-given but fallible. Butler: conscience is the supreme God-given faculty guiding us. Freud: conscience is the super-ego, internalised social and parental authority, not a voice of God.
