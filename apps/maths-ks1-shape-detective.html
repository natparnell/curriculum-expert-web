KS1 Maths · Geometry

🔍 Shape Detective

Teacher aim: pupils investigate the properties of 2D and 3D shapes (sides, vertices, edges, faces), spot 2D faces on 3D solids, sort shapes by a property, and describe quarter, half and three-quarter turns and right angles.

Be a shape detective

Pick a shape. Choose what to count, then tap each side, corner (vertex) or face. The shape tells you if you are right.

Sides found 0 of 3
Tap each side of the triangle.

Keyboard: press Tab to move between parts of the shape, then Enter or Space to count one.

Faces are flat 2D shapes

A 3D solid is made of flat faces. Each face is a 2D shape you already know. Which 2D shapes can you see on this solid?

Choose Faces above, then tap each flat face.

Sort by property

Drag (or pick up with the keyboard) each shape into the right basket. Rule: shapes with 4 sides versus shapes that do not have 4 sides.

✅ Has 4 sides

❌ Not 4 sides

Sort all six shapes to win.

Keyboard: Tab to a shape, press Enter to pick it up, then press 1 for the left basket or 2 for the right basket.

Turn the robot

A quarter turn is a right angle. Make the robot face the target arrow using turns. Two quarter turns make a half turn.

Tap a turn button to move the robot. Match the gold arrow.
