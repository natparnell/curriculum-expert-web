What is happening

Energy is never created or destroyed, only transferred between stores or dissipated to the surroundings. This is the principle of conservation of energy.

As the object falls or swings down, its gravitational potential store empties and fills its kinetic store. On the way back up the transfer reverses. In a perfect closed system (no friction) the total stays constant and the green total bar never moves.

  • Gravitational potential energy: GPE = m g h, with g = 9.8 N/kg.
  • Kinetic energy: KE = ½ m v².
  • At any instant in a closed system, KE + GPE = total.

Switch on friction (or air resistance) and some energy is transferred by heating into a thermal store of the surroundings. That energy is now spread out and cannot easily be recovered, so the useful mechanical total falls and the swing or roll dies away. The Sankey diagram shows the split between useful output and wasted energy, and efficiency = useful output ÷ total input.

Four ways energy is transferred

Unwanted transfers are reduced by lubrication (cuts friction), insulation and choosing materials with low thermal conductivity (slow the rate of energy transfer by heating), and streamlining (cuts air resistance).
