Three Ways to Make Theatre

Teaching aim: to explore how three contrasting practitioners and approaches, Stanislavski's naturalism, Brecht's epic theatre and the ensemble world of physical theatre, change the feeling of the same scene for an audience, building the vocabulary and choices that lead towards GCSE Drama.

Comparing the three approaches

None of these is the "right" way to make theatre. Each does a different job. Many modern plays mix all three.

Naturalism

Konstantin Stanislavski
  • Goal: believable, lifelike acting.
  • The actor really thinks and feels as the character.
  • Given circumstances: the who, where, when and why of the scene.
  • The magic if: "What would I do if I were really in this situation?"
  • The fourth wall stays up, so we forget we are watching a play.
Audience feels: drawn in, like watching real life.

Epic theatre

Bertolt Brecht
  • Goal: make the audience think about the message.
  • Reminds us it is a play, on purpose.
  • Devices: direct address, narration, placards and signs.
  • Breaking the fourth wall: actors speak to us.
  • The alienation effect: stop us getting lost in emotion so we can judge the ideas.
Audience feels: alert, thinking, questioning.

Physical theatre

Ensemble companies, e.g. Frantic Assembly
  • Goal: tell the story through the body.
  • Movement, shape and ensemble do the work, often without words.
  • The whole group can become objects, weather or feelings.
  • Strong stage images stay in the memory.
  • The body shows what words might not say.
Audience feels: gripped by a striking visual image.

Reflection prompts

Talk these through with a partner, or jot a few sentences for your drama log.

  1. For the letter scene, which approach would you choose, and what do you want the audience to feel at the end?
  2. Stanislavski wants you lost in the story; Brecht wants you thinking. Which is more useful for a play about an important issue, and why?
  3. Describe one stage image from physical theatre you created. What idea or emotion did it show without words?
  4. Where might you mix two approaches in a single performance? Give an example moment.
  5. Which new word from today (given circumstances, magic if, alienation effect, ensemble) will you use in your next rehearsal?
