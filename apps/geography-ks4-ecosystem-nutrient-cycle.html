Ecosystem Nutrient Cycle

Teaching aim: model the tropical rainforest nutrient cycle (biomass, litter and soil stores) and investigate how deforestation breaks the loop, raising leaching and lowering soil fertility.

Teacher notes and exam links
  • Specification fit: AQA / Edexcel / OCR GCSE Geography, The Living World: structure of an ecosystem, nutrient cycling, tropical rainforest and the impacts of deforestation.
  • Key idea: in a tropical rainforest the biomass store is largest because high temperatures and rainfall give rapid growth. Decomposition is fast, so litter is small; most nutrients are locked in living plants. The soil store is thin because nutrients are taken up quickly and heavy rain causes leaching.
  • Deforestation effect: removing biomass breaks root uptake, exposes soil to heavy rain, increases leaching and surface runoff, and reduces leaf fall. Within a few years the soil loses fertility (laterisation), so cleared land is often abandoned.
  • Stretch: contrast with a cold environment (tundra) where slow decomposition makes litter the dominant store and biomass is small. Ask pupils to redraw the diagram for that biome.
