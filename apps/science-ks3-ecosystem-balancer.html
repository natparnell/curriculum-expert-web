How to read the model
  • Grass grows in the sunlight but is eaten by rabbits.
  • Rabbits breed when there is grass, and are eaten by foxes.
  • Foxes breed only when there are enough rabbits to eat.
  • A healthy ecosystem settles into a steady pyramid of numbers: lots of grass, fewer rabbits, very few foxes.
  • Remove or overload one level and the whole chain can crash. That is interdependence.
