◆ What you are seeing

All living things are built from cells, the fundamental unit of life. Some organisms are made of a single cell (unicellular, such as bacteria, amoeba and yeast); most living things you can see are multicellular.

Movement of materials: diffusion

Substances such as oxygen, carbon dioxide and dissolved food move by diffusion: the net movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration, down a concentration gradient, until evenly spread. The cell membrane controls what diffuses in and out.

Unicellular life

A single-celled organism carries out every life process on its own: feeding, respiration, movement and reproduction, all within one cell.
