Does God Exist? Argument Lab

Teacher aim: build the Design argument and the First Cause (cosmological) argument premise by premise, then test them against GCSE counter-arguments and judge whether each argument still stands. Strand: Existence of God and revelation.

Choose an argument for God's existence

Each argument is a chain of reasoning. Pick one, then drag its bricks into the right order so each premise leads to the conclusion.

Build it: the Design Argument

Drag each brick into a numbered slot, or use the keyboard: press a brick with Enter to pick it up, then press a slot to drop it. The bricks are shuffled, so think about which premise comes first.

Stress test: the Design Argument

Now the critics fire back. For each counter-argument, decide: does it weaken the argument, or can the argument answer it? You will then write your own judgement.

Revelation check: a different route to belief

The arguments above use reason. Many believers say God is also known through revelation: God showing himself to people. Sort each statement into the right kind of revelation, then weigh the main critique.

Drag or keyboard-select each card to a column. General revelation = God known through nature and conscience, open to everyone. Special revelation = a direct, specific encounter (visions, scripture, miracles, Jesus for Christians).
