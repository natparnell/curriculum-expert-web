Newton-Raphson Visualiser

Teaching aim: see how the Newton-Raphson iteration uses a tangent at xₙ to reach the next estimate xₙ₊₁, why a change of sign locates a root in an interval, and how the trapezium rule approximates the area under the same curve. Tap, drag or use the keyboard.

Interactive graph

Tap or click the graph to set a starting estimate x₀, then press Iterate to drop the tangent. You can also drag the blue point.

The method

Newton-Raphson: xₙ₊₁ = xₙ − f(xₙ) / f′(xₙ). Each step follows the tangent at xₙ down to the x-axis.

Watch for the failure cases: a turning point where f′(xₙ) is near zero throws the tangent almost flat, and the next estimate can leap far away.
