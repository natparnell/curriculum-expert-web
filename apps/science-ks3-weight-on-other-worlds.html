Weight on Other Worlds W = m × g

Teacher aim: Pupils see that mass stays the same everywhere, but weight changes with gravitational field strength (g). Drop an object on different worlds, read the live W = m g calculation, then predict weights yourself.

1 Choose a world, then drop the rock

Mass is how much matter is in the rock. It does not change when you move worlds.

Drag the rock upward and let go to drop it, or press Space / the Drop button. Heavier gravity makes it fall faster.

2 Live weight calculation

W = 2.0 × 9.8 = 19.6 N

World: Earth g = 9.8 N/kg Mass: 2.0 kg
On Earth this rock would weigh 19.6 N. Pick another world to compare.

Bonus: why do we get seasons? (axial tilt)

Earth is tilted on its axis by about 23.5°. This tilt, not our distance from the Sun, causes the seasons. Move the slider to change the tilt and see what happens to summer and winter.
