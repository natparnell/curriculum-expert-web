The theory you need

Explainer

Dynamic equilibrium

In a closed system, a reversible reaction reaches dynamic equilibrium when the rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction. The macroscopic properties (concentrations, pressures, colour) stay constant, but molecules are still reacting in both directions. Equilibrium can be approached from either side and reaches the same position under the same conditions.

The equilibrium constant Kc

For the general homogeneous equilibrium aA + bB ⇌ cC + dD, the equilibrium constant in terms of concentration is written:

Kc = [C]c[D]d[A]a[B]b

Products go on top, reactants on the bottom, each raised to the power of its balancing number. Square brackets mean equilibrium concentration in mol dm⁻³. The units of Kc are worked out by substituting the units and cancelling, so they depend on the equation. Pure solids and pure liquids are left out of the expression.

The equilibrium constant Kp

For equilibria involving gases, we use partial pressures. The partial pressure of a gas is its mole fraction multiplied by the total pressure:

p(X) = x(X) × Ptotal   where  x(X) = moles of Xtotal moles of gas

Kp is then written like Kc but with partial pressures replacing concentrations:

Kp = p(C)c p(D)dp(A)a p(B)b

Only gases appear in Kp. Units are found in the same way, using the unit of pressure (atm, kPa or Pa).

Le Chatelier's principle

If a change is imposed on a system at equilibrium, the position of equilibrium shifts to oppose that change:

  • Concentration: adding a reactant shifts the position to the right (toward products); removing product also shifts right.
  • Pressure (gases): increasing total pressure shifts the position to the side with fewer moles of gas. In the Haber process, 4 moles of reactant gas form 2 moles of product, so high pressure favours ammonia.
  • Temperature: increasing temperature shifts the position in the endothermic direction. The Haber forward reaction is exothermic, so heating reduces the yield of ammonia.
The single most examined point. Only a change in temperature changes the value of Kc or Kp. Changing concentration or pressure, or adding a catalyst, shifts the position of equilibrium but the system always returns to the same K. A catalyst speeds up the forward and reverse reactions equally, so it reaches equilibrium faster without changing the yield or K.

The Haber process: a compromise

N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ is exothermic. Thermodynamics wants low temperature (higher yield) but kinetics wants high temperature (faster rate). Industry uses a compromise: about 450 °C, 200 atm and an iron catalyst, with unreacted gases recycled. High pressure is favourable for yield but costly and hazardous, so the chosen pressure is also a compromise.

Exam-style questions

OCR A H432
Q12 marks

Write an expression for Kc for the equilibrium 2SO₂(g) + O₂(g) ⇌ 2SO₃(g) and state its units.

Kc = [SO₃]² / ([SO₂]²[O₂])  (1 mark)

Units: (mol dm⁻³)² ÷ [(mol dm⁻³)² × (mol dm⁻³)] = dm³ mol⁻¹  (1 mark)

Q23 marks

At equilibrium, 2.0 mol H₂, 2.0 mol I₂ and 12.0 mol HI are present in a 1.0 dm³ vessel for H₂(g) + I₂(g) ⇌ 2HI(g). Calculate Kc.

Kc = [HI]² / ([H₂][I₂])  (1 mark)

= (12.0)² / (2.0 × 2.0) = 144 / 4.0  (1 mark)

Kc = 36, no units (the powers cancel)  (1 mark)

Q33 marks

For N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g) at equilibrium the mole fractions are N₂ 0.20, H₂ 0.60, NH₃ 0.20, at a total pressure of 100 atm. Calculate Kp.

Partial pressures: p(N₂)=20, p(H₂)=60, p(NH₃)=20 atm  (1 mark)

Kp = p(NH₃)² / (p(N₂) × p(H₂)³)

= (20)² / (20 × 60³) = 400 / (20 × 216000)  (1 mark)

Kp = 9.26 × 10⁻⁵ atm⁻²  (1 mark)

Q44 marks

The Haber forward reaction is exothermic. Explain the effect on the yield of ammonia and on the value of Kp of (a) increasing the total pressure and (b) increasing the temperature.

(a) Increasing pressure: the position of equilibrium shifts to the side with fewer gas moles (2 moles NH₃ rather than 4 moles of reactants), so the yield of ammonia increases. The value of Kp is unchanged, because pressure does not change K.  (2 marks)

(b) Increasing temperature: equilibrium shifts in the endothermic (reverse) direction, so the yield of ammonia decreases, and because temperature is the only factor that changes K, the value of Kp decreases.  (2 marks)

Q52 marks

State and explain the effect of adding an iron catalyst on the value of Kp and the time taken to reach equilibrium.

A catalyst has no effect on the value of Kp and no effect on the position of equilibrium  (1 mark), because it increases the rate of the forward and reverse reactions equally; it only allows equilibrium to be reached more quickly  (1 mark).
