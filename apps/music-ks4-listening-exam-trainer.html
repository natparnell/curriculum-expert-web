How to listen (exam technique)
  • Intervals: sing the lower note, then jump to the upper. A 5th feels open (the start of "Twinkle Twinkle"); an octave is the same note higher.
  • Tonality: major sounds bright and settled, minor sounds darker or sadder. Listen to whether the 3rd of the chord is high (major) or lowered (minor).
  • Cadences: hear the last two chords. Perfect (V–I) sounds finished; imperfect (ends on V) sounds unfinished; plagal (IV–I) is the "Amen"; interrupted (V–vi) surprises you.
  • Texture: count the independent lines. One line = monophonic; tune plus chords = homophonic; interweaving equal lines = polyphonic.
  • Devices: a sequence repeats a shape higher or lower; an ostinato repeats unchanged; a pedal holds one bass note while harmony changes above.
