What am I learning?

A clause tells you who did what (the main clause). An adverbial adds extra detail: when, where or how. You can put an adverbial at the end, or move it to the front. A fronted adverbial always takes a comma after it. Try saying each version aloud: the front version makes the listener wait, then surprises them with the action.
