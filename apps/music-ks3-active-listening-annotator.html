🎧 Active Listening Annotator

KS3 Music · Listening, analysing & appraising

Teacher aim: pupils listen critically and tag the moment a musical change happens, naming it with correct vocabulary from the elements of music (tempo, dynamics, instrumentation). The app marks each tag for timing and type, then builds analytical listening through targeted feedback.

1. Choose a listening extract

Three short fictional study pieces. Each has a hidden "answer map" of real musical changes for you to discover by ear.

How analytical listening is marked

A marker counts as correct when it lands within about 1 second of a real change and you named the right element. "Close" means the timing or type is slightly off, which is your cue to listen again.
Keyboard and accessibility notes

Focus the waveform, then use ← / → to scrub the cursor (hold for larger jumps with Shift), Space to play or pause, and Enter to drop a marker of the currently selected tag. Switch the tag type with the buttons above. All feedback is announced to screen readers.

3. Genre Decoder (extension)

Use the musical evidence you have just heard. Which genre best fits this extract? Choose one and the app explains the reasoning.
