Teacher notes and exam links (AQA / OCR / Edexcel A-level)

This demo models ligand substitution and the link between ligand field strength, d-orbital splitting (Δoct) and the complementary colour absorbed light produces. It is qualitative, intended for board work and quick prediction practice, not for exact wavelength calculation.

LigandDenticityChargeField strength (spectrochemical series)

Key points to draw out: water and ammonia give 6-coordinate octahedral complexes; chloride is larger so it commonly gives 4-coordinate tetrahedral; bidentate ligands such as ethane-1,2-diamine (en) occupy two sites each. Stronger-field ligands increase Δoct, so the complex absorbs higher-energy (shorter wavelength) light and the transmitted colour shifts. The classic demo: pale blue [Cu(H2O)6]2+ turns deep blue [Cu(NH3)4(H2O)2]2+ on adding ammonia.

Demonstration tool for classroom modelling. Colours are illustrative of the qualitative trend, not calibrated spectra.
