🔍 Build a Classification Key

Teaching aim: Pupils sort living things into groups by dragging yes or no questions to build a working dichotomous key, then test it live on a mystery animal. Covers classification, grouping by observable features, and animal life cycles (KS2 Living things and their habitats).

📦 Question cards

Drag a question onto a green drop zone, or select a card and press Enter, then choose a zone. Each yes or no question splits a group in two.

🐾 Organisms to sort

These eight animals all start together. A good key separates them one at a time.

🌳 Your key

Build downwards. A zone holding one animal is finished (gold). Keep adding questions until every animal has its own ending.

Drag your first question to the top zone to begin. Tip: think about what splits the most animals at once.
How a dichotomous key works (teacher note)
  • A dichotomous key splits a group with questions that have exactly two answers: yes or no.
  • Each split should sort by an observable feature (legs, wings, scales, where it lives).
  • The key is finished when every organism is alone at the end of a branch.
  • The "Life cycle" badge shows the matching life-cycle pattern (mammal, bird, insect, amphibian) for the named animal.
