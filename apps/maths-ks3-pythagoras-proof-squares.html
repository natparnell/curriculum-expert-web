Pythagoras Proof Squares

Teacher aim: pupils see, by rearranging area, why the square on the hypotenuse equals the sum of the squares on the two shorter sides: a² + b² = c².

KS3 Maths · Geometry and measures · Pythagoras

1. Build the right triangle

Change the two shorter sides. Watch the three squares grow. The grid shows that the small squares' area added together fills the large square exactly.

2. Rearrange the proof

Drag the two small squares' pieces (a² and b²) into the empty hypotenuse square (c²). When every piece lands, the large square is filled with no gaps and no overlaps. That is the proof.

  1. The a² pieces are blue; the b² pieces are green.
  2. Together their total area equals the dashed c² square.
  3. No overlaps means a² + b² really does equal c².
