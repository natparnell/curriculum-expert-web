Teacher notes & the science

What is the motor effect? When a current flows through a wire that sits in a magnetic field, the wire experiences a force. The wire's own magnetic field interacts with the magnet's field, pushing the wire.

  • Direction: use Fleming's left-hand rule. Field, Current and Force (motion) are all at right angles.
  • Size: the force grows if you increase the current, the field strength, or the length of wire in the field. As an equation: F = B I L (force = magnetic flux density × current × length).
  • Reverse it: swap the current direction OR swap the poles and the force flips. Swap both and it goes back to how it was.
  • Link to motors: a coil in a field feels a turning force on each side, so it spins. A split-ring commutator keeps it turning the same way. This is the basis of every electric motor.
  • Stretch (transformers): the opposite idea, moving a magnet near a coil to generate a current, is electromagnetic induction, the principle behind generators and transformers.
