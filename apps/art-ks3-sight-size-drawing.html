Sight-Size Drawing: Grid, Plumb and Angle

Measuring tools for observational drawing · Art and Design · KS3

Teacher aim: Pupils learn to record from observation by checking proportion, vertical alignment and angle against a fixed still life, using sight-size measuring methods rather than guessing.

The still life

Plumb line Angle stick Grid

How sight-size works: keep your eye, the measuring tool and the object in line. The grid checks proportion, the plumb line checks what sits directly above what, and the angle stick reads how steeply an edge tilts. Match what you see, then draw it the same.

Keyboard and touch controls

Tap a tool, then drag its round handles. On a keyboard, press Tab to reach a handle, then the ← → ↑ ↓ arrow keys to move it. Hold Shift for larger steps.

Sight-size and comparative measurement are core observational drawing skills in KS3 Art and Design (drawing and recording from observation). The still life shown is an illustration, not a photograph of real work.
