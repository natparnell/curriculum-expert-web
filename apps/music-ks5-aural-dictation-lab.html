Melodic dictation

Listen to a short tonal melody, then click the staff to notate each note. Rhythm runs left to right; pitch top to bottom. Then Check note by note.

Press New melody then Play to begin.

How to use the staff
  • Each column is one beat. Click the column at the height of the pitch you heard; a notehead appears.
  • Click the same cell again to clear it. Use the keyboard: arrow keys move the cursor, Enter places or clears a note.
  • With rhythm on, the melody may hold notes across beats. Place a note only on the beat where it starts, and leave continuation beats empty.
  • Check answer compares each beat: green for a correct pitch start, red for a wrong or missing one.

TipTap the steady pulse first. Decide how many sounds happen on each beat: one (crotchet), two (paired quavers), or a held value across beats.
Reading the values
  • ♩ minim = 2 beats · ♪ crotchet = 1 beat · ♫ paired quavers = half a beat each.
  • A dotted crotchet plus a quaver fills two beats; a crotchet rest is silence for one beat.
  • Each slot is one beat. A minim fills two slots (the second shows as a tie continuation).

How this maps to the AQA appraising paper
  • Section A asks you to respond to unfamiliar listening: melody, rhythm, harmony, tonality, texture, structure and instrumentation.
  • Accurate dictation underpins the short notation questions; interval and cadence recognition supports the harmony questions.
  • Naming textures and devices feeds the longer description and comparison answers.
