Key ideas

Averages: mean, median and mode

The mean adds every value and divides by how many there are. The median is the middle value once the data is in order (the average of the two middle values if there is an even count). The mode is the value that appears most often, and is the only average that works for categories such as travel method.

Range: a measure of spread

The range is the largest value minus the smallest. It tells you how spread out the data is. A small range means the values are close together; a large range means they are more varied. Two datasets can share the same mean but have very different ranges.

Choosing the right chart

Use a bar chart or pie chart for categorical data such as travel method. A pie chart shows each category as a share of the whole. Use a line graph for a time series, where the horizontal axis is time. Use a scatter graph for paired numerical data to look for correlation.

What is correlation?

A scatter graph plots paired numerical data, one pair as one point. We look at the overall pattern. Positive correlation means that as one variable increases the other tends to increase too (points rise from left to right). Negative correlation means one decreases as the other increases (points fall from left to right). If the points show no pattern there is no correlation. Correlation does not prove that one thing causes the other.

Check your understanding

Question 1
A class records its favourite sport: football, netball, rugby, hockey. Which chart and which average are most appropriate?
Bar chart or pie chart, and the mode. The data is categorical, so it has no numerical mean or median. A pie chart is useful if you want to show each sport as a share of the whole class.
Question 2
Nine houses sell for: £180k, £185k, £190k, £190k, £195k, £200k, £205k, £210k, £2,000k. Which average best represents a typical house, and why?
The median (£195k). The one very expensive house (£2,000k) is an outlier that drags the mean upwards, so the mean would overstate a typical price. The median is resistant to outliers.
Question 3
The daily maximum temperature is recorded for each of seven days. Which chart type best displays this, and why?
A line graph. The data is a time series (measured over consecutive days), so a line graph shows the trend over time and lets you read between the points.
Question 4
Set X has mean 50 and range 4. Set Y has mean 50 and range 30. What does this tell you about the two sets?
They have the same average but very different spread. Set X is consistent and tightly clustered around 50; Set Y is much more varied. The mean alone does not describe a distribution; you also need a measure of spread such as the range.
Question 5
A scatter graph of revision hours against test score shows points rising from left to right. Describe the correlation and what it suggests.
Positive correlation. As revision hours increase, test scores tend to increase. This suggests a relationship, but correlation does not prove that more revision causes higher scores; other factors could be involved.
