    Signal path and production reference

    The recording signal path: sound source → microphone → preamp (sets gain) → converter (analogue to digital) → channel in the DAW. Each channel here is a recorded stem: a bounced track of one part.

    In a channel strip, order matters:

    • Gain / fader: sets relative loudness. Balance is the single biggest factor in a good mix.
    • EQ: shapes tone. Cut to remove problems and make room; boost gently to add character. Low-frequency build-up between bass and kick is the classic clash.
    • Pan: places a source left to right, widening the stereo image. Lead vocal and bass usually stay centred.
    • Reverb send: adds space and depth. Too much washes detail; dry vocals sit forward, wet vocals sit back.

    Gain staging: keep the master peak below 0 dBFS. If it clips, the converter distorts. Pull faders down, do not just turn the master up.
