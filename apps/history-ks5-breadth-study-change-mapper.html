WeST · KS5 History · Component 1 Breadth Study

Breadth Study Change Mapper

Teacher aim: students analyse the pace and pattern of change across c.130 years by plotting political, economic, social and religious threads on parallel lanes, then test their own periodisation by sliding turning-point markers and justifying each break.

Worked breadth topic: England 1485 to 1603, the Tudor century (Component 1 style: change and continuity over c.100+ years).

1 Plot the four strands

Each card below is a key development. Drag it onto the matching lane on the map, or select it and press Enter to auto-place it on its correct lane. Horizontal position is fixed by date; you choose nothing about timing. The thinking is in reading the four strands against one another.

Keyboard: Tab to a card, press Enter to place it. On the map, Tab to a turning-point handle, then use ← / → to slide it (hold Shift for 10-year jumps).

2 The change map

Read across each lane to see how fast each strand moved. Read down each year-column to see when several strands changed at once: those clusters are candidate turning points. Drag the two amber markers to where you think the century genuinely breaks.

Political Economic Social Religious Turning-point marker

3 Test your periodisation

Sliding the markers splits the century into three sub-periods. A strong periodisation puts the breaks where the most strands change at once and where the change is most decisive. State where you have put each marker and why, then check it.
