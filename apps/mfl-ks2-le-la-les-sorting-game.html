Le, La, Les Sorting Game

Modern Foreign Languages Key Stage 2 French gender & articles

Teaching aim: pupils learn that every French noun is masculine (le), feminine (la) or plural (les). They drag each noun into the right basket, use a hint to spot the ending pattern, and try a short verb step. Difficulty grows as the class succeeds.

Teacher notes and the grammar behind the game
  • le goes before masculine singular nouns (le chat, le chien, le livre).
  • la goes before feminine singular nouns (la table, la maison, la pomme).
  • les goes before all plural nouns, masculine or feminine (les chats, les pommes).
  • Many feminine nouns end in -e (la table, la chaise), but this is a helpful clue, not a hard rule. The game teaches noticing patterns, then knowing exceptions (le livre is masculine even though it ends in -e).
  • Level 3 adds a gentle verb step using j'aime (I like): "J'aime le chien" means "I like the dog". This introduces high-frequency verb use alongside the article.
  • Difficulty scales automatically: a strong streak unlocks plurals and the verb step; mistakes ease it back so no pupil is left behind.
