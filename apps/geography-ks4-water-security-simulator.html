💧 Water Security Simulator

Manage the water supply of Vellmoor, a fictional region, and keep it secure as demand rises and drought bites.

Teacher aim: Pupils model the balance between water supply and demand, then test management strategies (transfer schemes, reservoirs, desalination, demand reduction) and watch the water scarcity index and the conflict risk index respond. They learn why water insecurity grows and how it can be managed.

Key terms (tap to reveal)
Water security
Reliable access to enough safe water for health, livelihoods and the economy.
Water insecurity
When demand is greater than reliable supply, so shortages and rationing occur.
Physical scarcity
Not enough water in the environment (low rainfall, drought).
Economic scarcity
Water exists but the money or infrastructure to deliver it is lacking.
Water transfer scheme
Moving water from a surplus region to a deficit region by canal or pipeline.
Over-abstraction
Taking groundwater faster than it is replaced, lowering the water table.
