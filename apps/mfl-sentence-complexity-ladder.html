About the Complexity Score

This tool helps you practise uplevelling: starting from a simple sentence and adding structures to widen your range. The score on the right measures only the range of structures you have used.

It is not a GCSE grade. At GCSE, the writing marks reward range and accuracy together. Examiners look at correct verb forms, tenses, word order, gender and adjective agreement, and spelling (including accents). Stacking on extra clauses does not raise a grade if the language is inaccurate. A shorter, accurate sentence often scores better than a long one full of mistakes.

How to use the score well

  • Use it to check you are varying your structures, not just repeating simple sentences.
  • Then re-read your sentence and check every word is accurate before you would use it in real writing.
  • Click Listen to hear the sentence, and notice the accents: they change pronunciation and are marked at GCSE.

Structures you can practise

  • Adjective: adds detail to a noun. Watch placement and agreement (French and Spanish adjectives usually follow the noun and agree; German adjectives go before the noun and take an ending).
  • Adverb: adds nuance to a verb. Position matters.
  • Connective: links ideas and extends the sentence.
  • Opinion phrase: gives a personal response. In German, openers ending in dass send the verb to the end of the clause.
  • Subordinate clause: shows grammatical control (e.g. weil, parce que, porque).
  • Relative clause: embeds extra information about a noun.
