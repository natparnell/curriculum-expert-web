Evidence Summary Explorer

CPD & Staff Tools · Professional Behaviours and Growth

Teacher aim: engage with the educational research and evidence base. Browse plain-English summaries of EEF strands and cognitive science, weigh up their confidence ratings and classroom implications, then test your judgement on real teaching scenarios.

Part one

Browse the evidence base

Each card is a plain-English summary of a strand from the Education Endowment Foundation toolkit or from cognitive science. Filter, search, then open a card to see what it means for your classroom and how confident the evidence is. Confidence is about the strength of the research, not a promise of impact in every room.

No summaries match those filters. Try widening your search.

Part two

Interrogate the evidence: scenario check

Reading summaries is the start. The real skill is choosing the right evidence-informed response in the moment. Read each classroom scenario and pick the option best supported by the evidence base above. You will get specific feedback explaining why, and which summary it links to.

How it works: there is one best-supported answer per scenario. After you choose, the app explains the reasoning and points you back to the relevant evidence card. Use the keyboard or tap to choose.
