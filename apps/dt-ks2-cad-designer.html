What is CAD, and why do designers use it?

CAD stands for computer-aided design. It means using a computer to draw and plan a product before it is made. Designers, engineers and architects use CAD every day to design everything from trainers and toys to bridges and buildings.

On paper, changing an idea means rubbing out and starting again. With CAD, you can move, resize, recolour, copy and undo in seconds. The computer keeps every line neat and snaps shapes to a grid, so the design stays tidy and easy to read.
