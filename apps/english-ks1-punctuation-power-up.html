Punctuation Power-Up ⚡

Teacher aim: Pupils choose the right end mark for a sentence, then hear how a full stop, question mark or exclamation mark changes whether it is a statement, a question or a command. Read each sentence aloud together first.

Drag the power mark into the box

Where does the sentence end? Drag a . ? or ! into the empty box, or pick one with the keyboard.

Sentence 1 of 6

Keyboard: press Tab to a mark, then Enter or Space to drop it in the box.

👋 Ready when you are

Read the sentence aloud. Does your voice go down (telling), up (asking) or get bigger (command)? Then choose the mark.

Your three power marks

A reminder card you can leave on screen.

.

Full stop

Ends a statement. We are just telling something. Our voice falls at the end.

?

Question mark

Ends a question. We are asking something. Our voice rises at the end.

!

Exclamation mark

Shows a command or strong feeling. Our voice gets bigger and louder.

Bonus: Join the clauses 🔗

Use a joining word (a conjunction) to make one longer sentence. Tap a word and listen to how the meaning changes.

Tom packed his bag … he found his boots.

Pick and, but or because to join the two ideas.
