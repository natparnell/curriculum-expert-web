Art & Design · KS4 · AO2 Refinement

Experiment Lab

Take one motif. Run side-by-side media trials. Read the feedback, then tag which experiments to take forward and which to park. This is the AO2 decision: refining ideas by reviewing and exploring media, materials and techniques.

Teacher aim: Pupils evidence AO2 by experimenting with at least four media trials on a single motif and justifying which to develop, linking each decision to their stated intention.

1

Set your motif and intention

Choose one subject to keep constant across every trial, then pick the quality you are reaching for. The lab judges each experiment against this intention, so a "messy" mark can be the right answer.

You want to capture surface texture. Media that build a tactile, broken or layered mark will score well; flat, even media will be marked down for this intention.

2

Run a media trial

Pick a medium and adjust how you handle it, then sketch on the motif with a mouse, finger or stylus. Use the keyboard buttons if you prefer not to draw. The lab gives targeted, technique-specific feedback against your intention.

4

Justify your refinement

In a sentence or two, explain which trial you are developing and why, in the language of an exam annotation. Then get the lab's verdict on your AO2 decision-making.
