Pentatonic Improviser 🎹

Teaching aim: pupils improvise and compose short melodies for a purpose, using the dimensions of music (pitch, dynamics, tempo, duration). The five pads use a pentatonic scale, so any combination sounds good over the groove. That confidence frees pupils to listen, experiment and shape their ideas.

🥁 The backing groove

Start the groove, choose how it feels, then improvise on the pads below.

Tempo (speed)
96 bpm

Tip: tempo is one of the dimensions of music. A slower tempo can feel calmer; a faster tempo can feel more excited.

🎵 Improvise on the pentatonic pads

Tap, click or use the keyboard keys A S D F G. Every choice fits the groove, so play freely and listen.

Press Start groove, then play a pad. I will give you a tip after each idea.

Idea: try a short pattern, leave a gap (a rest), then answer it. Question and answer is a great way to compose.

🤔 Listen and improve

Composers always go back and improve. Use these prompts with a partner (for example Priya and Marcus).

  • Did your melody have a clear shape? Many tunes step up then settle back down.
  • Where could a rest give the listener a moment to breathe?
  • Try the same melody at a different tempo or dynamic. How does the feeling change?
  • Can you end on the home note (pad 1) so it sounds finished?
