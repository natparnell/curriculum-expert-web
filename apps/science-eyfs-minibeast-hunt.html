🐛 Minibeast Hunt

Teacher aim: children explore the natural world by finding living things in the garden, then sort each minibeast by what its body has or where it lives (legs, wings, lives underground). Supports the EYFS Understanding the World: The Natural World goal.

Step 1: Hunt for minibeasts

Tap a leaf, a flower or the soil to see if a little creature is hiding there. Find all 8 minibeasts! Use the Read aloud button if you want to hear the words.

What did we learn?

Minibeasts are small living things, also called invertebrates because they have no bones inside. Scientists look closely and sort living things by their features, the parts of the body you can see.

  • Legs: a ladybird and an ant have six legs; a spider has eight legs; a woodlouse and a centipede have many more.
  • Wings: a butterfly, a bee and a ladybird can open wings and fly.
  • Underground: worms and many minibeasts live in the soil, which also helps plants grow.

Living things need care: just like a plant needs water, light and warmth to grow, minibeasts need a safe home in the garden. We look gently and put them back where we found them.
