    A method for any unseen poem

    You will never have studied the poem before. That is the point. Work through these five steps in order; you do not need to "get it" on the first read.

    AQA-style unseen questions

    Section C of Paper 2. Question 1 analyses a single poem; Question 2 compares its methods with a second unseen poem. Read each question, plan, then reveal the examiner focus.

    Question 1 · single poem · AO1 / AO2

    [24 marks]

    What the examiner is looking for

    • A clear line of argument about meaning, sustained across the answer (AO1).
    • Precise references to methods: language, imagery, form, structure, sound (AO2).
    • The effect of each method, linked back to the poem's overall message.
    • Short, embedded quotations rather than long copied lines.
    • Tentative, exploratory phrasing: "perhaps", "this might suggest", "the reader is invited to…".
    Question 2 · comparison · AO2

    [8 marks]

    What the examiner is looking for

    • Comparison of methods only; this question does not ask about feelings or ideas in depth.
    • Comparative connectives running through: "similarly", "by contrast", "whereas".
    • Both poems treated together, not one then the other in two separate halves.
    • The effect of each shared or contrasting method, briefly and precisely.
    • This is worth fewer marks: be selective, pick two or three strong points of comparison.
