Pathways Planner

KS4 Economic wellbeing: explore post-16 routes, careers and earnings.

Teacher aim: Pupils investigate how A-levels, T-levels, apprenticeships and going straight into work each open different careers, earnings and trade-offs, then justify a route that fits a person's strengths.

Step 1

Choose a Year 11 person to plan for

Each person has different GCSE results, interests and money worries. Pick one, then plan their next step.

Step 2

Drag your person onto a post-16 route

Move the token into one of the four routes. The planner reacts straight away with how well it fits, the careers it opens and likely earnings.

Quick facts to check your thinking (England, illustrative)
  • An apprentice earns a real wage from day one, but A-levels keep more doors to university open.
  • T-levels are large technical courses worth up to three A-levels and include a long work placement.
  • Higher earnings later often follow more qualifications, but that is not guaranteed and skilled trades can pay very well.
  • The "right" route depends on the person: their grades, what they enjoy, money pressures and how they like to learn.
  • Figures here are illustrative demonstration data for classroom discussion, not financial advice.
