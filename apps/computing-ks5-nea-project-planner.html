NEA Project Planner

A guided workbench for the OCR H446 programming project (the non-exam assessment, worth 20% of the A-level). Work through the four assessed stages: Analysis, Design, Development with iterative testing, and Evaluation. Capture your problem and stakeholder, build measurable success criteria, decompose the system into modules, plan your tests, and self-evaluate against every criterion, all tracked by a live completeness checklist.

All worked examples shown are illustrative only, to demonstrate the expected standard. Replace them with your own project. Nothing is saved or transmitted; refreshing the page clears your work.

Analysis Stage 1

Identify a genuine, non-trivial problem with a real stakeholder, research it, and define measurable success criteria that your finished solution can be tested against.

    No success criteria yet. Add specific, testable statements. These drive your Testing and Evaluation marks.

    Design Stage 2

    Decompose the problem into features and modules, then describe the algorithms, data structures, interface and test data. Top-down decomposition shows the moderator you have planned a maintainable solution.

    No features yet. Break the system into 3 to 6 features, then add the modules (sub-tasks) under each.

    Development & Testing Stage 3

    Develop iteratively in prototypes, justify your decisions, validate inputs, and test as you go. Build a test plan that includes normal, boundary and erroneous data, and tests that map back to your success criteria.

    Test plan

    No tests yet. Aim for a spread of normal, boundary and erroneous data, and reference the success criterion each test proves.

    Evaluation Stage 4

    Evaluate the finished solution against each success criterion in turn, judging whether it was met, partly met or not met, with evidence. Then reflect on limitations, future improvements and stakeholder feedback.

    Add success criteria on the Analysis tab first. Each one will appear here for you to evaluate.
