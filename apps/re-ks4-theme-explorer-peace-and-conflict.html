Theme Explorer: Peace and Conflict

GCSE Religious Studies • Component 2: Thematic Studies

Teacher aim: Pupils work a conflict scenario through three religious responses (just war, pacifism, forgiveness), justifying each with scriptural and non-religious arguments, then evaluate which response is strongest. Targets AO1 (knowledge) and AO2 (evaluation).

1. Choose a conflict scenario

Each scenario raises a real ethical question about how religious believers might respond to violence and wrongdoing. Pick one to explore.

2. Build the three religious responses

Drag each argument card from the bank onto the position it best supports. Scriptural sources and non-religious (secular) views both belong somewhere. Some cards genuinely fit more than one position; others have a single best home. You decide, then check your reasoning.

Scriptural source Non-religious view Just war Pacifism Forgiveness
Keyboard and tablet: tap or press Enter/Space on a card to pick it up, then tap or Enter on a position to drop it. Touch drag also works. Press Esc to cancel a pickup.

⚖️ Just War

Violence can be morally justified if strict conditions are met (just cause, last resort, proportionate force, legitimate authority). Associated with Christianity (Aquinas) and Islam (controlled struggle).

Drop supporting arguments here

🕊️ Pacifism

All violence is wrong; conflict must be resolved peacefully. Held by Quakers and many who follow Jesus's teaching of non-retaliation, alongside non-religious peace movements.

Drop supporting arguments here

🤝 Forgiveness and Reconciliation

Wrongdoing should be met with mercy and a chance to rebuild relationships rather than revenge. Central to Christianity and a major aim of justice in several faiths.
