KS4 Physical Education · Health, fitness and well-being

Energy Balance Simulator

Model how diet and activity decide whether energy goes in surplus, deficit or balance, and project the long-term effect on body mass and health.

Teacher aim: Pupils manipulate daily energy intake, food type and activity level to explain energy balance (intake versus expenditure), read a macronutrient breakdown, and link a sedentary lifestyle to long-term health consequences.

1. Set the daily inputs

Drag a slider or use the arrow keys. Pick a starter profile, then change one thing at a time.

2400 kcal

How much food energy is eaten in a day. A typical teenage guideline is roughly 2000 to 2800 kcal.

Diet type (sets macronutrient split)

Diet type changes the proportion of carbohydrate, protein and fat, not the total calories.

Daily activity level

Sets the activity multiplier applied to resting metabolism to give total energy expenditure.

30 min

Deliberate training on top of everyday activity (running, training, a match). Burns roughly 9 kcal per minute here.

Key terms (tap to open)
Energy balance
Energy intake compared with energy expenditure over time.
Energy surplus
Eating more than you use. The excess is stored, mostly as fat, so mass rises.
Energy deficit
Using more than you eat. Stored energy is used, so mass falls.
Macronutrients
Carbohydrate, protein and fat: the three nutrients that supply energy.
Sedentary lifestyle
Very little physical activity, low daily energy expenditure.
