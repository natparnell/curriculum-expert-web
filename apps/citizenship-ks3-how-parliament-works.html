How Parliament Works

Teacher aim: pupils move a real-style Bill through the Houses of Parliament, the House of Commons, the House of Lords and Royal Assent, making the choices that decide whether it becomes law. Covers UK government, Parliament and the democratic system.

The road to Royal Assent

Follow the lit chamber. A Bill must pass through both Houses and then receive Royal Assent from the monarch.

    The Bill on the table
    The School Streets (Safer Journeys) Bill
    A proposal to close roads outside schools to traffic at drop-off and pick-up times. It starts life in the House of Commons.
    Who holds power? (quick reminder)

    The monarch

    Head of state. Gives Royal Assent but by convention never refuses it. A ceremonial, not a political, role.

    The Prime Minister

    Head of government. Usually leads the largest party in the Commons and chooses the Cabinet.

    The Cabinet

    Senior ministers who run government departments and set policy.

    MPs and peers

    MPs are elected; peers are appointed. Both debate, amend and vote on Bills.
