Bird's Eye School 🦅

Teacher aim: KS1 pupils flip between a ground photo and an aerial view, learn N, S, E and W, then build a plan of the school using picture symbols and a key.

1. Flip the view

This is Brambledown School. From the ground you see the front of the building. A bird flying above sees the roofs and paths from straight up. Tap the picture to flip it over.

2. Which way? North, south, east, west

North is at the top of the map. South is at the bottom. East is to the right. West is to the left. Look at the bird's eye map, then tap the compass direction that answers the question.

Tip: N is always at the top of the map.

3. Make a plan of the school

A plan shows where things are from above. Each picture symbol means one real thing. Drag a symbol from the key onto the matching square, or tap a symbol then tap a square. Use the labels to help you put each one in the right place.
