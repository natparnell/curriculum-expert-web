What the criteria actually mean
  • Scope: narrow enough to argue in 2500 to 3000 words. A whole-novel "themes" title is too broad.
  • Comparability: the two texts (or two data sets) must genuinely illuminate each other, not just sit side by side.
  • Feasibility: you can find the evidence, in the time and word count you have.
  • Critical edge: the title invites argument and analysis, not retelling or description.
  • Ethics and data: any real-world language data must be collected and stored responsibly.
