KS4 Drama · GCSE Component 1

Devising Log Builder

Teacher aim: students draft a structured devising log across four phases (stimulus, intentions, rehearsal, evaluation), getting live feedback that maps their writing to AO1, AO2 and AO4 before exporting a marked-up draft.

1. Responding to the stimulus AO1

Examiners reward a personal, analytical response to the stimulus, not a plot retell. Pick the stimulus type, name your initial ideas, then explain how you interpreted it.

0 words

Live feedback

  • Choose a stimulus type and start writing to see AO1 feedback.

2. Aims and intentions AO1 AO2

State the intended impact on your audience and the genre or practitioner style you chose. Strong logs link every decision to a purpose.

0 words

Live feedback

  • Name a style and your intended impact to begin.

3. Rehearsal and development AO2

AO2 is about the process: applying drama skills and techniques to develop ideas. Log a specific decision, the technique used, and how you refined it. Avoid "we practised a lot".

0 words

Live feedback

  • Tag a technique and describe a specific rehearsal decision.

4. Analysis and evaluation AO4

AO4 rewards a balanced, evaluative judgement: what worked, what did not, and what you would change. Use evaluative connectives and refer to your original intention.

0 words

Live feedback

  • Drop in an evaluative connective, then weigh a strength against a weakness.
