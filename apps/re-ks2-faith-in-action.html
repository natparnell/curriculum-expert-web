Faith in Action 🤝

Teacher aim: Pupils explore how religious and non-religious beliefs inspire people to act with justice and kindness. They match real kinds of charitable action to the value and tradition behind them (for example zakah, tzedakah, seva, ahimsa), then build a class "Kindness in the World" board.

A new act of kindness appears below. Read who is acting and what they do, then choose the value or duty that inspires it. The board fills up as you match correctly. Use a mouse, touch, or the keyboard (Tab to move, Enter or Space to choose).

Word bank for pupils and teachers
  • Zakah (Islam): giving a set share of your wealth each year to those in need. One of the Five Pillars.
  • Tzedakah (Judaism): giving to those in need as an act of justice, not just kindness.
  • Seva (Sikhism and Hinduism): selfless service to others, expecting nothing back.
  • Ahimsa (Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism): not harming any living thing.
  • Sadaqah (Islam): any voluntary act of kindness or giving, big or small.
  • Agape (Christianity): unconditional love that leads to caring for your neighbour.
  • Dana (Buddhism): generous giving that frees you from selfishness.
  • Humanist care: helping others because we share one human life, with no belief in a god.
