Intervention Effect-Size Calculator

Did the intervention genuinely work, or is the change just noise?

Teacher aim: A tracker can log that an intervention happened and that scores changed. It cannot tell you whether that change is large enough to trust. This tool computes Cohen's d (effect size) and a 95% confidence interval on pre and post data for a small reading group, so pupils can judge whether the gain beats the natural noise in the numbers.

A small effect size with a wide confidence interval that crosses zero means the change could plausibly be nothing: do not claim impact. A large effect size with a tight interval well above zero is the signal you want before scaling an intervention. Confidence-interval width here shrinks with bigger groups and tighter spread; the school maths is simplified for teaching and uses fictional demonstration data.
