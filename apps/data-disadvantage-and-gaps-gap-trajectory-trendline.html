Gap Trajectory Trendline 📈

Teacher aim: read a multi-year disadvantage gap, judge whether it is narrowing or widening, and use the current rate of change to project when parity (a zero gap) would be reached.

The picture across the years

Demonstration data Greenfield Academy (a fictional school). Each point is the percentage of pupils meeting the expected standard. The gap is the difference between non-disadvantaged pupils and disadvantaged pupils. Drag the markers, use the sliders, or use a keyboard, then read the projection.

Show the projection working

The trend per year is the average change in the gap across the years shown (the slope of the best-fit line). If the gap is closing, parity year = latest year + (latest gap ÷ yearly closing rate), rounded up to the next whole year. If the gap is flat or widening, parity is never reached at the current rate.
