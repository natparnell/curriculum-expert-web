How to Read a 4-Figure Grid Reference

A 4-figure grid reference names a square by the two numbered grid lines that cross at its bottom-left corner. It always has four digits.

Step 1: Read ALONG the bottom first to the line on the left of the square. This two-digit number is the easting (e.g. 19).

Step 2: Read UP the side to the line below the square. This two-digit number is the northing (e.g. 44).

Step 3: Put them together with no gap: easting then northing, so 19 and 44 gives 1944.

Remember: "Along the corridor, up the stairs!" Eastings always come before northings.

Measuring Distance on a Map

Maps are drawn to scale. On the Distance tab, each grid square is 1 kilometre across. To measure how far apart two places are, count the squares between them.

For straight up, down, left or right moves, just count the squares. The Distance tab lets you practise this with instant feedback.
