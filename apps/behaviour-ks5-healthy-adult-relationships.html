Healthy Adult Relationships

Work through real-feeling case studies on commitment, conflict and consent in adult relationships. Rank the possible responses from healthiest to most harmful, then test your thinking against expert feedback and a debate prompt.

Teacher aim: Students evaluate responses to dilemmas about adult relationships, ongoing consent, and family or parenting choices, justifying a ranking and debating it as a class.
1 Pick a case 2 Rank the responses 3 Check & debate

1Choose a case study

Four post-16 scenarios. Complete each to track progress. All names are fictional.

2Rank the responses

Drag, or use the up/down buttons, to order these from healthiest (1) at the top to most harmful at the bottom. There is no single "trick" answer: defend your reasoning.

    Keyboard: focus a row's Move up or Move down button and press Enter or Space. Screen reader users hear position changes announced.

    What "healthy" looks like

    • Consent is ongoing: it can be withdrawn at any point, and silence is not a yes.
    • Communication: needs and boundaries are named honestly, not assumed.
    • Respect & equality: neither person controls, pressures or punishes the other.
    • Shared decisions: commitment, finances and parenting are agreed, not imposed.
    • Safety: threats, coercion or isolation are red flags, never normal conflict.
