What Makes Us British?

Build a shared picture of identity in the UK, then test your assumptions against the real census.

Citizenship Key Stage 3 Identity, diversity and living together in the UK
Teacher aim: Pupils explore the many national, regional, religious and ethnic identities that make up the UK, and practise mutual respect by comparing their own assumptions with verified census data.

1. What words say "us"?

Add words that describe who lives in the UK and what we share. Repeat a word to make it grow: the cloud shows which ideas your class values most. Click any word to remove it.

Tip: think about languages, foods, festivals, places, values and beliefs. Try tapping a suggestion below.

Add a few words to see what your cloud reveals about identity.

2. Where does each identity belong?

Drag each card into the type of identity it shows. Many of us hold all four at once: that is what makes the UK diverse. You get instant feedback on every drop.

For each question, move the slider to your best guess for England and Wales. Then reveal the real figure from the 2021 Census. How close were your assumptions?
