💡 What to Look For

Spring brings warmer days, blossom and new leaves. We often see showers and rainbows. The days are getting longer.

Summer is the warmest season with the longest, sunny days. Trees are full of green leaves.

Autumn turns leaves orange and brown, and they fall from the trees. It gets windier and cooler, and the days get shorter.

Winter is the coldest season with the shortest days. Trees are bare and we may see frost or snow.

Look out of your window each day. Watch how the trees, the weather and how light it is in the morning and evening keep changing all year.

❓ Quick Questions

Have a think, then tap to see the answer.

1. Which season has the longest days?

Summer has the longest days, with the most daylight and the warmest weather.

2. In which season do leaves turn orange and fall off the trees?

Autumn. The leaves change colour and fall, and it gets windier and cooler.

3. Which season is the coldest, with the shortest days and bare trees?

Winter. We may also see frost or snow, and it is dark early in the evening.

4. How many seasons are there in one year?

Four seasons: spring, summer, autumn and winter. They happen in that order every year.
