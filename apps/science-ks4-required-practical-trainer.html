1. The investigation brief

Class 10B is measuring how the concentration of hydrochloric acid affects the rate of reaction with marble chips (calcium carbonate). Read it, then run it.

Method

The reaction CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂ gives off carbon dioxide gas. Pupil Priya measures the volume of gas collected in 60 seconds for five different acid concentrations.

Each run uses the same mass of marble chips, the same total volume of acid, and the same temperature. Only the concentration is changed.

Independent what we change   Dependent what we measure   Control what we keep the same

On the bench

  • Conical flask + delivery tube
  • Gas syringe (0 to 100 cm³)
  • Stopwatch
  • Measuring cylinder for the acid
  • Balance for the marble chips
  • 5 g of small marble chips per run

Move to step 2 and collect a full set of results before sorting the variables.

2. Run the practical

Set the acid concentration, then press Start run. The gas syringe fills as carbon dioxide is produced. Collect a reading for all five concentrations.
