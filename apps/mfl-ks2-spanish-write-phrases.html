Accents and special characters

  • The acute accent (á, é, í, ó, ú) shows where to stress a word. It changes meaning: papa means potato but papá means dad.
  • The tilde on ñ makes a "ny" sound, as in niño (boy) and España (Spain). It is a different letter from n.
  • Spanish opens questions and exclamations with an upside-down mark: ¿Cómo estás? and ¡Hola!

Beginning to apply simple grammar

  • Articles agree with the noun. Use un and el for masculine words, una and la for feminine words: un gato, una casa.
  • Adjectives usually come after the noun and agree with it: un gato negro (a black cat), una casa blanca (a white house).
  • Many adjectives change their ending: -o for masculine, -a for feminine, add -s for more than one.
  • tengo means "I have", me llamo means "my name is", me gusta means "I like".

How to do well

  • Say the phrase aloud first, then write it.
  • Check every accent. A missing accent counts as a spelling slip.
  • In "Type it" mode, use the accent helper buttons rather than guessing.
