🌍 Lines on the Globe

Teacher aim: pupils explore the imaginary lines that map the Earth (Equator, Tropics, Prime Meridian) and discover why the time changes as you travel east or west: the Earth turns 15 degrees of longitude every hour.

Spin the Earth and add the lines

Facing you: longitude 0° (the Prime Meridian).

Tip: click the globe then use the left and right arrow keys to spin it.

🕑 Drag the clock around the world

The clock is sitting at longitude 0°. When it is 12 noon on the Prime Meridian (0°), every 15° east adds one hour and every 15° west takes one away.

Lines on the Globe is a teaching demonstration for KS2 geography (Year 3 to Year 6). It uses a simplified model: the Earth turns 360° in 24 hours, so 15° of longitude equals one hour. Real time zones bend around country borders and use daylight saving, so this is a clean classroom model rather than an exact clock.
