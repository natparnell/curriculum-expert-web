KS5 History · AO2 source evaluation

Source Value NOP Workbench

Interrogate a primary source by Nature, Origin and Purpose, then watch its assessed value move as the enquiry question changes. Build a substantiated AO2 judgement.

Teacher aim: Students model A-level provenance interrogation (NOP) and reach purpose-dependent value judgements, recognising that a source is never simply "reliable" or "unreliable": it is valuable for a specific enquiry.

The source
Source A · A despatch sent by the British military governor of a Caribbean garrison to the Colonial Office in London, marked "Confidential".

"I write to assure their Lordships that the recent disturbances among the labouring population have been wholly suppressed, and that order is everywhere restored. The reports reaching the London press of widespread unrest are, I am persuaded, much exaggerated by persons hostile to the present administration of the island. The labourers, left to themselves, are content; agitation is the work of a small and idle faction. I trust this account will set their Lordships' minds at rest, and that no inquiry into my conduct of affairs need be entertained."

— Governor Edmund Thorne to the Colonial Office, 1849 (fictional source, created for teaching)

A confidential official despatch. Read it closely before you tag it: every word of the wording is doing provenance work.

The same provenance that limits a source for one enquiry can make it powerful for another. The decisive question is always: valuable for what?

The meter reading is the same for everyone: it is driven by the source's purpose, not by opinion. Your job is to explain why it sits where it does.
