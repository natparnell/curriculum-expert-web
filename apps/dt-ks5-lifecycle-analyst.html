Design and Technology · KS5 · Sustainability and wider impacts

Lifecycle Analyst

Teacher aim: Students run a cradle-to-grave lifecycle assessment of a product across six stages, then model circular-economy interventions and watch the carbon footprint and wider social, ethical and legal impacts shift. Use it to teach lifecycle analysis, the 6 Rs and the duty of designers to act responsibly.

1. Choose a product to assess

Each product carries different baseline impacts. Pick one, then open the levers below.

2. Lifecycle stages

Carbon per stage in kg CO₂e for one unit. Bars are scaled to the largest baseline stage. Apply interventions on the right to see each stage respond.

A lifecycle assessment (LCA) follows a product from raw material extraction through to end-of-life disposal. The 6 Rs (rethink, refuse, reduce, reuse, repair, recycle) and a circular economy aim to keep materials in use and cut the impact of every stage.

3. Circular-economy interventions

Tick to apply a strategy. Some have a dial. Watch the totals and verdict update live.
