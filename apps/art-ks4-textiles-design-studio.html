Teaching aim: develop a surface-pattern print the way a textile designer does. Build a motif from the formal elements, repeat it into a length of cloth, and explore colourways, so you can show AO2 experimentation with techniques and processes and AO3 recording of your developing ideas.

Glossary: key textile and assessment terms
Motif
A single design unit (a shape or small cluster of shapes) that is repeated to build a pattern.
Repeat
The structure by which a motif is tiled across cloth: full drop, half drop, brick, mirror or rotational.
Colourway
One agreed combination of colours for a design. The same print is often offered in several colourways.
Formal elements
The visual building blocks: colour, pattern, texture, shape and line. In textiles, texture also includes the physical feel of cloth and stitch.
Sampling
Making small trial pieces to test a technique or idea before committing to a final outcome.
Resist
A method (wax in batik, ties in tie-dye) that stops dye reaching parts of the cloth, leaving patterned areas.
AO1 – Develop
Develop ideas through investigations, demonstrating critical understanding of sources.
AO2 – Experiment
Refine work by exploring ideas, selecting and experimenting with media, materials, techniques and processes.
AO3 – Record
Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions as work progresses.
AO4 – Present
Present a personal and meaningful response that realises intentions and, where appropriate, makes connections to visual and other sources.
