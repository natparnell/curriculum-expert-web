Reaction Balancer

Balance equations, prove mass is conserved, and explore acids and energy changes.

Teacher aim: Pupils balance symbol equations by adjusting coefficients, see atom counts update live, then weigh reactants and products to demonstrate conservation of mass.

Balance the symbol equation

Atoms are never created or destroyed in a reaction. Change the big numbers (the coefficients) so each element has the same total on both sides.

Tip: use the plus and minus buttons, or focus one and press the arrow keys. A green tick on every element means the equation is balanced.

Weigh the reaction

In a sealed container, the total mass before a reaction equals the total mass after. Drag a reactant onto the left pan, then watch the products appear on the right with the same mass.

Acid and Alkali Lab

Move the slider to mix acid and alkali. Watch the universal indicator colour and the pH change. The neutral point is pH 7.

pH value 7.0
A neutral solution. Universal indicator turns green.
Acids: pH 0 to 6 Neutral: pH 7 Alkalis: pH 8 to 14

When an acid reacts with an alkali, they neutralise to form a salt and water. The closer to pH 7, the more complete the neutralisation.

Energy in reactions

Some reactions give out energy and warm their surroundings (exothermic). Others take energy in and cool their surroundings (endothermic). Choose one and read the energy profile.
