Making Theatre: Practitioner in Practice

Teaching aim: For Component 2 (Making Theatre) you perform an extract from a published play realised in the style of a chosen theatre practitioner, then justify your choices in a reflective report. This coach lets you pair an extract with a practitioner, see how that practitioner's techniques reshape staging, performance and design, plot the realisation on a stage board, and scaffold the report (intent, technique, audience impact, evaluation). All extracts are illustrative.

How to use this coach · what the assessment rewards

Eduqas Component 2 asks you to take a scripted extract and stage it through the lens of one named practitioner. The skill being assessed is not "doing the play", it is making deliberate, justified artistic decisions that are consistent with the practitioner's methodology and aesthetic.

  • Step 1: Choose one extract from the illustrative bank.
  • Step 2: Choose one practitioner lens. The coach rewrites the extract's staging, performance and design through that lens.
  • Step 3: Plot the realisation on the stage board: place actors, mark proxemics, drop practitioner-specific devices.
  • Step 4: Draft the reflective report using the four-part scaffold. Live word counts and a knitted-together preview help you see the argument forming.

Examiners reward precise use of practitioner terminology (for example gestus, Verfremdungseffekt, given circumstances, the building block), clear cause and effect from technique to intended audience impact, and honest evaluation rather than description.
