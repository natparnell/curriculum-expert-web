🧵 Synthesis Studio

A-level Art and Design – Personal Investigation: turning scattered research into a resolved personal outcome.

Teacher aim: Pupils learn that synthesis is the assessment objective examiners reward most highly: not the amount of research, but how research strands are clustered, threaded and pulled together into one planned, resolved outcome. This studio models that thinking explicitly.
1. ClusterGroup your loose research into research strands.
2. ThreadChoose how strands inform decisions.
3. ResolveState the synthesised outcome.

Step 1 – Cluster the research

Drag each loose research fragment from Mira Acheampong's sketchbook into the strand it belongs to. Strong personal investigations group findings into a few coherent strands rather than leaving research isolated.

Research fragments . Drag or use keyboard to place each fragment into a strand.

Research strands

Keyboard: focus a fragment with Tab, press Enter to pick it up, then ← / → to choose a strand and Enter to drop. Esc cancels.

Step 2 – Pull the threads together

Now decide how each strand will inform a deliberate decision in your outcome. Synthesis means every strand earns its place by changing what you make.
