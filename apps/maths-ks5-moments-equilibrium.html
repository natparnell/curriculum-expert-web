How moments work

The moment of a force about a point is the turning effect it produces:

moment = force × perpendicular distance

For a horizontal rod with vertical forces, the perpendicular distance is just the horizontal gap between the force and the point. Units are newton metres (N m).

A body is in equilibrium when two conditions hold together:

  • Σ clockwise = Σ anticlockwise (no net turning)
  • Σ up = Σ down (no net vertical force)

A weight is W = mg acting downward at the centre of mass. A uniform rod has its weight at the midpoint; a non-uniform rod has it at a stated point. A light rod is treated as massless.

Choosing a clever point to take moments about is the key exam skill: take moments about a support and that reaction has zero distance, so it drops out and the unknown is found in one line. A rod tips about a support exactly when the reaction at the other support falls to zero.
