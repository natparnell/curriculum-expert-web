📐 Design Brief Builder

Teacher aim: Pupils research a real user need, write a design brief and specification, then communicate ideas with annotated sketches, a cross-section and a simple exploded diagram. Covers the KS2 Design programme of study, including first steps in CAD-style on-screen drawing.

🧑‍🌾 Our user this term

Step 1: Research your user

Good designers find out what the user really needs before drawing anything. Tap the research clues that tell you something useful about Mr Okafor's allotment problem. Some clues are just opinions or off topic: leave those out.

How to play Choose the clues that give you real, useful information about the user and the problem. Aim for the four strong clues.

Step 2: Write the design brief

A design brief is one or two clear sentences saying who it is for, what you will make and why. Fill the gaps, then check your brief.

Brief preview Your brief will appear here once you start typing.

Step 3: Build the design specification

A specification is a list of rules your design must follow. Each rule should be measurable or testable, not just "nice". Choose a feature and a measurable rule, then add it. Aim for at least four strong rules covering size, material, safety and the user.

Your specification Add rules to build a clear list. Measurable rules (with a number or a yes/no test) are strongest.

Step 4: Sketch, annotate and explode

Now communicate your idea on screen, just like simple CAD. Drag parts onto the grid to build your tool carrier. Select a part to add a label (annotation). Then flip the exploded view switch to pull the parts apart and show how they fit together.

Pulls the parts apart so you can show each one and how it joins.

Tip: pick a part on the left, then tap the grid to drop it. Drag placed parts to move them. On a keyboard, use the part buttons then the controls below.

Communicating your design Place the base, sides and handle, label at least two parts, then try the exploded view.

Step 5: Review your design work

Designers check their work against the brief and the success criteria. Here is what you have done so far. Green ticks mean it is ready; amber means go back and improve it.
