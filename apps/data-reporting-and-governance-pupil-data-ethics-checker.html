The principles you applied

  • Lawful basis (UK GDPR Art. 6): a school usually relies on public task for core education functions, not consent. Consent is rarely the right basis for compulsory schooling and can be withdrawn.
  • Data minimisation: share only the fields the recipient actually needs for the stated purpose, nothing more.
  • Anonymisation vs pseudonymisation: data is only anonymous if no one can re-identify a pupil, including through small-number "jigsaw" disclosure. Removing names but keeping a code, DOB, or tiny subgroup is still personal data.
  • Safeguarding: safeguarding concerns can override normal sharing limits, but route through the DSL and record the decision.
