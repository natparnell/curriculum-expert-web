Stroke Lab 🏊

PE · Key Stage 2 · Swimming and water safety

Teacher aim: pupils watch correct side-on stroke shapes, diagnose and fix common technique faults, rehearse water safety decisions, and track progress towards the statutory 25 metre outcome.

Side-on stroke animator

Pick a stroke and watch the correct shape from the side, the view a coach uses. Slow it down or step through one frame at a time to study each phase.

Phase: catch

Choose a stroke

Tip: use the Step frame button to freeze a phase, then ask pupils what the arms and legs are doing.

Fault finder: spot it and fix it

Each clip shows a swimmer with one common fault. Read what you can see, choose the fault, then choose the fix. The lab tells you straight away if your diagnosis is right.

    Water safety decisions

    Real swimmers stay safe by thinking before they act. Read each situation and choose the safest response. You will get specific feedback, not just right or wrong.

    Statutory 25 metre tracker

    By the end of KS2 every pupil should swim 25 metres confidently, use a range of strokes, and perform safe self-rescue. Log a fictional pupil's best distance and tick the strokes they can use. Demonstration data only.
