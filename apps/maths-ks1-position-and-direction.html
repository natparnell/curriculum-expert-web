Direction words

These are the turning words we use. A full circle is one whole turn.

Quarter turn (¼)
A small turn, like the corner of a square. Four of them make a whole turn.
Half turn (½)
You end up facing the opposite way. Two quarter turns.
Three-quarter turn (¾)
Three quarter turns. One more would be a whole turn.
Whole turn (1)
All the way round. You face the same way you started.
Clockwise ↻
The way the hands on a clock go round.
Anticlockwise ↺
The other way, against the clock.

Position words

We use these words to say where things are.

First, second, third
Ordinal words tell us the order in a line.
Left and right
Which side something is on.
Forwards and backwards
The way you move ahead or behind.
Above and below
Higher up or lower down.
Next to
Right beside something.
Between
In the middle of two things.

Check what you know

Have a think, then open the answer.

1. Beep faces North. It makes a half turn. Which way does it face now?

Show the answer
South. A half turn means you face the opposite way.

2. How many quarter turns are the same as a whole turn?

Show the answer
Four quarter turns make one whole turn.

3. Beep faces East and turns one quarter turn clockwise. Which way does it face?

Show the answer
South. Clockwise goes North, East, South, West.

4. In a line: dog is first, cat is second, fox is third. Who is between the dog and the fox?

Show the answer
The cat is between the dog and the fox.

5. A three-quarter turn clockwise faces the same way as a quarter turn which way?

Show the answer
A quarter turn anticlockwise. They end up facing the same direction.
