Cumulative Frequency & Box Plots

Teaching aim: Build a cumulative frequency table from grouped data, plot the ogive, read off the median, quartiles and any percentile by dragging a line, then draw and compare box plots from the five-figure summary. All data below is fabricated for teaching.

Cumulative frequency curve

Tip: the read-off line is interactive. Drag it left or right across the curve, or click the graph and use the arrow keys.

How it works

A cumulative frequency (CF) table adds up the frequencies as you go, so each row tells you how many values are less than or equal to the upper boundary of that class.

  • Plot each CF against the upper class boundary, then join the points with a smooth curve (the ogive).
  • The total n is the highest CF. Read the median at n/2, the lower quartile (Q1) at n/4 and the upper quartile (Q3) at 3n/4 on the vertical axis.
  • The interquartile range is IQR = Q3 - Q1. It measures the spread of the middle 50% and ignores extreme values.
  • A box plot shows the five-figure summary: minimum, Q1, median, Q3, maximum. The box spans the IQR; the line inside is the median.

These are estimates from grouped data, so we read values off the curve rather than calculating them exactly.

Compare in context

Move the slider to compare. Look at the two box plots together.

  • Average: compare the medians. A higher median means typically larger values.
  • Spread: compare the IQRs. A smaller IQR means more consistent, less varied values.
  • Always write your comparison in the context of the data, not just the numbers.
