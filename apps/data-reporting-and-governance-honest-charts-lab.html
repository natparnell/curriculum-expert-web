Honest Charts Lab 📊

Teaching aim: Train leaders and governors to recognise and avoid the three classic chart distortions (truncated axes, dual axes, cherry-picked baselines) so that board reporting tells the truth, not just a flattering story.

🎛️ Distortion controls

Toggle a trick on to see how the same true numbers can be made to mislead. Every toggle updates the chart and the verdict instantly.

Scenario. The data team at Maple Vale Academy reports attendance for two terms. The real figures are fixed and honest: Autumn 93.1%, Spring 94.2%. A rise of 1.1 percentage points. Your job is to keep the chart from exaggerating it.
1. Truncated y-axis
Start the axis at 92% instead of 0% so a tiny gap looks like a landslide.
2. Dual / second axis
Overlay a second, unrelated measure (exclusions) on its own hidden scale to imply a cause-and-effect link.
3. Cherry-picked baseline
Swap in the trust's single worst historic term (88.4%) as the comparison so the latest figure looks heroic.

Toggles are keyboard operable (Tab to a switch, Space to flip). Changes are announced for screen readers.
