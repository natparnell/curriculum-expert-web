🧪 KS5 Design & Technology Materials, components & systems

Advanced Material Matrix

Teacher aim: students compare advanced materials and composites across competing properties, then run a weighted selection matrix against a design brief and justify the optimal choice. Targets A-level "objective" decision making: weighting criteria, scoring evidence, and defending a judgement.

Multi-axis property chart

Each material is scored 0–10 on six engineering properties. Toggle materials in the legend to overlay and contrast their profiles. Note the trade-offs: nothing is best on every axis.

Select a material chip to read its profile, or use the chart to compare overlays.

Material library

Click a material to focus its values. These are typical A-level reference values for advanced and modern materials.

Choose a design brief

The brief sets what "good" means. A different brief should change which material wins, because it changes which properties matter most.

Weight the criteria

Distribute importance across the six properties. Weights are normalised automatically, so what matters is the balance between them, not the raw totals.

Suggested weights for this brief are pre-loaded.

Weighted selection matrix

Weighted score = property score (0–10) × normalised weight, summed across all six criteria. The highest total is the matrix-optimal material for this brief. Change the weights on step 2 and watch the ranking move.

Weighted scores of materials against the selected brief.

Justify the optimal choice

Which material do you recommend, and why? Pick one, then write a justification that names the brief, the dominant criteria, the property evidence, and the trade-off you accepted. The app checks your reasoning against the matrix.
