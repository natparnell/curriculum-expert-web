Chromatography and Tests

Teacher aim: Pupils run a paper chromatogram, measure spots to calculate Rf values, then use flame and gas tests to identify four unknown samples. KS4 Chemistry: chemical analysis, the atmosphere and using resources.

1. Run the paper chromatogram

A food technician at Brookfield Academy has a mystery green sweet dye. Lower the paper into the solvent, run the chromatogram, then drag the ruler line to measure each spot. Keep the start (pencil) line above the solvent.

Measurement line: ↑ / ↓ arrow keys move it, or drag it on the canvas. Press M to lock the line onto the nearest feature (solvent front or a spot).

2. Calculate the Rf values

Rf is a ratio with no units. It is always between 0 and 1. Use your measurements from Step 1, or type your own, then calculate.

Rf = distance moved by spot ÷ distance moved by solvent

3. Flame tests for metal ions

Four unknown salt solutions, labelled by fictional pupils, are waiting. Click Flame test to see the colour, then identify the metal ion from the menu. Each card gives instant feedback.

Flame test colours: lithium = crimson red, sodium = yellow, potassium = lilac, calcium = orange-red, copper = green.

4. Gas tests

Choose the correct test for each gas. The card shows the observation; pick the gas it identifies.

Reminders: hydrogen gives a squeaky pop with a lit splint; oxygen relights a glowing splint; carbon dioxide turns limewater cloudy; chlorine bleaches damp litmus paper.

5. The changing atmosphere

Today's air is about 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen and 0.04% carbon dioxide. Build the early atmosphere and watch how oxygen rose and carbon dioxide fell. Then explore one greenhouse gas link to climate change.
