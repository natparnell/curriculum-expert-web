Voices of the Holocaust: Why It Matters

A respectful journey from persecution to liberation. Read the testimony of people who lived through it, weigh the choices ordinary people faced, and decide how we remember.

Teacher aim: Pupils build a timeline of the Holocaust (1933 to 1945), match survivor and witness testimony to its moment, examine the choices of bystanders, resisters and rescuers, and compose a remembrance pledge. Handle with sensitivity. All people named here are fictional, voicing experiences drawn from the documented historical record.

Building the timeline 1933 to 1945

The Holocaust did not happen all at once. It moved through stages: first exclusion, then persecution, then deportation and mass murder, and finally liberation. Open each event to understand how the steps connected.

Historians describe four stages: 1. Exclusion (stripping rights), 2. Persecution (violence and ghettos), 3. Deportation and murder (the camps), 4. Liberation and aftermath. Click an event to read more, then continue.

Remembrance Why it matters

Six million Jewish people were murdered, alongside Roma and Sinti people, disabled people, political prisoners and others. We remember them not as numbers, but as people with names, families and futures. Build your own remembrance pledge from the ideas below.

🕯️ Holocaust Memorial Day is marked in the UK on 27 January, the day Auschwitz-Birkenau was liberated in 1945. Its message: "We remember, so that it can never happen again."
