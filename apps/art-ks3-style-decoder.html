Art and Design · Key Stage 3 · History and contexts of art

Style Decoder

Teacher aim: Pupils read the visual cues in an artwork (colour, line, subject, technique) to place it within an art movement, then learn the period and cultural context behind it. Project on the whiteboard or run on tablets.

1. Choose a work to decode

Pick a sample below, or upload your own image of an artwork to investigate. Samples are original schematic illustrations made for this app, not real paintings.

If you upload your own image, choose which movement you think it belongs to from the cue list (self-check mode).

2. Decode the visual cues

Look hard at the work. Tap each cue you can actually see. The app tells you straight away whether that cue really belongs to this movement.

Choose or upload a work above to begin decoding.

    How to use Style Decoder
    • Tap a cue only if you can genuinely see it in the picture. Guessing every box is not the point.
    • Green means the cue is a real signature of this movement; red means it is a clever distractor that belongs elsewhere.
    • When you have tested the cues, name the movement, then read the fact card for the period and cultural context.
    • Keyboard: use Tab to move between cues and buttons, then Enter or Space to select.
