Paragraph Planner

Learn to write brilliant paragraphs!

Topic Sentence Start with your main idea

The topic sentence tells the reader what the paragraph is about. It comes first!

"The rainforest is home to an amazing variety of animals."

Supporting Details Give evidence and examples

Detail sentences explain, describe or give examples. You usually need 2 or 3 of these.

"Colourful parrots fly between the treetops, while jaguars stalk through the undergrowth."

More Detail Add another point

Each detail should connect back to your topic sentence. Use connectives like "Also", "Furthermore" or "In addition".

"In addition, tiny poison dart frogs hide among the fallen leaves on the forest floor."

Concluding Sentence Wrap it up

The concluding sentence summarises or reflects on the paragraph. It should feel like an ending.

"Clearly, the rainforest is one of the most biodiverse places on Earth."
