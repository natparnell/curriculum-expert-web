Your upstander journey

The upstander toolkit your class has practised

  • Direct: calmly name what is happening as unfair, then move on. ("That is not okay.")
  • Distract: change the subject or start a new activity so the prejudice loses its audience.
  • Delegate: get a trusted adult or someone with more standing to step in. This is not telling tales; it is keeping people safe.
  • Delay: if it is not safe to act in the moment, check on the person afterwards and report it.
  • Document: note what was said or done so it can be reported honestly.

Prejudice is an unfair attitude or belief about a group of people. Discrimination is treating someone worse because they belong to that group. The Equality Act 2010 protects people in England from discrimination based on characteristics such as race, religion, disability, sex and sexual orientation. An upstander acts safely; a bystander stays silent.
