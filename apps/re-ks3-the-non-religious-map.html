For teachers and for checking understanding

This app treats non-religious worldviews as positions in their own right, not just "the absence of religion".

  • Atheism is a position on one question only: it means not believing that any God or gods exist. It says nothing on its own about morality or meaning.
  • Agnosticism is also about the God question: it holds that we cannot know for certain whether a God exists, so it sits in the middle of that axis.
  • Humanism is a fuller worldview. Humanists are usually atheist or agnostic, and they add positive commitments: morality comes from human reason and empathy, this life is the one we have, and meaning is something we create. The British Humanist tradition (Humanists UK) stresses living well without religion.
  • Secular ethics means working out right and wrong using reason, evidence and concern for others, rather than from sacred authority.

Discussion prompt: two people can both be atheists yet disagree about morality. Why does that show atheism is not a complete worldview, while Humanism is?
