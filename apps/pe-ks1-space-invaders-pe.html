Teacher aim: KS1 children learn simple attacking and defending in team games. They drag attackers and defenders on a top-down pitch to discover two big ideas: finding space when attacking and marking when defending. Teacher notes and curriculum link

National Curriculum (KS1 PE): "participate in team games, developing simple tactics for attacking and defending." This app focuses on two foundational tactics: finding space (attacking) and marking an opponent (defending).

  • Challenge 1 (Find the space): An attacker is best in an open area, not crowded by defenders. Ask: "Where can no one reach you quickly?"
  • Challenge 2 (Pass to a free player): Move the receiving attacker into a clear lane so the ball can travel without a defender blocking it.
  • Challenge 3 (Mark the attacker): A defender should stand between the attacker and the goal, close enough to mark. Ask: "Who are you guarding?"
  • Challenge 4 (Defend the goal): Position the defender on the line from the ball to the goal to block the shot.

Use Reveal best move on the whiteboard after children have tried, then have them recreate it on the floor with cones and bibs. Names shown are fictional demonstration characters.
