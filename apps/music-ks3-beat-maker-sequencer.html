🥁 Beat Maker Sequencer

Programme drum patterns and basslines on a step grid, just like a producer.

Teaching aim: pupils learn how electronic and pop grooves are built by sequencing samples on a 16 step grid. They explore the kick, snare and hi-hat backbeat, layer a looping bassline, then get targeted feedback on whether their pattern follows the conventions of a working groove. Covers the KS3 strand: music technology, recording and production.

100BPM

Tip: tap or click a square to switch a sound on or off. With the keyboard, Tab into the grid, move with the arrow keys, and toggle with Space. Press the play button (or focus it and press Enter) to hear your loop.

Producer's feedback

Build a pattern, then press "Check my groove".

Start with a kick on beats 1 and 3 and a snare on beats 2 and 4. That backbeat is the foundation of most pop and dance music.

Try a starting groove

Load a pattern, then change it and listen to how the feel shifts.

  • Kick (bass drum): the low thump that drives the pulse.
  • Snare: the sharp crack on the backbeat (usually beats 2 and 4).
  • Hi-hat: the fast ticking that keeps time.
  • Bass: a pitched loop that locks in with the kick.

Sample and Loop Lab: the mix

Producers balance the levels of each sample so nothing drowns out the rest. Adjust each track's volume.

A loop is a short pattern that repeats. Every bar you hear is the same 16 steps played round and round.
