How the forecast is built (teacher and pupil notes)

Attainment 8 is a points score across 8 subject slots. Each grade is worth its number of points (Grade 8 = 8 points). English and Maths count double, so the cohort fills 10 slot-weights in total. We add the weighted points and that gives the average Attainment 8 score per pupil.

SlotWeightCounts toward
English×2Attainment 8 and Progress 8
Maths×2Attainment 8 and Progress 8
EBacc 1, 2, 3×1Attainment 8 and Progress 8
Open 1, 2, 3×1Attainment 8 and Progress 8

Progress 8 compares this cohort's Attainment 8 to the national average for pupils with the same starting points. Here the demo national estimate for this prior-attainment cohort is A8 = 46.0. Progress 8 is (cohort A8 - 46.0) / 10, because each slot-grade above or below national adds one tenth of a grade per slot. A score of 0.00 means in line with national; +0.50 means half a grade per subject above; -0.50 means half a grade below (a floor-standard concern).

Scenario bands. No forecast is certain. The optimistic band assumes pupils on a grade boundary tip up; the cautious band assumes some slip. The band is wider when more slots sit exactly on a grade boundary, because those are the pupils most likely to move either way. The most-likely line sits in the middle.

On track vs off track. A slot is on track if its working-at grade is Grade 5 (a strong pass) or above. The forecast tells you how aggregated headline measures respond when you nudge a single subject, which is exactly the cohort-level question a Flight Path Tracker leaves unanswered.
