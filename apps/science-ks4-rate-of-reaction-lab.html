Collision theory in one paragraph

For a reaction to happen, particles must collide with enough energy (the activation energy) and the right orientation. Anything that makes collisions more frequent or more energetic speeds the reaction up. Higher temperature gives particles more kinetic energy, so they move faster and a larger fraction of collisions are successful. Higher concentration packs more particles into the same volume, so collisions are more frequent. Smaller pieces (more surface area) expose more particles to the acid. A catalyst lowers the activation energy, so more collisions succeed, without being used up itself.

🛢 Drag fractions into the tower

Crude oil is separated by fractional distillation. Short molecules have low boiling points and leave at the top (cool). Long molecules have high boiling points and leave at the bottom (hot). Drag each fraction to its level. Keyboard: focus a card, use arrow keys, press Enter to place.

🏭 Fractionating column

Alkanes have the formula CₙH₂ₙ₂₂. Cracking long alkanes makes shorter alkanes plus useful alkenes (such as ethene), which have a C=C double bond.
