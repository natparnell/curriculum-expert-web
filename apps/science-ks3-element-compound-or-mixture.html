Key ideas (KS3 chemistry)

Element A substance made of only one type of atom. It cannot be broken into anything simpler by chemistry. Example: oxygen (O₂), iron (Fe), helium (He). Elements are listed in the periodic table.

Compound Two or more different atoms chemically joined (bonded) in fixed proportions, making a new substance with new properties. Example: water (H₂O), salt (NaCl), carbon dioxide (CO₂). A compound can only be split by a chemical reaction.

Mixture Two or more substances jumbled together but not chemically joined. Each part keeps its own properties, so a mixture can be separated by physical methods. Example: air, sea water, ink.

Separating mixtures

  • Chromatography separates soluble coloured substances (like dyes in ink). More soluble dyes travel further up the paper.
  • Distillation separates a dissolved solid from its solvent, or two liquids with different boiling points. The liquid evaporates, then condenses back to pure liquid.
  • Filtration separates an insoluble solid from a liquid (for example sand from water).
  • Evaporation leaves a dissolved solid behind when the solvent evaporates (for example salt from salt water).

How to use this app

  • Mouse or touch: tap an atom to add it to the selected particle. Drag any built particle to reorder it.
  • Keyboard: Tab to each atom button and press Enter to add it. Use the + New particle button to start a separate particle.
  • Tab keys at the top switch activity; arrow keys move between them.
