👑The Feudal Pyramid Builder

Teacher aim: pupils build the feudal hierarchy of Norman England, then test how obligations and consequences rippled up and down it, and what happened when the Black Death pulled a layer out.

KS3 History · Medieval Britain 1066 to 1509: Church, state and society
After 1066 William the Conqueror reshaped England as a feudal pyramid. Drag each group to its correct level. The king owned all the land; everyone below held land in return for service. Top to bottom: most power and least number first.

The four groups

Drag a card into a slot, or select a card then press a slot's Place here button to use the keyboard.

The feudal pyramid

Tier 1 is the narrowest (fewest people, most power). Build downwards.

The pyramid only held together because of two-way obligations. Each group gave something and got something back. Pick a group to pull out of the structure and watch the consequences ripple up (who loses support) and down (who loses protection).

Pull a layer out

Build the pyramid first if these buttons are locked.

In 1348 to 1349 the Black Death killed roughly one in three people in England. Most deaths were at the bottom of the pyramid: the peasants. Drag the slider to set how many peasants survive, then run the outbreak to see how a shock at the base changed power for everyone.
