🇫🇷 Sound Spotter

Teacher aim: pupils link spelling, sound and meaning in French. They tap a letter pattern to hear it, meet three example words, then sort picture-words by their hidden sound.

Tap a French letter pattern

Each tile is a grapheme: a letter or group of letters. Tap to hear how it sounds, then meet three words that hide that sound.

Choose a tile above to see and hear three example words.

Why this matters

  • The same letters can make a very different sound in French. The tile ou sounds like "oo", not "ow".
  • Spotting the hidden sound helps pupils read new French words aloud with confidence.
  • Say each word with the class, then ask: which other words might hide the same sound?

Sound sorting game

Drag each picture-word into the bin for its hidden sound. Listen first if you are not sure. Watch the spelling: the sound is hiding inside the word.
