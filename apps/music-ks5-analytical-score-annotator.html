Analytical Score Annotator

KS5 Music · Listening, analysis & appraising

Teacher aim: Students annotate prescribed set-work extracts for harmony, structure and motivic development against a synced score and audio, then test their analysis and aural dictation under exam-style conditions. Project full screen; learners can drive it from a tablet or whiteboard.

1. Choose a set-work extract

Three fictional study extracts in different styles. Audio is synthesised live in the browser, no files needed.

2. Listen with the synced score

Press play to hear the extract while a playhead tracks the bars. Use this to hear the harmony and motifs before you label them.

76 bpm
Bar 1 · beat 1

Pick a tool below, then click a bar (or use the bar selector) to attach an annotation. Tab into the score and press Enter on a bar to annotate with the keyboard.

3. Annotate

Select an annotation type, choose the bar and a label, then add it. Annotations appear on the score and in the list below.

    Analysis challenge

    Higher-order listening questions on the selected extract: cadences, tonality, texture and motivic transformation. Immediate, specific feedback on every answer.

    Aural Dictation Pro

    Exam-style melodic dictation. Hear a short tonic-based phrase, then enter the scale degrees in order. You may replay it up to three times, just like the exam.

    Plays left: 3

    Tap a scale degree to add the next note. The tonic (doh) is degree 1. Phrases stay in a major key and move by step or small leap.
