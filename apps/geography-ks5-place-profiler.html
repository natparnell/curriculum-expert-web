KS5 Geography • Changing Places

Place Profiler

Lesson aim: Build a layered profile of a place, then judge how insider and outsider perspectives, lived experience and media representation shape the meaning and identity of that place.

Strand: Changing places. Key ideas: place identity and meaning; representation of place (formal and informal); insider and outsider perspectives; how demographic, economic and qualitative data combine to characterise a locality.

1 Choose a place to profile

All names and figures below are illustrative demonstration data, not a real survey.

2 Layer the data onto the place

Switch layers on to assemble the profile. Demographic, economic and qualitative layers each tell a partial story. Notice where they agree and where they clash.

3 Slide the perspective lens

Move the dial between a lifelong insider and a first-time outsider. The same place, read two ways.

← Insider (lived experience) Outsider (first impression) →

4 Sort the representations

Drag each representation into the perspective it comes from. On a keyboard or touch device, use the small Move button on a card. Decide: does this view come from someone who lives here, or someone looking in from outside?

🏠 Insider

Lived experience, belonging, everyday meaning.

👁️ Outsider

First impressions, media framing, looking in.

5 Write the place character

Use your layered profile and both perspectives to write a short characterisation. The checklist updates live as your writing hits each assessment criterion.
