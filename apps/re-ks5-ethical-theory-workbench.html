Ethical Theory Workbench ⚖️

Feed one moral dilemma to five normative theories at once. Read how natural law, situation ethics, virtue ethics, Kantian deontology and Bentham's utilitarianism each reason their way to a verdict, then contrast where and why they diverge.

Teacher aim: Pupils distinguish the five normative theories by their method of moral reasoning, not just their conclusions. They predict each verdict, test the prediction, and identify the precise point of disagreement between any two theories.

1. Choose a dilemma

Each is a clearly fictional A-level style case. Pick one to load it into the workbench.

2. Predict each theory's verdict

Before you run the workbench, commit to a prediction for each theory. This is the assessable thinking: you are reasoning from each theory's method, not guessing.

Make a prediction for at least one theory to run.

3. Compare the reasoning side by side

Natural law Situation ethics Virtue ethics Kant Bentham

4. Pinpoint the disagreement

Two theories can reach the same verdict for completely different reasons, or split on a single principle. Select any two to surface exactly where they part company on this dilemma.
