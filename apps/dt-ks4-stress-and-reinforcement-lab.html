Specialist principles: which reinforcement resists which force? (revision card)

Reinforcement works by changing how a load travels through a material. The goal is to convert a weakening force into one the member can carry, or to spread it over more material.

ForceWhat it doesBest reinforcementWhy
TensionPulls apart along the lengthWebbing (a tie)A taut tie carries the pull in pure tension, the strongest mode for a fibre or strap.
CompressionSquashes; long members buckleLaminationBonded layers increase the effective section and resist buckling far better than one sheet.
ShearSlides one part past anotherTriangulationTriangles cannot change shape without changing a side length, so they lock against sliding.
TorsionTwists about the long axisTriangulation (bracing)Diagonal bracing resists the twist; a closed, braced section is far stiffer in torsion.
