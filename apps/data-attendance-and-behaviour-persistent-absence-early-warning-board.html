Persistent Absence Early-Warning Board

Flag pupils against the 90% and 50% attendance thresholds, then plan recovery

🎯 Teacher aim: identify who is Persistently Absent (below 90%) or Severely Absent (below 50%), and work out how many sessions each needs to recover before year end.

How the thresholds work

In England, a pupil is Persistently Absent (PA) when their attendance falls below 90% of possible sessions, and Severely Absent (SA) when it falls below 50%. Each school day has 2 sessions (morning and afternoon). Each card opens on the year-end figure projected if the pupil continues at their current rate. Drag a recovery slider to model attending more (or fewer) of the remaining sessions, and watch the projected percentage, the RAG flag and the sessions-to-recover update live.

How to read this board. The percentage shown is the projected year-end figure: sessions already attended plus the future sessions you have planned, divided by all possible sessions for the full year. The dashed lines on each bar mark the 90% PA threshold and the 50% SA threshold. "Sessions to clear PA" is the smallest number of remaining sessions a pupil must attend to finish the year at or above 90%. Where that number exceeds the sessions still available, the pupil cannot mathematically recover this year and the card flags it. All names and figures are fictional.
