Composition Brief Builder 🎼

Teacher aim: Scaffold the GCSE Music composing-to-a-brief task. Pupils receive an exam-style brief, then build a melody, a chord progression, a structure and a development plan, hearing each layer played back before self-assessing against the assessment criteria.

Step 1: Generate your brief

GCSE composition asks you to respond to a set brief. Pick a style focus, or let the builder generate one for you. Read it carefully: every choice you make later should answer this brief.

Step 2: Build a melodic idea

Compose an eight-note motif in C major. Tap a key then tap a slot, or select a slot and play a key. Aim for a clear shape (mostly stepwise with one or two leaps) that ends on a stable note.

Selected slot: 1 of 8. Use number keys 1 to 8 to pick a slot, then letters C D E F G A B for pitches.

Add at least six notes, then check your melody for shape and ending advice.

Step 3: Harmony and cadence coach

Choose four chords (one per bar) to support your melody. In C major the common chords are shown. Strong progressions usually start on I and finish with a satisfying cadence.

Fill all four bars, then check your cadence. Tip: a perfect cadence is V then I.

Step 4: Plan the structure

Drag, or tap to add, sections onto the timeline to outline your whole piece. Examiners reward a clear, balanced structure with contrast. A typical song is Intro, Verse, Chorus, Verse, Chorus.

Tap a section above to append it. Tap a block on the timeline to remove it.

Add three or more sections to get feedback on balance and contrast.

Step 5: Develop your ideas

Top marks come from developing material, not just repeating it. Tick the techniques you will use, and hear two worked examples applied to your motif. Choose at least three for a developed composition.
