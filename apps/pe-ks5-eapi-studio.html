KS5 Physical Education · Performance analysis and improvement

EAPI Studio 🏃

Teacher aim: Take learners through the full EAPI coursework cycle. Identify a performance weakness, justify its causes against theory, and design a targeted programme of improvement, with live feedback at every stage.

Stage 1 · Identify the performance weakness

Choose a performer profile, then frame one specific, measurable weakness. The studio scores how precise and analytical your framing is.

Fictional performers for demonstration only.
Strong statements name the moment in performance, use a measurable indicator, and link to an outcome. Try to include words such as "shown by", "in the final", "leading to", or numbers and timings.

Stage 2 · Justify the causes against theory

A high mark band needs causes anchored in theory, not opinion. Drag each cause card into the theory area it belongs to. Use the keyboard buttons if you prefer.

Stage 3 · Build the programme of improvement

Design at least four sessions that target your weakness. The studio applies SMART and FITT logic and checks the principle of progressive overload across your weeks.

Progressive overload check looks at whether intensity or volume rises across your weeks. Specificity check compares your chosen methods to your Stage 1 component.

Stage 4 · Evaluate and review

Pull your work together. Set a SMART target and a measurable re-test, then read the studio's overall coursework readiness rating.
