How the Externally Set Assignment works, and what each assessment objective rewards

Component 2 is the AQA A-level Externally Set Assignment and is worth 40% of the qualification. AQA releases an exam paper of starting points or themes. You then have an extended preparatory period to develop a personal response, before a 15-hour period of supervised time in which you make your final outcome. Everything below is illustrative teaching content, not the real paper.

1. Paper releasedChoose one starting point and begin to respond personally.
2. Preparatory periodResearch, experiment, record and refine your idea over several weeks.
3. 15-hour supervised examProduce the final outcome under supervised conditions, usually across set sessions.

The four assessment objectives, at A-level depth

AO1 · Develop

Develop ideas through sustained and focused investigation of sources, informed by contextual and other references. Rewards depth, not just a tidy artist page: show how a source shapes your thinking and feeds your own intentions.

AO2 · Explore & refine

Explore and select appropriate resources, media, materials, techniques and processes, reviewing and refining as work develops. Rewards purposeful experiment and evidence that you learn from what does and does not work.

AO3 · Record

Record ideas, observations and insights relevant to intentions, reflecting critically on work and progress. Rewards first-hand observational recording and annotation that reasons, rather than describes.

AO4 · Present

Present a personal and meaningful response that realises intentions and, where appropriate, makes connections between visual and other elements. Rewards resolution: a final outcome that answers your own question and links back to your investigation.

Each AO is equally weighted at A-level. Strong responses thread one personal intention through all four, rather than treating them as four separate boxes to tick.
