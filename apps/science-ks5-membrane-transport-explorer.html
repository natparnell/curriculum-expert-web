What am I looking at? (the four mechanisms)

Simple diffusion: net movement of particles from high to low concentration, down the gradient, through the phospholipid bilayer. Only small, non-polar molecules (oxygen, carbon dioxide, steroid hormones) pass this way. Passive: no ATP. Stops at equilibrium.

Facilitated diffusion: still passive and still down the gradient, but large or polar molecules and ions use channel or carrier proteins. Rate depends on the number of protein channels, so it plateaus once all channels are saturated.

Osmosis: net movement of water molecules from a region of higher water potential to lower water potential, across a partially permeable membrane. A higher solute concentration gives a lower (more negative) water potential, so water moves towards the more concentrated side.

Active transport: carrier proteins (pumps) move solute against its concentration gradient, from low to high. This requires energy from ATP hydrolysis. Cut the ATP and the pump stalls.

Exam-style links (A-level)

Surface area, gradient and membrane thickness all feature in Fick's law: rate of diffusion is proportional to (surface area × concentration difference) ÷ diffusion distance. Try increasing the gradient and watch net flux rise.

Facilitated diffusion and active transport both use proteins, so both can saturate; only active transport needs ATP and only active transport can move solute up the gradient. Use the Challenge to prove this.

Water potential is written with the symbol psi and measured in kilopascals; pure water is 0 kPa and adding solute makes it negative.
