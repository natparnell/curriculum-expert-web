The formulae you need (GCSE Higher)

Edexcel 1MA1 expects you to recall the volume and surface area of prisms and cylinders, and to use the given formulae for cones, spheres and frustums. Lengths here are in centimetres, so volume comes out in cm³ and surface area in cm².

SolidVolumeSurface area
Cuboidl × w × h2(lw + lh + wh)
Prismcross-section × length2 × ends + perimeter × length
Cylinderπr²h2πr² + 2πrh
Cone⅓πr²hπr² + πrl  (l = slant)
Sphere⅄πr³4πr²
Square pyramid⅓ × base × hbase + 4 half-triangles
Frustumcone − small cone2 circles + slant band

Slant height

For a cone, the slant height l is found with Pythagoras: l = √(r² + h²). For a frustum, the sloped band uses the slant of the full cone minus the slant of the cut-off cone.

Compound solids

When two solids are joined, add the volumes. For surface area you must remove the faces that are hidden where they meet: a silo (cylinder + cone) loses the cylinder's top circle, so its surface is the base circle, the curved cylinder wall and the cone's curved (slant) surface only. When one solid is drilled out of another, subtract its volume but add the new internal surface.
