KS3 Science: variation, inheritance and natural selection

Teacher aim: pupils collect class data on a chosen trait, plot it as a histogram, and decide whether the variation is continuous or discontinuous. They then link the spread of variation to natural selection.

From variation to natural selection

Natural selection needs variation to work on. Where a trait varies, individuals best suited to their environment are more likely to survive, breed, and pass on the helpful version of the trait. Over many generations the whole population can shift.
