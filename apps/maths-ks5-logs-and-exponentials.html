Logarithms & Exponentials

Teaching aim: understand exponential and logarithmic functions as inverses, apply the laws of logarithms, solve exponential equations by taking logs, and linearise experimental data of the form y=axn or y=kbx to recover the model from a straight-line fit.

What am I looking at?

y=ax grows by a constant factor a for every unit step in x. It is always positive, so the x-axis (y=0) is a horizontal asymptote.

e ≈ 2.71828 is the special base whose curve has gradient equal to its own height: d/dx(ex)=ex.

ln x is the inverse of ex. It is only defined for x>0, passes through (1, 0), and has the y-axis as a vertical asymptote. Reflecting ex in the line y=x gives ln x.

The three laws + change of base

Why they work

The laws explained

Product: logxy = logx + logy. Multiplying numbers adds their logs, because logs are exponents and am×an=am+n.

Quotient: log(x/y) = logx − logy, from am÷an=am−n.

Power: log(xk) = k logx, from (am)k=amk.

Change of base: logax = logx / loga, valid for any common base. This is how a calculator finds log2x from ln or log10.

The numeric panels recompute live: the left and right sides match (to rounding), which is the proof in action.

Working

The strategy

When the unknown is in the exponent, you cannot reach it by ordinary algebra. Take logs of both sides so the power law log(ax)=x loga brings x down to the front, then divide.

For A ekx=c, divide by A first, then take the natural log so ln(ekx)=kx, and finally divide by k.

Plot

Recover the model

How linearising works

Power law y=axn: take logs of both sides to get logy = n logx + loga. So plotting logy against logx gives a straight line of gradient n and intercept loga.

Exponential y=kbx: take logs to get logy = (logb)x + logk. Plot logy against x: gradient is logb, intercept is logk.

Read the gradient and intercept off the fitted line, then undo the logs to recover the constants. This is the standard Edexcel exponential-models technique.
