Election Lab

Teacher aim: Pupils run one set of votes through three voting systems at once (First Past the Post, Alternative Vote and Proportional Representation) and explain why the same ballots can crown different winners.

How each system works (quick guide)

First Past the Post (FPTP): the party with the most first-choice votes wins the seat outright. Simple, but a party can win with well under half the votes if the others split.

Alternative Vote (AV): if no party has more than half, the last-placed party is knocked out and its voters' next choices are shared on. This repeats until one party passes 50%. The winner has broader support.

Proportional Representation (PR): seats are shared in line with the share of votes. A party on 30% of votes gets close to 30 of the 100 seats. Small parties win a fairer share, but one party rarely controls the council alone.
