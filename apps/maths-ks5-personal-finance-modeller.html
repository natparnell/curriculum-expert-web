📰 Estimate first, then judge the headline

A Fermi estimate uses sensible round numbers to get an order-of-magnitude answer. Estimate the figure yourself, then compare it with the headline claim and decide whether the claim is fair.

Headline (fictional, for teaching): "Average UK household throws away £1,000 of food a year, study finds."
Estimate it from the ground up. Roughly how much does a typical household spend on food per week, and what fraction gets binned?

Critical question to ask of any money headline: what is the base figure, is it a mean or a median, and is the change absolute (in pounds) or relative (a percentage)? A "100% rise" from £2 to £4 sounds dramatic but is only £2.
