Empires Connected

Teacher aim: Pupils study a non-British world empire (Mughal India or Qing China) and map its trade, idea and conflict links to Europe, building the concept of global interconnection rather than isolated national history. KS3 World History strand.

1. Choose a world empire to study

Both were among the wealthiest, most powerful states on Earth in their day, and both were tied into a connected world long before British rule. Pick one to investigate.

2. Map the connections to Europe

Trade link Idea / culture link Conflict / rivalry link

Drag each connection card onto the world region it links your empire to. On a keyboard, press a card to pick it up, then press a region to place it. Each card belongs to exactly one region.
