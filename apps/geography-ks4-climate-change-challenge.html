The Challenge of Climate Change

The key distinction: cause vs impact

Mitigation reduces or prevents the emission of greenhouse gases, so it attacks the cause of climate change. Examples in the AQA specification: switching to alternative (renewable) energy, carbon capture and storage, planting trees, and international agreements such as the Paris Agreement. Mitigation lowers the projected temperature rise.

Adaptation does not reduce emissions at all. It responds to the changes that are already happening, reducing the impact on people and the environment. Examples: changing agricultural systems (drought-resistant crops, new planting times), managing water supply (reservoirs, efficiency, desalination), and reducing the risk from rising sea levels (flood defences, managed retreat). Adaptation lowers the residual risk to people but leaves the temperature rise unchanged.

A strong answer recognises that we need both: mitigation alone is too slow to remove all risk this century, and adaptation alone does nothing to stop the warming getting worse.
