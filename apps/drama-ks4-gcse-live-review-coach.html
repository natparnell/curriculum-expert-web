    How the GCSE bands work for reviewing live theatre

    Most GCSE Drama specifications mark live theatre evaluation across three things: acting, design and the effect on the audience. The four-band scale below is a simplified, exam-board-style guide for the classroom.

    BandWhat it looks like
    Band 1Mostly description or vague opinion. Little subject terminology. Few or no specific moments from the production.
    Band 2Some terminology and examples, but uneven. One area covered well, others thin. Effect on the audience is asserted, not explained.
    Band 3Clear analysis of acting and design with accurate terminology and precise examples. Audience effect is explained.
    Band 4Sustained, evaluative judgement across acting, design and impact. Specific moments linked to the precise effect created for the audience, with confident terminology.

    Tip for pupils: a top review keeps answering "so what?" Name the moment, name the technique, then judge the effect on the audience watching.
