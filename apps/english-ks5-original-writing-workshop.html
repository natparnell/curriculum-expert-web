    ✓ What the mark scheme rewards

    AQA A-level English Language and Literature, original writing & commentary.

    The original writing
    • AO5 rewards expression: a sophisticated, controlled and conscious crafting of language that is well shaped for audience and purpose.
    • AO2 rewards how meanings are made: deliberate use of narrative voice, structure, imagery, register and form to create effects.
    • Top bands show flair and originality, sustained control of an authentic voice, and choices that clearly serve a defined reader.
    The reflective commentary
    • AO1 rewards accurate linguistic and literary terminology and a coherent, well-organised account.
    • AO3 rewards analysis of how context (audience, purpose, genre, the base text) shaped your choices.
    • Strong commentaries quote the candidate's own writing, name the technique, and explain the intended effect on the reader, not just label features.
    Common pitfalls
    • Listing features without explaining effect ("I used a simile here").
    • A commentary that retells the plot instead of justifying choices.
    • Voice or register that drifts and stops suiting the audience.
    • Over-writing: figurative language piled on until meaning blurs.
