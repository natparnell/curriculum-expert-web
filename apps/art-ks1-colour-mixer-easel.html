For the teacher
  • Primary colours are red, yellow and blue. You cannot make them by mixing.
  • Secondary colours: red plus yellow gives orange; yellow plus blue gives green; blue plus red gives purple.
  • Mixing all three, or the same colour twice, gives a muddy brown. Ask why this happens.
  • Discussion prompt: where do we see orange, green and purple outside? (Fictional pupils Asha, Bram and Cleo can each name one.)
  • Tablet tip: drag the paint blobs with a finger; the palette glows when a paint is over it.
