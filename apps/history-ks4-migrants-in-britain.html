Migrants in Britain, c800 to the present day

Teaching aim: to track the waves of migration to Britain across four eras and to reason as a historian about change and continuity, second-order concepts that sit at the heart of the Edexcel thematic study, while using the historic environment of Notting Hill, c1948 to c1970, to read the evidence on the ground.

How to use the era scrubber

  1. Drag the slider or click an era label to move across the period c800 to present.
  2. The timeline canvas highlights the era you have selected and plots the major migrant groups.
  3. The four live readout cards answer the historian's questions for that era: who migrated, why (push and pull factors), their experience, and their impact.

Thematic tracker: reasoning about change and continuity

  1. Pick a theme below. The tracker shows how that one theme plays out across all four eras side by side.
  2. Reading across the columns is how Edexcel expects you to argue change and continuity over the whole period.
  3. The two callout strips name the strongest change and the strongest continuity for your chosen theme, ready to drop into a "how far do you agree" answer.

Notting Hill, c1948 to c1970: the historic environment

  1. This is the Edexcel historic environment case study: a single place studied closely.
  2. Click a numbered site on the map, or use the buttons, to read what happened there and which sources a historian would use to investigate it.
  3. Notice how the same few streets hold poor housing, violence, community defence and celebration: that is the layered story of a real place.

Edexcel-style practice questions

These mirror Paper 1 of the Edexcel GCSE (1HI0): the thematic study questions and the historic environment questions. Plan your answer, then reveal the examiner's guidance. The model notes show the skill being assessed, not a sentence to copy.
