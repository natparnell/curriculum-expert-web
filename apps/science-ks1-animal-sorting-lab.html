Science · KS1 · Animals, including humans

Animal Sorting Lab 🔬

Teacher aim: pupils sort animals into the five groups (fish, amphibian, reptile, bird, mammal), choose each animal's diet (herbivore, carnivore, omnivore), name human body parts and recall what animals need to live.

1. Sort the animals into their pens

Drag an animal into the right pen. On a keyboard, press an animal to pick it up, then press a pen to drop it. After it lands, choose what it eats.

Pick an animal to begin.

Sorted: 0 of 10

Tip: mammals have fur or hair and feed milk to their babies. Birds have feathers. Fish live in water and have gills. Amphibians (like frogs) start in water. Reptiles have dry, scaly skin.

2. Label the body

Pick a word, then press the matching dotted spot on the body. The spot turns green when it is correct.

Choose a word to start.

3. What do animals need to live?

Every animal, including you, needs these things to stay alive and healthy.

  • 💧Water to drink
  • 🍎Food to eat
  • 💨Air to breathe
  • 🏠Shelter and a safe home
