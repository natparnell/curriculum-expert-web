How a join works

In Year 2 we begin to join letters. Most joins start with a diagonal stroke that climbs up from the baseline to meet the next letter. Letters that finish at the top (like o, r, v, w) use a flatter horizontal join instead. Always keep your letters sitting on the baseline.

The four handwriting families

Letters that are made in a similar way belong to the same family. Learning them in families helps every letter start in the right place and go in the right direction.

Top handwriting tips

  • Start every letter at the correct starting point. Most lower-case letters begin at the top.
  • Keep tall letters (ascenders) reaching up to the top line, and tails (descenders) dropping below the baseline.
  • Capital letters and digits are all tall: they sit between the top line and the baseline.
  • Sit your letters neatly on the baseline so words look tidy and are easy to join later.
