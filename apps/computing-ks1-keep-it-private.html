🔐 Keep It Private

KS1 Computing: using technology safely and respectfully

Teacher aim: Pupils decide which information is safe to share online and which to keep private, and learn who they can tell if something worries them.

How to play

A card shows one piece of information. Decide: is it safe to share, or should we keep it private?

Drag the card into a bucket, or use the buttons. You can also press Tab to reach the card, then the S key for Share or the P key for Private.

Teacher notes and curriculum links
  • Strand: Computing (KS1), "use technology safely and respectfully, keeping personal information private; identify where to go for help and support".
  • Key idea: Personal information (full name, home address, school, phone number, passwords, photos that show where I am) is private. General, non-identifying things (favourite colour, a drawing, a made-up username) can usually be shared.
  • Talk prompts: "Why might a stranger want to know where you live?" and "Who would you tell first?"
  • Names used are fictional and for demonstration only. No real pupil information is shown.
  • Pair this with your school's online safety policy and the "tell a trusted adult" message.
