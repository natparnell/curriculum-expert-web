✋ Grip and Grab

EYFS · Physical Education · Fine Motor Skills and Hand Control
Teacher aim: Model correct pinch and tool grip on the big screen before active play. Children watch, then have a go: threading, pinching and tracing build the small hand muscles and the tripod grip needed for pencils, scissors and PE equipment.

Thread the Beads

Drag each bead with one finger and pop it onto the string in order: red, then yellow, then blue. Slow, steady hands.

👋Start with the red bead.
Beads threaded: 0 of 3

Keyboard: Tab to a bead, press Enter to pick up, move with arrow keys, press Enter to drop.

Pinch and Pick

Use a careful pinch (thumb and finger) to drag each berry into the bowl. Drop a berry too far away and it rolls back. Aim for the bowl.

🍇Pinch a berry and drop it in the bowl.
Berries in the bowl: 0 of 5

Keyboard: Tab to a berry, press Enter to pick up, move with arrow keys, press Enter to drop.

Trace the Path

Hold like a pencil and keep your finger on the line. Travel from the green dot to the red flag without leaving the path.

✏️Put your finger on the green dot to begin.
Path traced: 0%

Keyboard: click the path, then press the right arrow key to travel along it.

For the adult (not for the children)

  • Strand: EYFS Physical Development, Fine Motor Skills. Builds the small hand and finger muscles and the tripod (pinch) grip that underpins pencil control, scissor use and handling PE apparatus.
  • Model first: project on the whiteboard, narrate "pinch with thumb and finger", "slow, steady hand", then invite a child up. Use it as a 3 minute warm up before the active PE session.
  • Watch for: whole-hand grabbing instead of a pinch, pressing too hard, or rushing. Praise control over speed.
  • Then move: follow with the physical equivalent. Real beads and laces, tweezers and pompoms, chalk on the playground.
  • Touch, mouse and keyboard all work. Names and data shown are made up.
