Live structure checklist

A top-band discursive essay answers the question with a clear line of argument, weighs both sides and ends with a justified conclusion.

1

Thesis statement

Your line of argument

State your overall position in the target language. The examiner should know from the first sentence what you will argue. Avoid sitting on the fence.

2

Argument paragraphs

Point + evidence + link

Build at least one paragraph that supports your thesis and one that argues the other side. Each paragraph needs a clear point, a piece of supporting evidence or example, and a sophisticated linking phrase to introduce or join it.

3

Conclusion

Answer the question

Draw the threads together and give a justified verdict that directly answers the question set. A subjunctive trigger (for example bien que, aunque) signals evaluative, top-band writing.

What the AQA mark scheme rewards

AO4 · Response to the question
Critical & analytical response

A clear, sustained line of argument that addresses the precise question, develops ideas with relevant evidence and reaches a justified conclusion. Description alone scores in the lower bands.

AO3 · Range & accuracy of language
Grammar & complexity

Varied, complex structures used accurately: the subjunctive, the conditional, relative and subordinate clauses, the passive. Top bands need ambitious language that mostly stays accurate.

Organisation & cohesion
Structure

A logical structure with a thesis, balanced development, a counter-argument and a conclusion. Sophisticated connectives signpost the argument and link ideas across paragraphs.

Evidence & the work studied
Detail & precision

Specific evidence, examples and reference to the book or film studied, used to support points rather than retell the plot. Precision of vocabulary and idiom lifts the response.

Grammar points being tested

    How AQA marks translation

    Into the target language
    Communication + accuracy

    The passage is divided into chunks. Marks are awarded for conveying the meaning of each chunk and for grammatical accuracy. Tenses, agreement, the subjunctive and pronoun placement are the usual traps.

    Out of the target language
    Into accurate English

    Accurate, natural English that captures register and idiom. Avoid word-for-word renderings: show you understand tense, mood and structure rather than translating mechanically.
