Physical Education · Key Stage 2 · Striking and fielding

Field the Ball 🏏

Teaching aim: pupils learn to position fielders in a striking and fielding game (cricket, rounders) so the team stops the most balls. They test a plan against a chosen type of hit and read the percentage stopped.

1. Choose the batter's hit

Different batters hit to different places. Pick a hit, then drag your seven fielders to cover where the ball is likely to go.

2. Position your fielders

Drag a fielder to move them. On a keyboard: press Tab to a fielder, then use the arrow keys to nudge them.

    Why fielding placement matters

    In cricket and rounders the fielding team cannot cover every blade of grass with only a few players. Good teams read the batter and the bowling, then move fielders to the most likely landing zones. That is a tactic, not luck.

    • Match your shape to the hit: pack the leg side against a pull, the off side against a drive.
    • Keep some depth: leave one or two fielders deep so a big hit does not run for extra rounders or runs.
    • Do not bunch everyone in one spot. Two fielders one metre apart cover little more than one.
