The electrolysis rules (KS4)
  • Molten ionic compound: metal forms at the cathode, non-metal at the anode. Simple.
  • Solution (aqueous): water also splits into H⁺ and OH⁻.
  • Cathode rule: if the metal is more reactive than hydrogen (e.g. Na, K, Ca, Al), hydrogen is produced. If the metal is less reactive (e.g. Cu, Ag), the metal is produced.
  • Anode rule: if a halide (Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻) is present, the halogen is produced. Otherwise (sulfate, nitrate) oxygen is produced from OH⁻.

3. Energy profile of the change 📈

Electrolysis is driven by electrical energy: the products store more energy than the reactants, so it is an endothermic process overall. Drag the slider to change the activation energy and read the profile. Then label this profile.
