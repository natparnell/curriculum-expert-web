Build a Settlement

KS2 Geography · Human geography: settlements, land use, economic activity, trade and resources

Teacher aim: Pupils place homes, shops, farms and a river crossing on the land and discover why real settlements grow where there is fresh water, flat ground, food and a place to trade.

Why settlements grow

Geographers say people choose places that meet their needs. Watch how the model rewards good choices.

💧 Water
Homes near the river have fresh water. Homes too far away struggle.
🌾 Food
Farms need flat, green farmland, not the steep hills.
🌉 Crossing point
A bridge lets people cross the river to trade. Settlements grew at crossing points.
🏪 Trade
Shops do best in the middle of homes, near the bridge.
⛰️ Hills
Steep hills are hard to build on. Few people settle there.

Map key

  • River fresh water, you cannot build on it
  • Hills steep, hard to build on
  • Farmland flat and green, good for farms
Where does food come from?

The bread in a shop starts as wheat on a farm. The more farms near your shops, the more your settlement can feed itself and trade the rest. This is the journey of resources: farm to shop to home.
