Poem Pair Comparer

KS4 English Literature – poetry comparison (anthology and unseen)

Teacher aim: Pupils choose two poems, sort shared and distinct themes and methods into a Venn comparison, and build precise comparative sentences. Target: GCSE AO2 (methods and effects) and AO3 (links between poems). Use on a projector or tablet.

1 Choose two poems

Pick one poem on each side. Try a pair from the same cluster first (the colour tag shows the cluster), then test a tougher cross-cluster pair.

2 Sort the themes and methods

Drag each card into a zone, or select a card with the keyboard and press A, S or B to place it. The app checks whether a card really is shared or distinct, and tells you why.

3 Build comparative sentences

Sentence stems are generated from the cards you sorted into the Shared zone (and any distinct cards). Choose the move you want, then generate a stem to adapt in your own writing.

Demonstration content for teaching comparison skills. Poem summaries are short paraphrases for classroom use, not the poems themselves. Anthology references reflect the AQA Power and Conflict and Love and Relationships clusters.
