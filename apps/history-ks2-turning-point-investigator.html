Turning Point Investigator

Teaching aim: pupils weigh evidence of daily life before and after a key event to judge how much really changed, using the historical concept of change and continuity.

1 Choose a turning point to investigate

Choose a turning point

A turning point is a moment in history when things change. Pick one to find out: did daily life really change a lot, or did much of it stay the same?

2 Sort the evidence onto the balance

Drag each picture of daily life onto the matching pan: Before the turning point, or After it. On a keyboard, select a card with Enter or Space, then press B for Before, A for After, or T to send it back to the tray.
