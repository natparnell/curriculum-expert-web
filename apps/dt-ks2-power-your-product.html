Power Your Product 🔦

D&T · KS2 · Technical knowledge: electrical systems

Teacher aim: pupils build a simple circuit (cell, switch and an output: bulb, buzzer or motor), test that it works, then fit the working circuit inside a product they have designed. They learn that a circuit is a system inside a product, not just a science diagram.

Step 1

Choose your design brief

Pick the product your circuit will power. The output you need changes with the product.

Step 2

Wire the circuit Circuit works

Drag each component onto a slot in the loop. You need a power source, a switch and the right output. Then close the switch and test it.

Component tray

Keyboard: press Enter on a component, then press Enter on a flashing slot to place it.

Circuit board

Tick to close the switch. A circuit only works when the switch is closed.
Place a power source, a switch and the correct output, then close the switch and test.
Step 3

Fit the circuit inside your product Product complete

Your circuit is now a working module. Drag the module into the empty space inside the product casing so it is hidden and protected.

Design review: 0 of 3 done

  • The circuit lights, buzzes or spins when tested.
  • The switch lets the user turn it on and off.
  • The module is fitted neatly inside the product casing.
Drag the circuit module into the casing.
