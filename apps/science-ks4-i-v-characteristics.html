Physics behind the shapes

Ohmic conductor (fixed resistor): at constant temperature, current is directly proportional to potential difference (V = IR with R constant). The I-V graph is a straight line through the origin.

Filament lamp: as current increases the metal filament heats up, the metal ions vibrate more, electrons collide more often, so resistance rises. The line curves and flattens (an S-shape through the origin).

Diode: conducts in one direction only. Almost no current until forward p.d. passes about 0.7 V, then current rises steeply. In reverse the current stays near zero.
