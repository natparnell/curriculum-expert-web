Contemporary Compass

Teacher aim: students situate their personal work within contemporary and historical practice by positioning it on three axes (medium, concept, era) against living and recent artists, then articulate a genuine, defensible connection rather than a borrowed one. How to read the compass (teacher and student notes)

Connections in critical and contextual studies must be defensible: rooted in shared concerns of medium, idea or moment, not just a name dropped because the work looks superficially similar. This tool gives every reference artist a position on the same three axes a student rates their own work on.

  • Medium (left to right): from traditional handling (paint, print, drawing) to experimental forms (installation, digital, lens-based, time-based).
  • Concept (down to up): from representational, depicting the visible world, to conceptual, where the idea leads the form.
  • Era (down to up): from historically rooted practice to of-the-moment, emergent practice.
  • Distance on all three axes is what the app scores. A strong link is close on at least two axes and the written claim names a shared concern.

Keyboard: Tab to the orange marker, then use ← → for medium and ↑ ↓ for the active vertical axis. Hold Shift for larger steps.

Artists shown are real practitioners chosen as exam-relevant references; their plotted positions are a teaching simplification for discussion, not a definitive ranking. Encourage students to challenge a position and justify a move.
