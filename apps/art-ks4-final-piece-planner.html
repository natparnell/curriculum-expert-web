Final Piece Planner

Teacher aim: pupils assemble their compositional studies into a mock-up of the final piece, then check that the outcome presents a personal, meaningful response (AO4) that connects back to their AO1 to AO3 evidence.

1. Your study tray Drag onto the canvas

These are the studies in Maya Trelawny's mock exam sketchbook on the theme "Erosion". Each is tagged with the assessment objective it gives evidence for. Place the ones you want to build your final piece from.

Keyboard: press Enter or Space on a study, then choose a canvas zone. Mouse or touch: drag a study onto the canvas grid.

2. Final piece mock-up AO4 outcome

Drop studies into the nine compositional zones. The centre zones sit on the rule-of-thirds intersections, so what you place there reads as your focal point.

What the examiner is looking for (AO4)
  • A personal and meaningful response, not a generic copy.
  • A coherent outcome with a clear focal point and considered composition.
  • Visible connections to your earlier recording (AO3), experimenting (AO2) and developing of ideas (AO1).
  • Selection and refinement: you do not need every study, you need the right ones.
