The American West c1835 to 1895

Teaching aim: to understand how the West developed across three phases, and to analyse the causes and consequences of the conflict between the Plains Indians, the white settlers and the United States government that led to conquest and the closing of the frontier.

An interactive timeline of the West's development

The course divides the period into three phases. Select an event on the timeline to reveal its causes and its consequences, the core skill tested in Edexcel period-study questions. Use the filter to focus on one phase at a time.

Conflict over the Plains: three perspectives

The same forces (the buffalo, the railroads, the broken treaties, the spread of settlement) were experienced very differently by the Plains Indians, the white settlers and the United States government. Choose a factor to contrast all three viewpoints and see how it pushed the period towards conquest.

Edexcel period-study questions

The period study (Paper 2, part A) tests three question types: consequence, narrative analysis of how events led to a development, and importance. Attempt each in your own words, then reveal a model approach and the mark-scheme focus.

Specification: Edexcel GCSE History (1HI0), Paper 2 Period Study P4: The American West, c1835 to c1895. Three phases: early settlement, development of the Plains, conflicts and conquest. Key concepts: cause, consequence, change, significance.
