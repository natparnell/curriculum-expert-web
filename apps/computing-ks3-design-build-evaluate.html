Design-Build-Evaluate Studio

Computing · Key Stage 3 · Digital creativity and developing solutions to real problems

Teacher aim  Pupils design a digital product to a client brief, prototype it from a toolkit, then run a structured user-test and iterate against real user needs.

1. Define the problem

Read the client brief, then pin down who the product is for and what success looks like.

Client brief

From: Ms Okafor, Year 7 Form Tutor (fictional)

Problem: New Year 7 pupils keep getting lost, forgetting equipment and missing clubs in their first half term. Paper planners get left at home.

Ask: Design a small digital product that helps a new Year 7 find their way and stay organised in their first weeks.

2. Design the concept

Sketch your idea in words. Good designers make the user's journey explicit before building anything.

3. Prototype with the toolkit

A real digital product combines several tools. Drag tools into your build, or use the keyboard: focus a tool and press Enter to add it. Aim for the right tools for your design, not the most tools.

Toolkit (drag or press Enter)

Your prototype build

Drop tools here to assemble your product.

4. Run a structured user-test

Three fictional testers try your prototype. Set how well it met each of them, based on what your build actually contains. Be honest: weak builds get weak scores.

5. Evaluate and iterate

Compare what you built against the brief and the user-test. Then decide one change to make next.
