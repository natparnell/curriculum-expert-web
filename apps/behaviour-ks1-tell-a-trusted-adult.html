KS1 • Being Safe

Tell a Trusted Adult

Teacher aim: pupils learn that worries and "secrets that feel wrong" should be told to a trusted adult, that surprises are happy and secrets that upset us are not, and that telling brings relief and help.

Let's help our friends feel safe

In our class we have a big rule. If something makes you feel worried, scared or yucky inside, you do not keep it in. You tell a trusted adult.

We are going to meet some children. You will help them choose what to do. Watch what happens to their worry when they tell.

How to play together: read each scene out loud, talk as a class, then tap a choice. The app will show you what happens next.
🙂

What should they do?

Worry inside

Secret or surprise?

A surprise is a happy secret that ends with a smile, and it will be told soon. A secret that feels wrong makes you worried and someone says you must never tell. Sort each one.

A surprise we tell soon and it makes people happy. A secret that makes you feel worried is always one to tell a trusted adult.

My trusted adults

A trusted adult is a grown-up who keeps you safe and listens. If one is busy or you are not sure, you keep telling until someone helps. Choose the grown-ups you could tell.

Underwear rule: the parts of your body covered by your underwear are private. They are yours. If anyone makes you feel worried, you can say no, get away, and tell a trusted adult.
