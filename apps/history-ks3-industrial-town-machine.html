Teacher notes and curriculum links
  • Strand: Britain 1745–1901: ideas, political power, industry and empire, including slavery and its abolition.
  • Concepts modelled: cause and consequence (industry drives growth but raises pollution and child labour), change over time (reforms shift the dials), and significance (the 1807 and 1833 Acts, the Factory and Reform Acts).
  • Key dates to draw out: 1807 abolition of the slave trade; 1832 Great Reform Act (widened the franchise); 1833 Factory Act and Slavery Abolition Act; 1842 Mines Act; 1847 Ten Hours Act.
  • Discussion prompts: Could Britain have industrialised without empire and enslaved labour? Why did reform take decades? Who gained the vote in 1832, and who did not?
