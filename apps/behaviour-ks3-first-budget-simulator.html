Behaviour & Pastoral · KS3 · Living in the wider world: money and economic wellbeing
Teacher aim: Pupils manage a teenager's monthly money, sorting needs from wants and reacting to a surprise cost. They see the immediate consequence of every spending choice and learn why a small emergency saving fund matters.

For the teacher: discussion and curriculum links

This activity supports the KS3 strand Living in the wider world: money, careers and economic wellbeing.

  • Needs vs wants: ask why rent, food and travel come before streaming or trainers.
  • Opportunity cost: every want bought is money not saved. What did Jordan give up?
  • Emergency saving: the surprise cost shows why a small buffer protects you from debt.
  • Discuss: what would change if Jordan's hours were cut, or a bill went up?
