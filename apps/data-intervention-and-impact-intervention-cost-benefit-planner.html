How the maths works (teacher and pupil notes)

Adjusted months = months of progress × evidence strength. Weak evidence discounts the headline gain, because an impressive number from one small cohort is less trustworthy than the same number from a large trial.

Cost per month of progress = total cost ÷ (adjusted months × pupils reached). This is the key value-for-money figure: it tells you how many pounds it takes to buy one month of progress for one pupil. Lower is better.

Why it matters: a cheap intervention is not automatically good value, and an expensive one is not automatically wasteful. A £20,000 programme that lifts 50 pupils by 5 months can be better value than a £2,000 one that lifts 4 pupils by 1 month. Ranking by cost per month of progress is the fair comparison.
