Information Processing Lab

KS5 Physical Education · Skill acquisition and motor learning. Route a sporting example through the Whiting and Welford information processing model, then run a reaction-time experiment to see how choice and stimulus number stretch decision time.

Teacher aim: students can label each stage of the information processing model with a concrete example, and can explain Hick's Law and the psychological refractory period using their own collected data.

Reaction-time experiment

Choose how many lamps are in play. With one lamp you measure simple reaction time. With more lamps you measure choice reaction time, where you must select the matching response. Hit the lit lamp as fast as you can.

Press Start, then hit the lamp the moment it lights.

Keyboard: each lamp is a button. Press Start, wait, then press Enter or Space on the lit lamp. Jumping early counts as a false start.
