Teacher notes and how to use this in a lesson
  • Curriculum link: KS3 Drama, vocal and physical performance skills. Pupils make deliberate choices about pitch, pace, projection and posture and explain the effect on an audience.
  • Starter: project a brief, ask the class to predict the four settings before anyone touches a slider, then test the prediction.
  • Vocabulary in play: pitch, tone, pace, tempo, projection, posture, status, gesture, gait. The feedback names the skill each time so pupils build the language.
  • Stretch: ask pupils to find a setting that scores above 85 percent, then perform the brief aloud in role for ten seconds with those exact qualities.
  • Discussion: two skills are flagged as the brief's signature focus. Why might pitch matter more for a nervous character than for a confident one?
