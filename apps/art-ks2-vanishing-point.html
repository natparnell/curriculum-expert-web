Drag a handle, or click it and use the ← → ↑ ↓ keys. The blue line is your eye level; the red dot is where the road disappears.

What is happening?

Start dragging. Move the red vanishing point and watch every building edge swing to follow it. That is the secret of one-point perspective.

Teacher and pupil notes

Key vocabulary: horizon line, eye level, vanishing point, receding lines (orthogonals), foreground, background, tone.

  • The horizon line sits at the viewer's eye level. Lower it and you look up at tall buildings; raise it and you look down the street.
  • In one-point perspective every line going away from us meets at one vanishing point.
  • Tone helps depth too: distant objects look paler and have softer edges (aerial perspective).
