Understanding the Carbon Cycle

The carbon cycle describes how carbon moves between four major stores: the atmosphere, oceans, land biosphere (plants and soil), and fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas). Fluxes are the flows of carbon between these stores.

Before industrialisation, the cycle was roughly in balance. Human activities (burning fossil fuels, deforestation) have added extra carbon to the atmosphere, increasing CO₂ from ~280 ppm to over 420 ppm.
