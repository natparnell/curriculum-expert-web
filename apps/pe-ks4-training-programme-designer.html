PE • KS4 • Physical training and fitness testing

Training Programme Designer

Teacher aim: pupils build a six-week periodised plan for a chosen performer, applying SPORT and FITT. The app checks every change live and flags overtraining, missing rest and progression errors, so misconceptions surface in the moment rather than at marking.

1. Design the programme

Pick the performer and goal, choose a training method per week, then set the weekly training load. Aim for gradual progression with recovery built in.

SPORT, FITT and the rules I am checked against

SPORT = Specificity, Progression, Overload, Reversibility, Tedium. FITT = Frequency, Intensity, Time, Type.

PrincipleWhat the app checks
ProgressionLoad should rise gradually through the build, not jump or stall.
Overload (safe)A week-on-week rise above ~25% is flagged as overtraining risk.
RecoveryAt least one lighter recovery or rest week (a taper) before competition.
ReversibilityA long stall or drop mid-cycle loses adaptation.
SpecificityThe method should match the goal (for example continuous running for aerobic endurance).

3. Fitness Test Bench

Match each fitness test to the component it measures, then read the protocol. Drag the slider to set how hard you would push, and the bench shows whether you are below, at or above a typical 16-year-old rating. Use this to justify your method choices above.
