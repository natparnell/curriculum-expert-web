Why do triangles win?

A frame structure is made of straight members joined at the corners. A four-sided shape (a square) can be pushed sideways into a parallelogram, so it is not rigid. A triangle cannot change shape unless a member bends or breaks, so it is rigid.

Adding a diagonal member is called triangulation. It splits each weak square into two strong triangles. Engineers also call extra diagonal members bracing. Real bridges, cranes, pylons and roof trusses are full of triangles for exactly this reason.
