Teacher and governor notes

  • The point score is one estimate, not the truth. Each cohort is a sample of how pupils could have performed; the confidence interval shows the plausible range for the school's underlying performance.
  • Small cohorts swing harder. One or two pupils with very different outcomes move a small school's average far more than a large school's, so the interval is wider. The width scales roughly with one over the square root of the cohort size.
  • Statistically significant here means the 95% interval sits entirely above zero (significantly above average) or entirely below zero (significantly below). If it crosses zero, treat the headline as broadly in line with average.
  • Figures are illustrative for teaching. They mirror how the DfE publishes Progress 8 with confidence intervals, but use a simplified model. All names and cohorts are fictional.
