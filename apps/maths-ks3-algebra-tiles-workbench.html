How algebra tiles work
  • A large square is x², a long rectangle is x, a small square is 1. Each has its own colour.
  • A dashed tile is the negative of that piece. A solid tile and the matching dashed tile make a zero pair: they cancel.
  • You can only add together tiles of the same kind (like terms). Different shapes stay separate.
  • To expand a(b + c) you tile out a rows of (b + c). To factorise you arrange all tiles into one tidy rectangle and read off its side lengths.
