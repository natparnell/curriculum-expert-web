How to read for meaning

Main points vs details. Read the whole text once for the gist before looking at the questions. Main-point questions reward the overall idea; detail questions need a precise word or number from the text.

True / False / Not stated. Mark a statement Not stated only when the text neither confirms nor contradicts it. A common trap is choosing False when the idea is simply absent.

Inference. Some answers are implied, not written. Use surrounding clues (reasons, contrasts, emotions) to decide what the writer means.

Tenses. Endings and auxiliaries signal time: French -ai/-as (future), imparfait -ais/-ait (past description), avoir/être + past participle (perfect); Spanish -é/-ás (future), -aba/-ía (imperfect), he/ha + participle (perfect).

Negatives. French ne...pas / ne...jamais / ne...plus and Spanish no / nunca / ya no reverse meaning. Miss the negative and you reverse the answer.

Connectives. Words such as mais/pero (but), parce que/porque (because) and cependant/sin embargo (however) show how ideas link and often flag a contrast the examiner tests.

Opinions. Look for phrases like je pense que / creo que, plus the adjective that follows, to identify whether a view is positive or negative.

Working out unknown words

Cognates. Many words resemble English (important, famille/familia). Trust the resemblance, but watch for false friends (actuellement = currently, not actually).

Word families. If you know travail (work) you can reach travailler (to work) and travailleur (worker).

Context. Use the rest of the sentence to narrow a meaning before you reach for a gloss. The click-to-gloss tool is here to confirm, not to replace, the strategy.

About this lab

Texts are authentic-style passages written for practice across the Edexcel GCSE themes: identity and culture, local area and travel, school and future study, and global issues. They are not extracts from a live paper.

Marking is automatic. Short-answer and translation tasks accept reasonable variations, so always read the model and feedback rather than relying on the tick alone.
