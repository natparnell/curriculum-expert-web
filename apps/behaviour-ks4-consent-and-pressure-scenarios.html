Consent and Pressure Scenarios

Teacher aim: pupils identify consent, recognise coercion and pressure, apply the law on age and capacity, and rehearse clear, respectful communication. Run as a whole-class projector activity or individually on tablets.

Part 1: Work through the situation

Read each moment and choose what you think is the right response. You get instant feedback on consent, pressure and the law. There is no penalty for thinking it through; choose, read, then move on.

The key points to keep

  • Consent must be freely given, informed, specific and ongoing. It can be withdrawn at any time.
  • Silence, going along with it, or being too scared to say no is not consent.
  • Pressure, threats, guilt-tripping and persistence are coercion, not romance.
  • In England the age of consent is 16. A person who is drunk, asleep or unable to understand cannot legally consent.
  • Contraception and condoms protect against pregnancy; only condoms also reduce the risk of most STIs.
  • Free, confidential advice is available from a GP, a sexual health clinic, or a trusted adult.
If something in this lesson worried you: you can talk to a member of pastoral staff, your GP, or a confidential service such as a young people's sexual health clinic. In a real situation, no one has the right to pressure you, and it is never too late to say no.
