How dictation is marked

Edexcel assesses each word for correct spelling, including accents. A missing or wrong accent is a spelling error, even if the word is otherwise right.

French: silent letters & liaison ›

French often writes letters it does not pronounce. Listen for the meaning, then spell the full written form.

  • Silent final consonants: petit, grand, vous parlez.
  • Liaison links words: les amis sounds like “lez-amis” but is two words.
  • Silent h: les heures, un homme.
  • Accents change sound: é (café) vs è (mère) vs à (à la maison).
Spanish: regular sound-spelling ›

Spanish is highly regular, but a few sounds map to more than one spelling.

  • Silent h: hola, hermano, ahora.
  • b and v sound the same: vivir, beber.
  • c/z and c/qu: cinco, zapato, queso.
  • The tilde matters: año (year) vs ano; written accents mark stress: está, mamá.
How to use this trainer ›
  • Play once at normal speed, then Replay or tick Slow it down.
  • Type the full sentence, then Check answer.
  • Green = correct, amber = right word but an accent slip, red = wrong or missing.
  • The English translation appears after you check.
About the audio: speech is generated by your browser’s built-in Web Speech API and depends on the French and Spanish voices installed on your device. If a target-language voice is missing, the trainer shows the written sentence instead so you can still practise spelling and accents.
