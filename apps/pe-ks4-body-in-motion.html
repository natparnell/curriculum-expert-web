Body in Motion

Teacher aim: pupils analyse a sporting movement to identify the working muscles, the antagonistic pairs that drive each joint action, and how the cardio-respiratory system supplies the oxygen those muscles need.

KS4 GCSE PE Applied anatomy & physiology Musculoskeletal system Antagonistic pairs Cardio-respiratory

1. Animate a movement

Pick a movement, then play it. The muscle that shortens to create the action (the agonist) turns red; its relaxing partner (the antagonist) turns blue. Click or tab to any muscle for its role.

Choose one of the four movements above to see which muscles do the work.

Keyboard: Tab to a muscle then Enter to read its role. Use the movement buttons with Space.

2. Cardio-respiratory walkthrough

Those working muscles need oxygen. Step through how blood carries oxygen from the lungs to the muscle and returns carbon dioxide. Follow the highlighted flow.

1. Lungs load oxygen 2. To the left heart 3. Out to the muscle 4. Gas exchange at muscle 5. Back to the right heart
Press Next to begin. You will trace one full circuit of double circulation as your muscle works during exercise.
