🎧 The dimensions of music

Composers shape music using these building blocks. Notice which ones you are changing as you compose and refine your piece.

🎹 Pitch

How high or low a note is. Higher notes sit at the top of the grid, lower notes at the bottom.

⏱ Duration

How long or short the sounds and silences are. Empty beats are rests (silence).

🔈 Dynamics

How loud or quiet the music is, from pp (very quiet) to ff (very loud).

⚡ Tempo

How fast or slow the beat goes, measured in beats per minute (bpm).

🎼 Timbre

The kind of sound, or tone colour. A music box and an organ play the same note differently.

🎹 Texture

How many sounds happen at once. One note per beat is thin; stacking notes makes it thicker.

🧩 Structure

The plan of the whole piece. Using A and B sections lets you build patterns like A B A.

How to compose and refine (open me)
  • Improvise first: tap a few notes, press Play, and just explore the sounds.
  • Make a melody: place one note per beat for a clear tune, or leave gaps for rests.
  • Build a Section B that feels different (higher, busier, or quieter) for contrast.
  • Structure it: choose A B A so your tune returns like a chorus.
  • Refine: Play, listen, then change notes, tempo or dynamics to make it better.
  • Keyboard: use Arrow keys to move around the grid, Enter or Space to place a note.

💡 Composing prompts

Pick a challenge, compose it on the grid, then refine until you are happy with it.

  1. Compose a calm, gentle tune for a sleepy bedtime. Use a slow tempo and quiet dynamics.
  2. Compose a bouncy, busy tune for a playground. Use a fast tempo and a clear rhythm.
  3. Make Section A go up like climbing stairs, and Section B come back down. Then play A B A.
  4. Use only three beats in Section B so it feels like an echo or a question.
  5. Change just the timbre and listen: does the same tune feel different on a music box and an organ?
