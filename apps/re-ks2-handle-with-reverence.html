Handle With Reverence

Religious Education • Key Stage 2 • Sacred texts: structure, authority and interpretation

Teaching aim: Pupils explore how four holy books are stored, carried and read, and discover that believers treat their sacred texts with great care and respect (reverence). Drag each care practice to the book it belongs with.

A sacred text is a book that a faith treats as holy and full of authority. Believers do not just keep these books on any old shelf. They store them, carry them and read them in special, careful ways. Match each care practice below to the right holy book. Some books share practices, so read the clues on each card.

The big idea

Look across all four books. Every faith treats its holy book with reverence: clean hands, a special place to keep it, careful carrying and respectful reading. The exact practices are different, but the deep respect is shared.
