Heart and Blood Vessels

Trace the double circulation, label the vessels, then drive cardiac output by changing heart rate and stroke volume.

Teacher aim: Pupils describe the path of blood through the double circulation, identify the four chambers and main vessels, and apply cardiac output = heart rate × stroke volume to explain how the body raises blood flow during exercise.

Interactive heart and circulation

Tap a vessel or chamber on the diagram, or use the keyboard (Tab to move, Enter to select). The beating animation speed follows your heart rate slider.

Select a part of the diagram to read about it. Notice the left side of the heart (your right as you look) pumps oxygenated blood to the body; the right side pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs.
