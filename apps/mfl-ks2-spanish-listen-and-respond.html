MFL · KS2 · National Curriculum · Spanish: Listening

Spanish: Listen & Respond

Press to hear a Spanish word spoken aloud, then choose the picture or English meaning that matches. Listen, respond and tune your ear to the sounds of Spanish.

Teaching aim: children listen attentively to spoken Spanish and show their understanding by responding, building a foundation of vocabulary across familiar topics and appreciating the sounds of the language.

▸ How to play and what it teaches

This is a listening game for Key Stage 2 Spanish. Choose a topic, press Hear the word and listen carefully. Then tap the picture or the English word that matches what you heard.

How to play

  • Press Hear the word to listen to the Spanish.
  • Use Hear it again to replay, or Slow to hear it spoken slowly.
  • Tap the matching answer. You will get cheerful feedback and a point for every correct answer.
  • Switch between Pictures and English words as your answer choices.
  • Tap any item in the Vocabulary panel to hear it pronounced and train your ear.

Curriculum links (KS2 Languages)

  • Listen attentively to spoken language and show understanding by responding.
  • Explore the patterns and sounds of language through songs, rhymes and speech.
  • Develop accurate pronunciation by listening to a native-style voice.
  • Broaden vocabulary across familiar topics: greetings, numbers, colours, animals, classroom objects, food and family.

About the spoken voice

The Spanish is spoken using your device's built-in speech voices. If no Spanish voice is installed, the game still works: the written Spanish word is shown on screen with a friendly note, so children can read and respond.
