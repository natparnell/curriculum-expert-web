🧪 Test It! Fair Test Maker

Teacher aim: Pupils plan a simple comparative test by choosing one thing to change and one thing to measure, and keeping everything else the same. Working scientifically: asking questions and carrying out a fair test.

1Our question
2What to change
3What to measure
4Keep it fair
5Run the test

Step 1: Choose our question

Scientists start with a question. Pick the one our class wants to test today.

🧻Which paper towel soaks up the most water?
⚽Which ball rolls the furthest?
🧥Which coat keeps us warmest?
For this demonstration we will test the paper towels. The other questions show the same idea.

Step 2: Pick ONE thing to change

Our question: Which paper towel soaks up the most water?

In a fair test we change just one thing. Tap the one thing we will change each time we test.

🧻The kind of paper towel
💧How much water we use
⏱How long we wait

Step 3: Pick ONE thing to measure

We are changing: the kind of paper towel

Now choose what we will look at to find the winner. This is what we measure.

📏How much water it soaks up
🎨What colour the towel is
😊Which one we like best

Step 4: Keep everything else the same

A test is only fair if everything else stays the same. For each thing, decide: do we change it, or keep it the same?

We change this

🧻 The kind of paper towel

Each towel is different. That is the one thing we change.

We keep these the same

    Set each one. Tap Change or Same.

      Step 5: Run the test and read the results

      Same amount of water on each towel. Same wait. Only the towel is different. Tap to pour and watch what each one soaks up.
