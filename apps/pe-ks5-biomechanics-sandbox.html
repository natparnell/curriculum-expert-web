Governing factors and the exam-board mechanics (open for notes)

Projectile motion in A Level PE is analysed by splitting release velocity into horizontal and vertical components:

  • Horizontal: vₓ = v · cos(θ). No horizontal force in the ideal model, so horizontal velocity stays roughly constant. This carries the projectile forward.
  • Vertical: vᵉ = v · sin(θ). Gravity (g = 9.81 m/s²) decelerates the rise, then accelerates the fall, giving the symmetrical parabola.
  • Angle of release: a higher angle puts more velocity into the vertical, raising peak height and flight time but shortening range past the optimum. In a vacuum the optimum is 45°; real air resistance lowers it.
  • Velocity of release: the strongest lever. Range scales with the square of release speed, so technique that adds speed pays off disproportionately.
  • Height of release: releasing above the landing surface (shot put, javelin) adds flight time on the way down, so the optimum angle drops below 45° and range increases.

The three other forces in the wider topic (weight, air resistance and the lift/Magnus effect from fluid mechanics) explain why a real shot, discus or football deviates from the ideal parabola. This sandbox isolates the four release variables so the cause of each change is visible.
