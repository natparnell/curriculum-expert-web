Theodicy Tester

KS5 Religious Education · The problem of evil and theodicies

Teacher aim: Pupils stress-test three theodicies (Hick's soul-making, Plantinga's free-will defence, process theology) against natural and moral evil, judging which claims survive, strain or break.

Step 1  Choose a case of evil to throw at a theodicy

Pick one case. Natural evil arises without a human agent; moral evil flows from a free human choice. The harder the case, the more pressure it puts on a theodicy.

Step 2  Choose a theodicy to defend

Each theodicy makes a different set of claims about why an all-good, all-powerful God permits evil. You are about to put those claims under load.

Select a case and a theodicy to enable the test.
