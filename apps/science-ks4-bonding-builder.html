Bonding Builder ⚛️

Build ionic, covalent and metallic bonds by moving electrons, draw the dot-and-cross diagram, then predict the properties.

Teacher aim: Pupils model the transfer or sharing of outer electrons, explain why a substance forms its structure, and link that structure to melting point, conductivity and solubility (AQA/Edexcel/OCR Chemistry: Bonding, structure and the properties of matter).

Step 1: Choose two elements

The pairs below are chosen so their outer shells fit together neatly. The badge tells you whether each element is a metal or a non-metal, which decides the type of bond.

Step 2: Move the outer electrons

Drag each blue electron to an empty dotted slot. Metals lose electrons to non-metals (ionic); two non-metals share a pair in the overlap (covalent). Each atom is happy when its outer shell is full (8, or 2 for the small shells).

Make your moves, then press Check my bond.
Move every outer electron into a valid position so both atoms have a full outer shell.

Step 3: Predict the properties

You built a bond. Now decide how the whole substance behaves. Choose the best answer for each property; you get instant feedback and the reason why.
