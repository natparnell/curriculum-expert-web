Teacher and pupil notes: how the model works
  • Income tax is charged on earnings above a tax-free personal allowance (here taken as £12,570). Move the dial and Priya's deduction changes.
  • National Insurance is a second deduction on earnings. It is not the same as income tax, but it also funds public services such as the NHS and the State Pension.
  • VAT is a tax on spending, not earning. It is the same rate for everyone, so it takes a bigger share of a low earner's budget. Economists call this regressive.
  • Deficit means spending more than you raise. Government borrows to cover the gap, and borrowing has to be paid back with interest.
  • Trade-offs: cutting taxes is popular but leaves less to spend; funding every service fully needs high taxes, which is unpopular. There is no answer with no downside. That is the point.
  • All figures are simplified teaching estimates, not the exact UK accounts. They are designed to show how the levers interact.
