Constructions & Loci Lab

Teaching aim: use a virtual pair of compasses and a straightedge to perform the standard ruler-and-compass constructions, watching the construction arcs build step by step, then explore how each construction defines a locus: the set of points obeying a rule. Combine two or more loci to find the region that satisfies a real scenario.

Drawing board

Pick a construction and press Play to watch the arcs build.

Tip: drag points A, B and any markers to reshape the figure. The faint amber arcs are the construction marks a student would leave in with compasses.

Construction

Step 0 Press Play to begin the construction, or Step to advance one stage at a time.
Why it works Every standard construction relies on the fact that two equal radii sweep out points equidistant from their centres. Where two such arcs cross, the crossing point is equidistant from both starting points, which is exactly what the construction needs.

Loci to display

Scenario: siting a phone mast A new mast must be within 3 cm of building A (signal range) and nearer to road L1 than road L2 (access). Drag A, B and the road endpoints, then press Shade combined region to reveal the permitted zone in pink. Adjust the range slider to see the zone grow or shrink.
