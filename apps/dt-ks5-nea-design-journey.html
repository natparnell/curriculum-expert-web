NEA Design Journey

A-level Design and Technology: scaffolding the iterative design and make project

Teacher aim: students work through the substantial design and make task (NEA), capturing evidence as they explore a context, research a client, iterate prototypes against criteria, test for performance and judge commercial viability.
KS5 / A-level Iterative design Evidence-led Commercial viability
AO1 Identifying and investigating design possibilities

Stage 1: Explore the context

Turn a broad context into a defensible design opportunity. Examiners reward a brief that names a real user, a genuine need and a measurable outcome, not a vague wish.

Set context (demonstration): "Independence in the kitchen for people with limited hand strength." Frame a design opportunity within it.

Avoid "everyone" or "people". Name the user group and the constraint they live with.

A strong brief is measurable and testable. Include a quantity, unit or standard if you can (force, time, dimension, cost).

AO1 Investigating needs and producing a specification

Stage 2: Research the client

Primary and secondary research must lead somewhere. Sort each finding into the design specification point it justifies, then judge whether your evidence base is balanced.

Drag, or use the arrow keys after selecting, to move each research finding to a specification heading. Keyboard: focus a finding, press Enter to pick up, then 1 to 4 to drop it.

    AO2 Designing and making prototypes / iterative development

    Stage 3: Iterate your prototypes

    Iteration is not a list of ideas; it is a chain of decisions. Each prototype should respond to what the previous one revealed. Log a cycle and the app checks whether your "what changed and why" actually closes the loop.
