Process vocabulary (assessment objective language)
Glaze
A thin, translucent layer of colour laid over a dried layer so light passes through and bounces back, giving luminous depth. Tone is built by layering, not by mixing on the palette.
Scumble
An opaque or semi-opaque layer dragged thinly over a darker one, broken so the layer below shows through. The opposite logic to a glaze.
Intaglio
Printmaking where the image sits in lines bitten or cut below the plate surface; ink is held in those recesses and pressed into damp paper.
Bite
The action of acid (or mordant) cutting the exposed metal in etching. Longer bites cut deeper lines that hold more ink and print darker.
Aquatint
A resin ground that lets the plate hold areas of tone, not just line, by creating a fine pitted surface that traps ink.
Ground / support
The prepared surface a work is built on. Its absorbency and tooth change how every later layer behaves.
