Teacher notes and the maths behind the model

What students should take away

Stress σ = F / A (force over original cross-section area). Strain ε = ΔL / L₀ (extension over original gauge length), and is dimensionless. In the straight elastic region, σ = E ε, so Young's modulus E is the gradient. The yield point marks where permanent (plastic) deformation begins; the ultimate tensile strength is the highest stress the material reaches before necking and fracture.

Factor of safety

Working stress comes from the design load over the chosen area. Factor of safety FoS = σlimit / σworking, where the limit is yield (no permanent set) or ultimate (collapse), depending on the failure mode you care about. Brittle materials (GRP, CFRP) have little or no yield plateau, so designers usually base FoS on ultimate strength and use a larger required value.

Model and limits

Curves are idealised piecewise models for teaching, not measured datasheet values. Mild steel shows a clear yield drop and long plastic region; the alloy yields gradually; GRP and CFRP stay almost linear then snap with negligible plasticity. Use the auto-pull button on a projector, or let students drag the load slider and read the live phase. All values are demonstration figures only.
