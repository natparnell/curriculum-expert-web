      How to use this app, and how the marks really work

      The walkthrough

      Pick a paper at the top, then click any question on the exam-paper map. The panel on the right shows what the question is testing, how many marks it carries, a sensible time budget, the assessment objective behind it and a worked approach against an original source text written for this app. Model-answer snippets and a quick-check question let you test understanding before you move on.

      Why timing matters

      A common mistake is over-writing the low-tariff questions and running out of time on Question 4 and Question 5, which together carry the heaviest marks. Use the suggested timings as a budget: roughly one minute per mark on reading, plus dedicated planning time before each Section B answer.

      What examiners reward in Q5

      For AO5 they want a clear, controlled purpose, audience and form, deliberate structure (a planned opening, development and ending, not a brain-dump) and crafted ideas. For AO6 they want ambitious, precise vocabulary, a deliberate range of sentence forms (short for impact, complex for nuance) and accurate punctuation including commas, semicolons, colons and dashes used on purpose. Devices help, but only when they earn their place. The toolkit's self-check mirrors these criteria.
