# Key Publications in Geography Education

## Introduction

This document provides an overview of the **essential publications** that have shaped geography education in England. These texts represent the accumulated wisdom of the geography education community, offering guidance on curriculum, pedagogy, assessment, and the nature of geographical knowledge. For geography teachers and curriculum leaders, familiarity with these publications provides a foundation for informed practice and professional development.

## Foundational Texts

### Teaching Geography Journal

The Geographical Association's *Teaching Geography* is the premier professional journal for geography educators in England. Published quarterly, it features:
- Practical classroom ideas and strategies
- Curriculum development case studies
- Reviews of research and its implications for practice
- Discussion of policy developments and their impact
- Regular columns on assessment, fieldwork, and resources

**Why it matters**: *Teaching Geography* represents the collective wisdom of the geography education community, connecting academic research to classroom practice.

### Primary Geography Magazine

Also published by the Geographical Association, *Primary Geography* supports geography teaching in the primary phase:
- Activity ideas for EYFS, Key Stage 1 and Key Stage 2
- Guidance on curriculum planning in primary settings
- Case studies from primary classrooms
- Resources for non-specialist primary teachers

**Why it matters**: Primary geography often lacks specialist support; this publication helps generalist primary teachers deliver effective geography.

### Geography Journal (RGS/GA)

The Royal Geographical Society's *Geography* journal publishes academic research on geographical education:
- Research articles on teaching and learning geography
- International perspectives on geography education
- Theoretical developments in the field
- Reviews of curriculum and policy developments

**Why it matters**: Provides access to research evidence that can inform practice and professional reflection.

## Curriculum and Pedagogy Guidance

### The GA's Curriculum Making Resources

The Geographical Association has published extensive guidance on curriculum development:
- *A Framework for the School Geography Curriculum* (GA, 2023)
- *Primary Geography Handbook* (various editions)
- Curriculum Making website and supporting materials
- Subject leadership guidance

**Why they matter**: These resources provide practical support for curriculum leaders seeking to develop coherent, engaging geography curricula.

### Learning to Teach Geography

The standard text for initial teacher education:
- Biddulph, M., Lambert, D. and Balderstone, D. *Learning to Teach Geography in the Secondary School* (4th edn, Routledge, 2021)

Covers all aspects of geography teaching from curriculum planning to assessment, providing comprehensive guidance for new geography teachers.

**Why it matters**: The standard reference for trainee geography teachers and those new to the profession.

### Teaching Geography Creatively

Scoffham, S. (ed.) *Teaching Geography Creatively* (3rd edn, Routledge, 2023)

Explores creative approaches to geography teaching, including:
- Story and narrative in geography
- Using visual media and technology
- Drama and role-play
- Creative writing and geographical imagination

**Why it matters**: Offers approaches to making geography engaging and memorable through creative pedagogy.

## Assessment and Examination

### GA Assessment Guidance

The Geographical Association has produced extensive guidance on assessment without levels:
- Assessment frameworks and progression models
- Guidance on formative assessment in geography
- Case studies of school-based assessment approaches
- Materials for assessment moderation

**Why they matter**: Provide support for schools developing their assessment practices following the removal of National Curriculum levels.

### Exam Board Specifications and Support Materials

The major exam boards (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, Eduqas) provide:
- Detailed specifications for GCSE and A Level
- Sample assessment materials and mark schemes
- Teacher guidance and resource lists
- Training and professional development

**Why they matter**: Essential for teachers preparing students for public examinations.

## Subject Knowledge Development

### Philip's Atlases

Philip's publish a range of atlases widely used in schools:
- *Philip's Modern School Atlas*
- *Philip's RGS Student Atlas*
- Digital atlas resources

**Why they matter**: Quality atlases remain essential tools for developing locational knowledge and spatial understanding.

### Geography Subject Knowledge Books

Various publishers produce subject knowledge books for teachers:
- *The Geography Teachers' Handbook* (various)
- Subject knowledge guides for specific topics (coasts, rivers, urban geography, etc.)
- Case study collections

**Why they matter**: Support non-specialist teachers and provide depth for specialists teaching outside their areas of expertise.

## Fieldwork and Outdoor Learning

### GA Fieldwork Guidance

The Geographical Association publishes extensive fieldwork resources:
- *A Guide to Fieldwork in Geography*
- Risk assessment guidance
- Fieldwork activity ideas by key stage
- Digital fieldwork resources

**Why they matter**: Support teachers in planning and conducting safe, educationally valuable fieldwork.

### Ordnance Survey Resources

Ordnance Survey provides educational resources:
- Digimap for Schools (digital mapping service)
- Map reading guides
- Teaching resources using OS maps
- Educational licensing for map use

**Why they matter**: OS maps are the standard for UK geography; these resources support their effective educational use.

## Historical Perspectives

### The School Geography Tradition

Understanding the history of geography education provides context for current practice:
- Walford, R. (ed.) *Geography and Education: National and International Perspectives* (1996)
- Various historical accounts of geography teaching

**Why they matter**: Understanding how geography education has developed helps teachers make sense of current curriculum debates.

## Digital and Online Resources

### GA Website (www.geography.org.uk)

The Geographical Association's website provides:
- Teaching resources and lesson ideas
- Curriculum guidance and policy updates
- Professional development opportunities
- Links to further resources and organisations

**Why it matters**: The primary hub for geography education in England, providing up-to-date resources and information.

### RGS Geography Teaching Resources

The Royal Geographical Society provides:
- Teaching resources for various topics
- Discovering Britain series
- Research-informed teaching materials
- Award and grant information

**Why they matter**: Connects school geography to the wider geographical community and current research.

## Keeping Up to Date

### Ofsted Publications

Ofsted's subject-specific publications inform understanding of inspection:
- Research review series: geography (2021)
- Subject report: geography (2023)
- Various case studies and good practice examples

**Why they matter**: Provide insight into inspection expectations and national patterns of quality in geography education.

### Government Publications

Key government documents include:
- National Curriculum programmes of study
- GCSE and A Level subject criteria (Ofqual)
- Policy statements on curriculum and assessment

**Why they matter**: Define the statutory requirements for geography education.

## Building a Professional Library

### Priorities for Different Roles

**For trainee teachers**:
- *Learning to Teach Geography in the Secondary School*
- *Teaching Geography* journal subscription
- Access to a quality atlas

**For primary teachers**:
- *Primary Geography* magazine
- *Primary Geography Handbook*
- Early years geography guidance

**For subject leaders**:
- Full range of GA curriculum resources
- Assessment guidance materials
- Subject knowledge development books

**For all teachers**:
- *Teaching Geography* subscription
- Regular access to updated atlases
- Awareness of current Ofsted and policy publications

## Conclusion

The publications outlined above represent the accumulated knowledge of the geography education community. While no teacher can engage with everything, familiarity with key texts—particularly the GA's professional journals and curriculum resources—provides a foundation for informed, reflective practice. In a subject where curriculum and policy continue to evolve, ongoing engagement with professional publications remains essential for maintaining and developing expertise.
