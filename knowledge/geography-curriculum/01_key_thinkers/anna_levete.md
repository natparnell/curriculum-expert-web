# Anna Levete: Champion of Primary Geography Education

## Biographical Overview

Anna Levete is a distinguished figure in UK primary geography education whose career has been dedicated to supporting teachers, developing curriculum resources, and advocating for the importance of geography in the primary curriculum. Through her extensive work as an author, consultant, and former member of the Geographical Association's Primary Committee, Levete has made significant contributions to the theory and practice of primary geography teaching. Her publications have reached thousands of primary teachers, providing accessible guidance on implementing the National Curriculum and developing engaging geographical learning experiences for young children.

Levete's work is characterised by its practical orientation, pedagogical insight, and commitment to making geography accessible and meaningful for primary-aged children. She has written extensively for teachers, teacher educators, and children themselves, producing resources that span professional development materials, curriculum guidance, and children's non-fiction books. Her influence on primary geography education is evident in the widespread use of her publications in schools and initial teacher education programmes across the UK.

## Key Publications and Intellectual Contributions

### The Primary Geography Handbook (2004)

Levete's most significant contribution to geography education is undoubtedly *The Primary Geography Handbook* (2004), published by the Geographical Association. This comprehensive guide has become an essential reference for primary teachers seeking to understand and implement the geography curriculum. The handbook provides:

- **Clear explanation of the National Curriculum requirements**, breaking down the programmes of study into accessible language for non-specialist teachers
- **Practical guidance on long-term, medium-term, and short-term planning**, including examples of effective geography curricula
- **Detailed exploration of the key concepts in primary geography**, including place, space, scale, environment, and sustainability
- **Guidance on developing geographical skills**, from map work and fieldwork to the use of information and communication technology
- **Case studies of good practice** from primary schools across the UK
- **Assessment strategies** appropriate for primary geography
- **Cross-curricular links**, showing how geography connects to other subjects including literacy, mathematics, and citizenship

The handbook has been praised for its accessibility and practical utility. Writing in the journal *Primary Geography*, reviewers noted that the handbook "provides a comprehensive and accessible introduction to primary geography for teachers at all levels of experience." The book has been widely adopted by initial teacher education programmes and continues to be recommended to trainee teachers as an essential text.

Levete's approach in the handbook is distinctive in its recognition that many primary teachers lack confidence in teaching geography, often having had limited exposure to the subject in their own education. The handbook addresses this by providing clear explanations of geographical concepts, practical teaching ideas, and reassurance that effective primary geography does not require specialist expertise but rather curiosity, enthusiasm, and willingness to explore the world with children.

### Children's Non-Fiction Books

Levete has authored numerous children's books that introduce geographical concepts to young readers. Her "Mapping the World" series, published by Franklin Watts, uses engaging text and high-quality visual materials to help children understand maps, mapping, and spatial thinking. Titles in this series include:

- *Mapping Our World*
- *Mapping Our Local Area*
- *Mapping the Environment*

These books are notable for their accessibility and their connection to the primary curriculum. They provide teachers with resources for teaching map skills while also engaging children directly with geographical content.

Levete has also written extensively on environmental themes, producing books that help children understand environmental issues and sustainability. Her "Energy Forever?" series explores renewable energy sources, while other titles address topics such as recycling, conservation, and environmental change. These books connect to the geography curriculum's emphasis on environmental understanding and sustainable development.

### Professional Development Resources

Beyond her major publications, Levete has contributed numerous articles and resources to professional journals, particularly the Geographical Association's *Primary Geography* magazine. Her articles have addressed topics including:

- **Developing enquiry-based geography in the primary classroom**
- **Using stories and literature to teach geography**
- **Making local fieldwork meaningful for young children**
- **Assessment in primary geography**
- **Differentiation strategies for mixed-ability geography classes**

These contributions have provided ongoing support for practising teachers, offering practical ideas and inspiration for classroom practice.

## Research Themes and Scholarly Focus

### The Nature of Primary Geography

Throughout her work, Levete has been concerned with defining the distinctive contribution of primary geography and advocating for its place in the curriculum. She argues that primary geography should:

- **Start from children's own experiences and gradually extend their horizons**, moving from the familiar to the unfamiliar
- **Be enquiry-based**, engaging children in asking and answering geographical questions
- **Be active and experiential**, involving children in exploration, investigation, and fieldwork
- **Develop geographical concepts**, including place, space, scale, environment, and sustainability
- **Connect to real-world issues**, helping children understand their place in the world and their responsibilities as global citizens

Levete's vision of primary geography is one that is rigorous and challenging while remaining accessible and engaging for young children. She rejects approaches that reduce geography to a collection of facts about distant places, advocating instead for a geography education that develops children's thinking skills, their sense of place, and their understanding of environmental and social issues.

### Supporting Non-Specialist Teachers

A recurring theme in Levete's work is the need to support non-specialist teachers in teaching geography. Recognising that many primary teachers lack confidence in geography, she has consistently sought to:

- **Demystify geographical concepts**, explaining them in accessible language
- **Provide practical teaching ideas** that can be implemented without specialist equipment or expertise
- **Offer reassurance** that enthusiasm and curiosity are more important than extensive subject knowledge
- **Model good practice** through case studies and examples
- **Provide subject knowledge support**, helping teachers develop their own geographical understanding

This focus on teacher support reflects Levete's understanding of the realities of primary teaching, where teachers are expected to teach across the curriculum regardless of their specialisms. Her work acknowledges these challenges while providing practical strategies for overcoming them.

### Enquiry-Based Learning

Levete is a strong advocate for enquiry-based approaches to primary geography. She argues that children learn geography best when they are actively engaged in asking and answering geographical questions, rather than passively receiving information. Her publications provide:

- **Frameworks for geographical enquiry**, including the enquiry process (ask, investigate, analyse, communicate)
- **Examples of enquiry questions** appropriate for different age groups
- **Guidance on planning enquiry-based units**, from single lessons to extended projects
- **Strategies for managing enquiry-based learning**, including organisation, resources, and assessment

This emphasis on enquiry connects to broader developments in primary education, including the rise of topic-based and cross-curricular approaches, while maintaining geographical rigour.

### The Importance of Fieldwork

Levete has consistently emphasised the importance of fieldwork in primary geography. She argues that direct experience of the environment is essential for developing geographical understanding and enthusiasm. Her work provides:

- **Guidance on planning local fieldwork** that is safe, manageable, and educationally valuable
- **Ideas for developing children's observational skills** in the local environment
- **Strategies for using the school grounds** as a resource for geographical learning
- **Suggestions for virtual fieldwork** and the use of secondary sources to extend beyond the local area

Levete's approach to fieldwork recognises the constraints facing primary schools (time, cost, safety concerns) while arguing that even limited fieldwork experiences are valuable. She advocates for "micro-fieldwork"—short, frequent observations of the school environment—as well as more extended visits.

## Pedagogical Philosophy and Approach

### Child-Centred Geography

Levete's pedagogical approach is fundamentally child-centred. She believes that effective primary geography must:

- **Start from children's own experiences and perspectives**
- **Build on children's natural curiosity** about the world
- **Engage children actively** in geographical enquiry
- **Respect children's thinking** and allow them to develop their own geographical questions
- **Make connections** to children's lives, interests, and concerns

This child-centred approach is evident in her writing, which consistently emphasises the importance of listening to children, observing their responses, and adapting teaching accordingly.

### Integration with the Wider Curriculum

Levete recognises that primary geography does not exist in isolation but is part of a broader curriculum. Her work explores connections between geography and:

- **Literacy**, including the use of stories, poetry, and non-fiction texts to teach geography
- **Mathematics**, including the use of data handling, measurement, and scale
- **Science**, particularly in relation to environmental understanding and the study of the natural world
- **Citizenship and PSHE**, in relation to global citizenship, sustainable development, and social justice
- **History**, in exploring the geographical dimensions of historical change
- **Art and Design**, in representing and interpreting places

This integrated approach reflects the realities of primary teaching, where subjects are often taught through topics or themes, while ensuring that geographical learning remains distinct and rigorous.

### Development of Geographical Thinking

While practical and accessible, Levete's work is also intellectually ambitious. She seeks to develop children's geographical thinking, not merely their geographical knowledge. This involves:

- **Developing spatial thinking**, including the ability to use and interpret maps and diagrams
- **Fostering environmental awareness**, including understanding of environmental issues and sustainability
- **Cultivating global citizenship**, including understanding of interdependence and cultural diversity
- **Building enquiry skills**, including the ability to ask geographical questions and investigate them systematically
- **Developing critical thinking**, including the ability to evaluate sources and consider different perspectives

This focus on thinking skills aligns with the Geographical Association's vision of "geographical capability"—the ability to think geographically and apply geographical understanding to new situations.

## Influence and Legacy

### Impact on Initial Teacher Education

Levete's work has had a significant impact on initial teacher education for primary teachers. Her handbook is widely used in university-based and school-based teacher training programmes, providing trainee teachers with:

- A comprehensive introduction to primary geography
- Practical guidance for planning and teaching
- Subject knowledge support
- Reassurance and confidence-building for non-specialist teachers

Many geography teacher educators cite Levete's work as a key resource in their teaching, and trainee teachers report that her practical approach has helped them develop confidence in teaching geography.

### Contribution to the Geographical Association

As a former member of the Geographical Association's Primary Committee, Levete has contributed to the development of national policy and guidance for primary geography. Her work with the GA has included:

- Contributing to the development of curriculum resources
- Writing for *Primary Geography* magazine
- Presenting at conferences and professional development events
- Supporting the development of the Primary Geography Quality Mark

Through these contributions, she has helped to shape the direction of primary geography education in the UK.

### Influence on Curriculum Development

Levete's work has influenced curriculum development in primary schools across the UK. Her emphasis on enquiry-based learning, fieldwork, and the development of geographical thinking has informed the design of many primary geography curricula. Schools using her handbook report that it has helped them to:

- Develop coherent geography curricula
- Improve the quality of geography teaching
- Increase teacher confidence in teaching geography
- Enhance children's geographical learning

### Longevity and Continuing Relevance

First published in 2004, *The Primary Geography Handbook* remains relevant despite changes to the National Curriculum. The principles Levete articulates—enquiry-based learning, fieldwork, the development of geographical thinking—continue to underpin effective primary geography. While specific curriculum requirements have changed, the pedagogical approaches she advocates remain sound.

This longevity is a testament to the quality of her work and its foundation in enduring principles of effective primary education. Rather than being tied to specific curriculum content, Levete's work focuses on how children learn geography and how teachers can support that learning—insights that remain valuable regardless of curriculum changes.

## Critical Reception and Ongoing Debates

### Balancing Accessibility and Rigor

One of the challenges of Levete's work is the balance between accessibility for non-specialist teachers and the intellectual rigour of geographical education. While widely praised for its practicality, some critics have questioned whether the handbook sufficiently develops teachers' own geographical understanding. Levete would likely respond that the handbook is intended as a starting point, with teachers encouraged to develop their expertise through further reading, professional development, and classroom experience.

### The Place of Knowledge in Primary Geography

Levete's emphasis on enquiry skills and geographical thinking has sometimes been contrasted with approaches that prioritise geographical knowledge. In the context of debates about knowledge-rich curricula, some have questioned whether enquiry-based approaches provide sufficient content. Levete's work suggests that knowledge and skills are not in opposition—enquiry requires knowledge, and knowledge is most effectively developed through enquiry.

### The Challenge of Curriculum Time

Levete's vision of rich, enquiry-based geography with extensive fieldwork faces the challenge of limited curriculum time in primary schools. With increasing pressure on time and accountability measures focused on core subjects, geography (along with other foundation subjects) often receives limited attention. Levete's work provides guidance for making the most of limited time, but the broader structural challenge remains.

## Implications for Classroom Practice

### Planning for Geography

Levete's work offers several principles for planning primary geography:

**Start with Enquiry Questions:**
Frame units around compelling geographical questions that engage children's curiosity. Examples might include: "Why does our local area look the way it does?" "How is life in a village in India different from life in our town?" "What happens to our rubbish?"

**Build in Fieldwork:**
Plan regular opportunities for children to observe and explore their environment. This might include:
- Walks around the local area
- Observation of weather and seasons
- Study of the school grounds
- Virtual exploration of distant places

**Develop Geographical Skills Progressively:**
Plan for the progressive development of skills including:
- Map skills (from plans and aerial photos to Ordnance Survey maps)
- Observational skills
- Data collection and presentation
- Use of geographical vocabulary

**Make Connections:**
Plan for connections within geography (between different topics) and across the curriculum (with other subjects). Show children how geographical knowledge and skills are relevant beyond the geography lesson.

### Teaching Strategies

From Levete's work, several effective teaching strategies emerge:

**Using Stories:**
Use stories, both fiction and non-fiction, to engage children with geographical content. Stories can provide context for understanding places, introduce environmental issues, and develop empathy with people in different situations.

**Handling Artefacts:**
Use objects and artefacts to stimulate interest and develop observational skills. Objects from different places can help children think about how people live in different environments.

**Visual Resources:**
Use high-quality photographs, videos, and satellite images to bring distant places into the classroom. Encourage children to observe carefully and ask questions about what they see.

**Talk and Discussion:**
Provide opportunities for children to discuss geographical ideas, explain their thinking, and listen to different perspectives. Geographical understanding develops through dialogue.

**Recording and Communicating:**
Encourage children to record and communicate their geographical learning in various ways—through writing, drawing, mapping, drama, and digital media. The process of communication helps consolidate learning.

### Assessment for Learning

Levete's approach to assessment emphasises:

- **Ongoing observation** of children's geographical thinking and learning
- **Listening to children's talk** to understand their developing understanding
- **Reviewing children's work** to see how they apply geographical skills
- **Asking questions** that reveal children's thinking
- **Providing feedback** that moves learning forward

Assessment is seen not as an end in itself but as a tool for supporting teaching and learning. Teachers should use assessment to identify what children understand and where they need further support.

## Conclusion

Anna Levete's contributions to primary geography education have been substantial and enduring. Through her handbook, children's books, and professional development resources, she has supported thousands of teachers in developing their practice and has enriched the geographical education of countless children.

Her work is characterised by its practicality, accessibility, and commitment to making geography meaningful for young children. She has shown that primary geography can be rigorous and challenging while remaining engaging and accessible—that enquiry, fieldwork, and the development of geographical thinking are not luxuries but essentials of effective geography education.

For contemporary primary educators, Levete's work remains a valuable resource. While curriculum requirements have evolved, the principles she articulates—starting from children's experience, engaging them in enquiry, developing their geographical thinking, and supporting their growth as global citizens—continue to underpin effective primary geography. Her legacy is evident in the many primary classrooms where geography is taught with enthusiasm and rigour, and in the many teachers whose confidence in teaching geography has been supported by her work.

As primary geography continues to navigate the challenges of curriculum time, accountability pressures, and the need to prepare children for an increasingly complex and interconnected world, Levete's vision of geography education—practical, engaging, and intellectually ambitious—offers a compelling model for the future.
