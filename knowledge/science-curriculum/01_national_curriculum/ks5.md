# Key Stage 5 Science: A Level Overview

This document provides a comprehensive overview of Key Stage 5 science education in England, covering A Level Biology, Chemistry, and Physics. It addresses the statutory subject criteria established by the Department for Education (DfE) and the regulatory requirements set by Ofqual, including the practical endorsement that forms a compulsory component of all A Level science qualifications.

## 1. Regulatory Framework and Subject Criteria

### 1.1 DfE Subject Content Requirements (2014)

The current A Level science specifications, first taught from September 2015, are built upon the GCE AS and A Level Subject Content for Biology, Chemistry and Physics published by the Department for Education in April 2014. This document establishes the minimum knowledge, understanding, and skills required for all AS and A Level specifications in these subjects, regardless of awarding body.

The DfE subject content is organised to ensure that all students develop:

- **Subject knowledge**: A deep understanding of core concepts, principles, and theories within each scientific discipline
- **Scientific thinking**: The ability to analyse, interpret, and evaluate scientific information and methodology
- **Practical skills**: Competency in laboratory techniques and experimental design
- **Mathematical proficiency**: Application of mathematical skills appropriate to each science (minimum 10% biology, 20% chemistry, 40% physics)
- **Scientific literacy**: The capacity to communicate scientific ideas effectively and understand their social and ethical implications

### 1.2 Ofqual Regulatory Requirements

Ofqual, as the qualifications regulator, establishes the conditions and requirements that awarding organisations must meet when developing and delivering A Level science qualifications. These include:

- **Content coverage**: Specifications must cover all content defined in the DfE subject content document
- **Assessment structure**: Three written papers at A Level (plus the practical endorsement)
- **Question types**: A range including multiple choice, short answer, long answer, calculations, and extended writing
- **Practical endorsement**: A separate Pass/Unclassified grade reported alongside the A*-E grade

The Ofqual subject-level conditions ensure consistency and comparability across different awarding bodies (AQA, Edexcel, OCR, WJEC/Eduqas) while allowing sufficient flexibility for distinctive pedagogical approaches.

## 2. A Level Biology

### 2.1 Content Overview

A Level Biology specifications divide content between AS (first year) and A Level (two-year) programmes. While AS is a standalone qualification, the full A Level provides the comprehensive preparation required for higher education in biological sciences, medicine, and related fields.

**Core Content Areas (AQA 7402 / Edexcel 9BI0):**

1. **Biological Molecules**: Monomers and polymers; carbohydrates; lipids; proteins; nucleic acids; ATP; water; inorganic ions
2. **Cells**: Cell structure; cell division; transport across membranes; cell recognition and the immune system
3. **Organisms Exchange Substances**: Gas exchange; digestion; mass transport in animals; mass transport in plants
4. **Genetic Information**: DNA, genes and chromosomes; DNA replication; protein synthesis; mutations; cell cycle and division; meiosis; genetic diversity; species and taxonomy; biodiversity
5. **Energy Transfers**: Photosynthesis; respiration; energy transfer in ecosystems; nutrient cycling
6. **Response to Stimuli**: Survival and response; receptors; control of heart rate; nerve impulses; synaptic transmission; skeletal muscles; homeostasis
7. **Genetics, Populations and Evolution**: Inheritance; populations and evolution; populations in ecosystems
8. **Control of Gene Expression**: Gene mutations; stem cells; gene expression; genome projects; gene technologies

**Edexcel B (Salters-Nuffield) Alternative Structure:**

- Paper 1: Advanced Biochemistry, Microbiology and Genetics (30%)
- Paper 2: Advanced Physiology, Evolution and Ecology (30%)
- Paper 3: General and Practical Applications in Biology (40%)

### 2.2 Required Practical Activities

The biology practical endorsement requires students to complete at least 12 practical activities that demonstrate competency in the Common Practical Assessment Criteria (CPAC). These typically include:

- Use of light microscopes and measurement of cells
- Investigating biological molecules through biochemical tests
- Investigating osmosis in plant tissue
- Investigating membrane permeability
- Dissection of animal or plant gas exchange system
- Investigating factors affecting enzyme activity
- Investigating the rate of photosynthesis
- Investigating the rate of respiration
- Investigating reflex actions
- Investigating population size and distribution
- Investigating genetic diversity using gel electrophoresis
- Investigating the effect of antibiotics on bacterial growth

### 2.3 Mathematical Skills in Biology

A minimum of 10% of assessment marks must target mathematical skills, including:

- Arithmetic and numerical computation
- Handling data (mean, standard deviation, standard error)
- Algebra (ratios, percentages, solving equations)
- Graphs (plotting, interpretation, gradients, intercepts)
- Geometry and trigonometry (surface area to volume ratios)
- Statistics (Spearman's rank, t-tests, chi-squared)

## 3. A Level Chemistry

### 3.1 Content Overview

A Level Chemistry is structured into three major strands: Physical Chemistry, Inorganic Chemistry, and Organic Chemistry. The DfE subject content prescribes specific topics within each strand that must be covered.

**Physical Chemistry (AQA 7405):**

- Atomic structure: protons, neutrons, electrons; mass spectrometry; electron configuration; ionisation energy
- Amount of substance: relative atomic mass; amount of substance; equations; Avogadro constant; molar calculations
- Bonding: ionic, covalent, and metallic bonding; bond polarity; forces between molecules; electronegativity; shapes of molecules and ions
- Energetics: enthalpy changes; calorimetry; Hess's Law; bond enthalpies
- Kinetics: collision theory; Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution; catalysts; rate equations; activation energy
- Chemical equilibria: dynamic equilibrium; equilibrium constant Kc; Le Chatelier's principle
- Redox: oxidation, reduction, and redox equations; electrode potentials
- Thermodynamics: Born-Haber cycles; entropy; free energy
- Rate equations: rate constant; order of reaction; half-life
- Equilibrium constant Kp for homogeneous systems
- Electrode potentials and electrochemical cells
- Acids and bases: Br√∏nsted-Lowry theory; pH; buffers; neutralisation

**Inorganic Chemistry:**

- Periodicity: periodic table structure; trends in properties across periods and down groups
- Group 2, the alkaline earth metals: trends in physical properties; reactions with water and oxygen; uses
- Group 7(17), the halogens: trends in physical properties; redox reactions; reactions of chlorine; uses
- Properties of Period 3 elements and their oxides: sodium to chlorine; acid-base behaviour of oxides
- Transition metals: general properties; complex ions; coloured ions; catalysts
- Reactions of ions in aqueous solution: reactions of metal-aqua ions; amphoteric hydroxides

**Organic Chemistry:**

- Introduction to organic chemistry: nomenclature; reaction mechanisms; isomerism
- Alkanes: fuels; free-radical substitution
- Halogenoalkanes: nucleophilic substitution; elimination
- Alkenes: electrophilic addition; polymerisation; industrial importance
- Alcohols: production; oxidation; elimination
- Organic analysis: tests for functional groups; chromatography; spectroscopy (mass spec, IR, NMR)
- Optical isomerism: chiral centres; racemic mixtures
- Aldehydes and ketones: nucleophilic addition; reduction; oxidation
- Carboxylic acids and derivatives: acidity; esters; acyl chlorides
- Aromatic chemistry: benzene structure; electrophilic substitution (nitration, halogenation, Friedel-Crafts)
- Amines: basicity; preparation; reactions
- Polymers: condensation polymers; biodegradable polymers
- Amino acids, proteins, and DNA: peptide bonds; protein structure; DNA structure and replication
- Organic synthesis: synthetic routes; retrosynthesis
- Nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy: proton NMR; carbon-13 NMR; interpretation

### 3.2 Required Practical Activities

The chemistry practical endorsement requires completion of at least 12 practical activities, including:

- Measuring the enthalpy change of a reaction
- Finding the composition of a mixture using gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS)
- Investigating how pH changes when a weak acid reacts with a strong base
- Preparing a standard solution and carrying out a titration
- Measuring the rate of reaction by an initial rate method
- Measuring the rate of reaction by a continuous monitoring method
- Finding the order of a reaction
- Identifying an unknown organic liquid or solid
- Identifying transition metal ions in solution
- Investigating iron tablets
- Investigating the chemistry of the transition metal copper
- Following the rate of the iodine-propanone reaction by a titrimetric method

### 3.3 Mathematical Skills in Chemistry

A minimum of 20% of assessment marks must target mathematical skills, including:

- Arithmetic and numerical computation
- Handling data (mean, standard deviation, uncertainty)
- Algebra (ratios, percentages, logarithms, solving equations)
- Graphs (plotting, interpretation, gradients, intercepts, areas under curves)
- Geometry and trigonometry (surface areas, volumes, angles in molecular structures)

## 4. A Level Physics

### 4.1 Content Overview

A Level Physics covers fundamental physical principles and their applications. The content is divided into core topics typically assessed across three papers, with a significant emphasis on mathematical treatment.

**Core Content Areas (AQA 7408 / Edexcel 9PH0):**

1. **Measurements and Their Errors**: SI units; limitations of physical measurements; uncertainty
2. **Particles and Radiation**: Constituents of the atom; stable and unstable nuclei; particles and antiparticles; particle interactions; photoelectric effect; collisions of electrons with atoms; energy levels and photon emission; wave-particle duality
3. **Waves**: Progressive and stationary waves; refraction, diffraction and interference
4. **Mechanics and Materials**: Vectors and scalars; motion along a straight line; projectile motion; Newton's laws of motion; momentum; work, energy and power; conservation of energy; bulk properties of solids; the Young modulus
5. **Electricity**: Basics of electricity; current-voltage characteristics; resistivity; circuits; potential dividers; electromotive force and internal resistance
6. **Further Mechanics and Thermal Physics**: Periodic motion; circular motion; simple harmonic motion; simple harmonic systems; forced vibrations and resonance; thermal energy transfer; molecular kinetic theory model; ideal gas behaviour
7. **Fields and Their Consequences**: Gravitational fields; gravitational potential; orbital motion; electric fields; electric potential; capacitors; magnetic fields; electromagnetic induction; alternating currents
8. **Nuclear Physics**: Rutherford scattering; nuclear radius and density; nuclear instability; nuclear radius; mass and energy; induced fission; safety aspects

**Options (typically one chosen):**

- Astrophysics
- Medical physics
- Engineering physics
- Turning points in physics
- Electronics

### 4.2 Required Practical Activities

The physics practical endorsement requires completion of at least 12 practical activities, including:

- Investigation of the variation of the frequency of stationary waves on a string with length, tension and mass per unit length
- Determination of the acceleration of free fall using a simple pendulum
- Determination of the resistivity of a wire
- Investigation of the emf and internal resistance of electric cells
- Investigation of the interference of sound waves using a two-source method
- Determination of the wavelength of light using a laser and a double slit
- Investigation of how the force on a wire varies with flux density, current and length of wire
- Investigation of Boyle's Law for a gas
- Investigation of the charge and discharge of capacitors
- Investigation of the effect of temperature on resistance
- Determination of specific heat capacity
- Investigation of the inverse-square law for gamma radiation

### 4.3 Mathematical Skills in Physics

A minimum of 40% of assessment marks must target mathematical skills, including:

- Arithmetic and numerical computation
- Handling data (mean, standard deviation, uncertainty, significant figures)
- Algebra (symbols, indices, logarithms, solving equations, exponentials)
- Geometry and trigonometry (Pythagoras, sine/cosine rules, angles in polygons)
- Graphs (plotting, interpretation, gradients, intercepts, areas, log-linear graphs)
- Vectors (resolution, addition, resultant vectors)

## 5. The Practical Endorsement

### 5.1 Overview

The A Level Science Practical Endorsement is a formal requirement for all A Level Biology, Chemistry, and Physics qualifications in England. Introduced with the 2015 specifications, it represents a significant reform in how practical skills are assessed and reported.

**Key characteristics:**

- Awarded as a separate Pass or Unclassified grade
- Reported alongside the A*-E grade on certificates
- Requires demonstration of competency across five Common Practical Assessment Criteria (CPAC)
- Based on teacher observation and assessment of students completing at least 12 practical activities
- Not assessed through written examination (though written papers assess practical knowledge)

### 5.2 Common Practical Assessment Criteria (CPAC)

Ofqual has established five CPAC that students must meet consistently to achieve a Pass in the practical endorsement:

**CPAC 1: Follows Written Procedures**
- Correctly follows instructions to carry out experimental techniques or procedures
- Works safely and methodically
- Identifies and uses appropriate apparatus

**CPAC 2: Applies Investigative Approaches and Methods**
- Applies investigative approaches and methods when using instruments and equipment
- Identifies variables and controls where appropriate
- Makes decisions about measurements and observations

**CPAC 3: Safely Uses a Range of Practical Equipment and Materials**
- Uses equipment safely and methodically
- Applies appropriate techniques for handling apparatus and materials
- Minimises risks to themselves and others

**CPAC 4: Makes and Records Observations**
- Makes accurate observations relevant to the experimental procedure
- Records observations appropriately using tables or other suitable formats
- Records measurements with appropriate units and precision

**CPAC 5: Researches, References and Reports**
- Researches and identifies relevant sources of information
- Reports findings using appropriate scientific vocabulary and conventions
- References sources appropriately
- Identifies sources of error and uncertainty
- Evaluates methods and suggests improvements

### 5.3 Monitoring and Quality Assurance

The practical endorsement is subject to external monitoring by awarding organisations. This typically involves:

- Monitoring visits to centres (usually once every two years)
- Review of records and evidence of student competency
- Observation of practical activities
- Verification of teacher assessment decisions

Centres must retain records including:

- Tracking documents showing which CPAC each student has demonstrated and when
- Evidence of student work (written records, data, observations)
- Centre-designed or adapted practical activities
- Head teacher declaration form confirming completion of requirements

The deadline for submission of the Head teacher declaration form is typically 15 May of the examination year.

## 6. Assessment Structure and Progression

### 6.1 AS Level Assessment

AS Level is a standalone qualification representing the first year of A Level study. It consists of:

- **Two written papers** covering the AS content (typically 50% each)
- **No practical endorsement** required (though practical work is expected)
- **Grades A-E** awarded

Note: AS results do not contribute to the A Level grade; the A Level is a linear qualification examined at the end of the two-year course.

### 6.2 A Level Assessment

The full A Level qualification comprises:

- **Three written papers** covering all content (AS and A Level)
- **Practical endorsement** (Pass/Unclassified)
- **Grades A*-E** for the written component

**Typical paper structure (AQA):**

- Paper 1: Short and long answer questions, including multiple choice, covering first year content plus relevant practical skills
- Paper 2: Short and long answer questions, including multiple choice, covering second year content plus relevant practical skills
- Paper 3: Structured questions including practical techniques, critical analysis of experimental data, and extended response synoptic questions

### 6.3 Progression and Higher Education

A Level sciences are gateway qualifications for numerous higher education pathways:

- **Biology**: Medicine, dentistry, veterinary science, biochemistry, biomedical sciences, pharmacy, nursing, environmental science
- **Chemistry**: Chemistry, chemical engineering, biochemistry, medicine, pharmacy, materials science, forensic science
- **Physics**: Physics, engineering (all branches), astrophysics, computer science, mathematics, medicine

The Russell Group of universities identifies A Level sciences as "facilitating subjects" that provide the widest range of options for university study.

## 7. Pedagogical Approaches in KS5 Science

### 7.1 Teaching the Practical Endorsement

Effective delivery of the practical endorsement requires:

- **Integration with theory**: Practical work should reinforce conceptual understanding, not be an add-on
- **Progressive skill development**: Students need multiple opportunities to develop competency before assessment
- **Authentic investigations**: Include open-ended enquiries alongside recipe-following activities
- **CPAC mapping**: Clear documentation of when and how each student meets each criterion
- **Digital portfolios**: Electronic systems for tracking and evidencing competency

### 7.2 Addressing Mathematical Requirements

Given the significant mathematical demands (especially in physics at 40%), effective teaching includes:

- **Pre-teaching mathematical skills** before application in science contexts
- **Explicit modelling** of calculations, including showing full working
- **Regular practice** with past paper mathematical questions
- **Graph-drawing skills** taught explicitly
