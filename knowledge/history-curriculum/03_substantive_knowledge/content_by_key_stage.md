# Content by Key Stage: Mapping the History National Curriculum

## Introduction

This document provides a comprehensive mapping of the prescribed history content across all key stages of the English National Curriculum, from the Early Years Foundation Stage through to A Level. It identifies what pupils are expected to learn at each stage, the progression between key stages, and the conceptual threads that connect different periods and topics.

## Early Years Foundation Stage: Foundations

The EYFS establishes foundations for historical learning through the **Understanding the World** area:

### ELG: Past and Present
- Talk about lives of people around them and their roles in society
- Know similarities and differences between past and present
- Understand the past through stories and settings

### ELG: People, Culture and Communities
- Describe their immediate environment using observation, stories, and maps
- Know similarities and differences between communities
- Know similarities and differences between life in this country and other countries

### ELG: The Natural World
- Explore the natural world, observing animals and plants
- Know similarities and differences between local and contrasting environments
- Understand seasons and natural changes

## Key Stage 1: Developing Historical Awareness

### Changes Within Living Memory
Topics revealing aspects of change in national life:
- Toys and games through time
- Transport changes
- Communication evolution
- School life changes
- Homes and household objects
- The local high street

### Events Beyond Living Memory
- **The Great Fire of London (1666)**
- **First aeroplane flight (1903)**
- Remembrance and World Wars
- The Gunpowder Plot (1605)

### Lives of Significant Individuals
Comparing aspects of life in different periods:
- Elizabeth I and Queen Victoria
- Neil Armstrong and Christopher Columbus
- Florence Nightingale and Mary Seacole
- Mary Anning
- Rosa Parks

### Local History
Significant historical events, people and places in pupils' own locality.

## Key Stage 2: Building the National Narrative

### British History: Stone Age to 1066

**Stone Age (c. 10,000 BCE – 2,500 BCE)**
- Hunter-gatherer to farming transition
- Monumental structures: Stonehenge, Avebury
- Burial practices and beliefs

**Bronze Age (c. 2,500 BCE – 800 BCE)**
- Introduction of metalworking
- Trade networks
- Beaker people and cultural change
- Round barrows and burial customs

**Iron Age (c. 800 BCE – 43 CE)**
- Iron technology
- Hill forts and social organisation
- Tribal kingdoms
- Contact with Rome

### Roman Britain (43 CE – 410 CE)

**Conquest and Consolidation**
- Claudian invasion of 43 CE
- Boudica's revolt (60/61 CE)
- Hadrian's Wall
- Roman military organisation

**Society and Culture**
- Towns and urban life
- Road networks
- Villas and rural life
- Religion and Christianity
- Trade and economy

### Anglo-Saxon and Viking Britain (410-1066)

**Anglo-Saxon Settlement (c. 410-600)**
- Germanic settlement patterns
- Kingdoms: Kent, Northumbria, Mercia, Wessex
- Sutton Hoo
- Conversion to Christianity

**Viking Age (793-1066)**
- Raids and Lindisfarne
- The Great Heathen Army
- Alfred the Great
- The Danelaw
- Danish conquest and reconquest
- Kingdom of England emergence

### Post-1066 British Study Options
Schools choose one:
- Changing power of monarchs
- Lives of significant individuals
- Significant turning points
- Legacy of Greek/Roman culture
- Significant world society or issue

### Ancient Civilisations (Depth Study)
One of: Ancient Sumer, Indus Valley, Shang Dynasty, **Ancient Egypt**

### Ancient Greece
- Greek life and achievements
- Influence on the Western world
- Legacy through time

### Non-European Society
One of:
- **Early Islamic Civilisation** (c. AD 900), including Baghdad
- **Mayan Civilisation** (c. AD 900)
- **Benin (West Africa)** (c. AD 900-1300)

## Key Stage 3: Sophisticated Understanding

### Medieval Britain (1066-1509)
- Norman Conquest and impact
- Medieval Church and society
- Black Death and social consequences
- Magna Carta
- Hundred Years' War
- Wars of the Roses

### Early Modern Britain (1509-1745)
- Reformation: Henry VIII, Edward VI, Mary I, Elizabeth I
- Religious settlement and pluralism
- Growth of towns and trade
- Exploration and empire beginnings
- Civil Wars and Interregnum
- Glorious Revolution and parliamentary monarchy

### Britain 1745-1901
- Enlightenment ideas
- American and French Revolutions
- Industrial Revolution
- Social and environmental change
- Empire expansion
- Slave trade and abolition
- Indian Empire

### Britain 1901-Present
- **First World War**: causes, events, consequences
- Interwar period and rise of dictatorships
- **Second World War**: causes, events, Holocaust
- Post-war Britain: welfare state, NHS, decolonisation
- Social change: migration, equality movements
- Cold War context
- Contemporary challenges

### The Holocaust (Mandatory)
A unique aspect of the KS3 curriculum:
- Systematic study required
- Teaching guidance from the Holocaust Educational Trust
- Sensitive and age-appropriate approaches
- Historical contextualisation essential

## Key Stage 4: GCSE History

### Ofqual Requirements
- History of Britain (minimum 25%)
- History of the wider world (minimum 25%)
- Thematic study across time (minimum 5%)
- Study of a significant historic environment (linked to another topic)
- Period study (minimum 5%)
- British depth study (minimum 5%)

### Common Topics Across Specifications

**British Depth Studies**
- Anglo-Saxon and Norman England
- Henry VIII and his ministers
- Elizabethan England
- Restoration England
- Germany 1890-1945 (often the most popular)
- Russia 1894-1945
- America 1920-1973

**Thematic Studies**
- Crime and punishment through time
- Medicine through time
- Migration and empires
- Warfare through time

**Period Studies**
- Russia 1894-1945
- Weimar and Nazi Germany
- The USA 1954-75
- The making of America

**British/World Studies**
- Britain: Power and the people
- Britain: Health and the people
- Conflict in the Middle East

## Key Stage 5: A Level History

### Core Components
- **Breadth study**: Significant periods of historical change (minimum 50 years)
- **Depth study**: Focus on a shorter period in greater detail
- **Historical investigation (NEA)**: Independent research essay (3,000-4,000 words)

### Common Breadth Study Topics
- The Crusades
- England 1199-1272
- The Wars of the Roses
- The Tudors
- The Stuarts
- Industrialisation and the people
- Democracy and Nazism: Germany
- The making of modern Britain

### Common Depth Study Topics
- France in Revolution
- Russia in Revolution
- Civil Rights in the USA
- The Cold War
- The English Revolution
- The Unification of Italy or Germany

### Historical Investigation (NEA)
- Student-selected topic
- 3,000-4,000 word essay
- Focus on different historical interpretations
- Primary and secondary source analysis
- Independent research skills

## Progression Across Key Stages

### Chronological Development
| Key Stage | Chronological Focus | Key Periods |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| EYFS | Personal, immediate | Living memory |
| KS1 | Living memory + selected events | 1605-1903 + local |
| KS2 | Prehistory to present | c.10,000 BCE-present |
| KS3 | 1066-present with depth | 1066-present |
| KS4 | Depth studies across time | Varies by specification |
| KS5 | Extended depth and breadth | 50+ year breadth studies |

### Conceptual Progression
- **KS1**: Change, significance, similarity/difference
- **KS2**: Continuity, causation, evidence, interpretation
- **KS3**: Historical explanation, diversity of experience, source evaluation
- **KS4/5**: Independent analysis, historiography, multiple interpretations
